'''Kathleen Doyle''' "'''Kathy'''" '''Bates''' (born June 28, 1948)<ref name="FilmReference"/> is an American actress and film director. After appearing in several minor roles in film and television during the 1970s and the 1980s, Bates rose to prominence with her performance in ''[[Misery (film)|Misery]]'' (1990), for which she won the [[Academy Award for Best Actress]]; she also received a [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actress - Motion Picture Drama|Golden Globe]].<ref name="nyt">{{cite news|last=Bagger|first=The|title=Kathy Bates: An Oscar and a Lovely Career to Go With It|url=http://carpetbagger.blogs.nytimes.com/2009/01/09/kathy-bates-an-oscar-and-a-lovely-career-to-go-with-it/|accessdate=January 17, 2011|newspaper=The New York Times|date=January 9, 2009}}</ref> She followed this with major roles in ''[[Fried Green Tomatoes (film)|Fried Green Tomatoes]]'' (1991) and ''[[Dolores Claiborne (film)|Dolores Claiborne]]'' (1995), before playing a featured role as [[Margaret Brown|Molly Brown]] in ''[[Titanic (1997 film)|Titanic]]'' (1997).

She received a [[Tony Award]] nomination for her 1983 performance in the Broadway play ''[['night, Mother]]''. She won a [[Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Female Actor in a Supporting Role - Motion Picture|Screen Actors Guild Award]] for her performance in ''[[Primary Colors (film)|Primary Colors]]'' (1998), for which she also received an Academy Award nomination for [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress|Best Supporting Actress]]. She was also nominated for an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress for ''[[About Schmidt]]'' (2002).

Her television work has resulted in twelve [[Emmy Award]] nominations, two of which were for her starring role on the television series ''[[Harry's Law]]''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.emmys.com/celebrities/kathy-bates|title=Kathy Bates|publisher=emmys.com|accessdate=September 17, 2012}}</ref> and most recently, a win for her highly praised appearance on the [[American Horror Story: Coven|third series]] of [[FX (TV channel)|FX]] horror-thriller series ''[[American Horror Story]]'', where she portrayed [[Delphine LaLaurie (American Horror Story)|Delphine LaLaurie]]. She won another Emmy Award in 2012 for her portrayal as the ghost of [[Charlie Harper (Two and a Half Men)|Charlie Harper]] on [[CBS]] comedy series ''[[Two and a Half Men]]'', a role formerly portrayed by [[Charlie Sheen]].<ref>

==Early life==
Bates was born in [[Memphis, Tennessee]], the youngest of three daughters of Bertye Kathleen (née Talbert; 1907–97), a homemaker, and Langdon Doyle Bates (1900–89), a mechanical engineer.<ref name="FilmReference"/> Her paternal grandfather was lawyer and author [[Finis L. Bates]]. One of her great-great-grandfathers, who emigrated from Ireland to [[New Orleans]], [[Louisiana]], served as President [[Andrew Jackson]]'s doctor.<ref name="ScottsMovies"/> She graduated from [[White Station High School]] and from [[Southern Methodist University]] (1969), where she majored in Theatre and became a member of [[Alpha Delta Pi]] sorority. She moved to [[New York City]] in 1970 to pursue an acting career.<ref name="yahoo"/> She was raised [[Methodist]].{{citation needed|date=September 2012}}

==Career==
Bates' history of Broadway appearances includes [[Lanford Wilson]]'s ''[[Fifth of July]]'' and the [[Robert Altman]]-directed ''[[Come Back to the Five and Dime, Jimmy Dean, Jimmy Dean (film)|Come Back to the Five and Dime, Jimmy Dean, Jimmy Dean]]'' opposite [[Karen Black]] and [[Cher]]. She received a [[Tony Award]] nomination in 1983 for her stage role in the [[Pulitzer Prize]]-winning play ''[['night, Mother]]'' opposite [[Anne Pitoniak]]. The production of ''{{'}}night, Mother'' ran for more than a year. One of her other successful New York stage productions was, [[Off Broadway]], in [[Terrence McNally]]'s ''[[Frankie and Johnny in the Clair de Lune]]'' which ran 533 performances. McNally specifically wrote the play for Bates and [[F. Murray Abraham]], who had to drop out and was replaced by [[Kenneth Welsh]]. The play was later filmed as ''[[Frankie and Johnny (1991 film)|Frankie and Johnny]]'', starring [[Al Pacino]] and [[Michelle Pfeiffer]]. She succeeded [[Amy Irving]] in the off-Broadway production of ''[[The Road to Mecca]]'' in 1988.

Bates' first feature film role was the 1971 [[Miloš Forman]] comedy ''[[Taking Off (film)|Taking Off]]'' (credited as "Bobo Bates"), wherein she sings an original song "Even Horses Had Wings". Bates' next feature was the [[Dustin Hoffman]] film ''[[Straight Time]]'' (1978). In 1977, Bates made her soap opera debut as Phyllis on NBC's soap opera ''[[The Doctors (1963 TV series)|The Doctors]]''. From 1983 to 1984, she played prison inmate Belle Bodelle on ''[[All My Children]]'' and from 1984 to 1985, she played Evelyn Maddox on ''[[One Life to Live]]''.

In 1990, she would appear again with Hoffman in [[Warren Beatty]]'s ''[[Dick Tracy (1990 film)|Dick Tracy]]'' as a stenographer. She appeared in films like ''[[The Morning After]]'' and ''[[Summer Heat (1987 film)|Summer Heat]]'', while guest-starring on television's ''[[L.A. Law]]'', before landing the role of obsessed fan [[Annie Wilkes]], who holds her favorite author (played by [[James Caan (actor)|James Caan]]) captive, in the 1990 thriller film ''[[Misery (film)|Misery]]'', based on the [[Stephen King]] novel. Bates received her first [[Academy Award]] nomination for that role, winning Best Actress. Soon after, she starred with [[Jessica Tandy]] in the acclaimed 1991 movie ''[[Fried Green Tomatoes (film)|Fried Green Tomatoes]]'', based on the novel by comedic actress [[Fannie Flagg]].

In 1995, Bates played the title character in ''[[Dolores Claiborne (film)|Dolores Claiborne]]'', a film adaption of another Stephen King novel, although she was not nominated for an Oscar. In 1997, Bates played [[Margaret Brown|Molly Brown]] in [[James Cameron]]'s ''[[Titanic (1997 film)|Titanic]]''. Based on the 1912 sinking of the [[RMS Titanic]], the film went on to earn more than US$1.8&nbsp;billion in box-office receipts worldwide.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.boxofficemojo.com/alltime/world|title=Worldwide Grosses|publisher=[[Box Office Mojo]]|accessdate=January 20, 2009}}</ref>

Bates also excelled in her role as the acid-tongued "dustbuster" political advisor Libby Holden in the 1998 drama film ''[[Primary Colors (film)|Primary Colors]]'', which was adapted from the book in which political journalist [[Joe Klein]] novelized his experiences on the Presidential campaign trail in 1991–1992. For this performance, she received her second Academy Award nomination, for [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress|Best Supporting Actress]]. In 2002 she received her third nomination, for ''[[About Schmidt]]''. More recently, she and [[Terry Bradshaw]] played the parents of [[Matthew McConaughey]]'s character in the 2006 film ''[[Failure to Launch]]''. Bates was featured in an uncredited cameo in the miniseries of [[Stephen King]]'s ''[[The Stand (TV miniseries)|The Stand]]''.

Bates has been nominated for an ''[[Emmy Award]]''<ref>[http://www.emmys.com/celebrities/kathy-bates Kathy Bates Emmy Nominated]</ref> twelve times, winning two: [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Miniseries or a Movie|Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Miniseries or a Movie]], for her performance as [[Jay Leno]]'s manager [[Helen Kushnick]] in [[HBO]]'s ''[[The Late Shift (film)|The Late Shift]]'' (1996), and, twice again in the same category; as Miss Hannigan in Disney's remake of ''[[Annie (1999 film)|Annie]]'' (1999), and for the HBO [[Franklin Roosevelt]] biopic ''[[Warm Springs (film)|Warm Springs]]'' (2005). She was nominated for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Miniseries or Movie for [[Lifetime Television]]'s ''[[Ambulance Girl]]'' (2006), which she also directed and received a Supporting Actress nomination for ''Alice''. 
[[File:Kathy Bates 2006.jpg|thumb|upright|left|Bates in July 2006]]
She appeared in 10 episodes of the [[HBO]] cable television series ''[[Six Feet Under (TV series)|Six Feet Under]]'' for which she received an [[Emmy Award]] nomination for Outstanding Guest Actress in a Drama Series, as Bettina, in 2003. She also was nominated for Outstanding Guest Actress in a Comedy Series for ''[[3rd Rock from the Sun]]'' in 1999, the same year that she was nominated for Outstanding Directing in a Miniseries or Movie for the [[Dashiell Hammett]]-[[Lillian Hellman]] biopic ''Dash & Lilly''. She also had a recurring guest role on the American version of ''[[The Office (U.S. TV series)|The Office]]'' as [[Jo Bennett]].

Starting in the 1990s, Bates forged a formidable career as a director. She has directed episodes of ''[[Homicide: Life on the Street]]'', ''[[NYPD Blue]]'', ''[[Oz (TV series)|Oz]]'', ''[[Six Feet Under (TV series)|Six Feet Under]]'', and ''[[Everwood]]''. Bates directed the television movies ''Dash and Lilly'' and the self-starring ''Ambulance Girl''. She directed and co-starred in ''Have Mercy'' (2006) with [[Melanie Griffith]]. In 2008, she re-teamed with [[Leonardo DiCaprio]] and [[Kate Winslet]] in ''[[Revolutionary Road (film)|Revolutionary Road]]''. She starred in [[David E. Kelley]]'s legal drama ''[[Harry's Law]]'', which began airing on [[NBC]] on January 17, 2011, but was cancelled on May 14, 2012.<ref>{{cite press release|url=http://www.nbc.com/news/2010/05/14/nbc-adds-to-new-scripted-lineup-with-four-new-series----the-cape-outlaw-harrys-law-and-friends-with/|title=NBC Adds To New Scripted Lineup With Four New Series&nbsp;– 'The Cape', 'Outlaw', 'Harry's Law', And 'Friends With Benefits'&nbsp;– And Renews 'Chuck' For Fourth Season In 2010–11|date=May 14, 2010|publisher=NBC Universal|accessdate=May 16, 2010}}</ref>

In 2012, Bates made a guest appearance on ''[[Two and a Half Men]]'' as the ghost of Charlie Harper on the episode, "Why We Gave Up Women", which aired on April 30, 2012. In the episode [[Charlie Harper (Two and a Half Men)|Charlie]] has returned as a ghost to haunt his brother, [[Alan Harper (Two and a Half Men)|Alan]] ([[Jon Cryer]]). He tells Alan that after a life of womanizing and debauchery, he was sent to hell and condemned to spend eternity in a woman's body. This guest appearance resulted in Bates' winning the [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Guest Actress in a Comedy Series]]. It was Bates' first Emmy win after nine nominations.

In 2013, she began starring in the ''[[American Horror Story]]'' series' third season, ''[[American Horror Story: Coven|Coven]]'' as [[Delphine LaLaurie (American Horror Story)|Delphine LaLaurie]], an immortal racist who is brought back into the modern world after spending years buried alive. For that role, she won an Emmy Award for [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Miniseries or a Movie|Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Miniseries or a Movie]]. Bates returned in its fourth season ''[[American Horror Story: Freak Show|Freak Show]]'' as Ethel Darling, a [[Bearded lady|bearded Lady]] who performs in the freak show the season is centred on.[http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/american-horror-story-first-look-728545]

;Affiliations
Bates is the Executive Committee Chair of the Actors Branch of the [[Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences]] Board of Governors.<ref>{{cite web|author=Published June 27, 2007 by|url=http://www.theinsider.com/news/250917_June_28th_2007_Happy_Birthday_Kathy_Bates|title=June 28, 2007&nbsp;– Happy Birthday Kathy Bates!|publisher=Theinsider.com|accessdate=August 4, 2010}}</ref> In 2014, Bates became a national spokesperson for lymphedema and Chairperson for the [http://lymphaticnetwork.org/ Lymphatic Education & Research Network's] [http://lymphaticnetwork.org/about/honorary-board/ Honorary Board].

==Personal life==
Bates has successfully battled [[ovarian cancer]] since her diagnosis in 2003. In September 2012, she revealed via [[Twitter]] that she had been diagnosed with [[breast cancer]] two months earlier and had undergone a double [[mastectomy]].<ref name="cancer">{{cite news|title=Kathy Bates reveals she is battling breast cancer|url=http://news.health.com/2012/09/13/kathy-bates-mastectomy-breast-cancer|accessdate=September 13, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Celizic|first=Mike|title=Kathy Bates reveals her triumph over ovarian cancer|url=http://today.msnbc.msn.com/id/28580263/ns/today-today_people|accessdate=January 17, 2011|newspaper=MSN|date=January 9, 2009}}</ref> In 2014, at the Walk for Lymphedema & Lymphatic Diseases, Bates announced via [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xEY5r5RbSJA pre-recorded audio] that, due to the double mastectomy, she has [[lymphedema]] in both arms. She was married to Tony Campisi for six years, from 1991-1997.
