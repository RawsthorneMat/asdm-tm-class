'''Akshay Kumar''' (also known by his birth name '''Rajiv Hari Om Bhatia''') (born on 9th September 1967) is an Indian film actor, producer and [[Martial arts|martial artist]] who has appeared in [[Akshay Kumar filmography|over a hundred]] [[Bollywood|Hindi films]].<ref>{{cite news|author=Mehul S Thakkar|url=http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2013-02-12/news-interviews/37058715_1_akshay-kumar-film-mujhme |title=I am used to rewards and not awards: Akshay Kumar |work=[[The Times of India]]|publisher=[[The Times Group]]|date=12 February 2013 |accessdate=9 March 2013|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130708203353/http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2013-02-12/news-interviews/37058715_1_akshay-kumar-film-mujhme|archivedate=8 July 2013}}</ref> He has been nominated for [[Filmfare Awards]] several times, [[List of awards and nominations received by Akshay Kumar|winning it two times]]. When he began his acting career in the 1990s, he primarily starred in [[action film]]s and was known for his appearances in films of the "[[Khiladi (film series)|''Khiladi'' series]]" as well as other action films such as ''[[Waqt Hamara Hai]]'' (1993), ''[[Mohra]]'' (1994), ''[[Elaan (1994 film)|Elaan]]'' (1994), ''[[Suhaag (1994 film)|Suhaag]]'' (1994), ''[[Sapoot]]'' (1996). 

Later, Kumar also gained fame for his drama, romance and comic roles. His performances in [[romance film]]s like ''[[Yeh Dillagi]]'' (1994), ''[[Dhadkan]]'' (2000), ''[[Andaaz]]'' (2003) and ''[[Namastey London]]'' (2007), as well as drama films such as ''[[Waqt: The Race Against Time]]'' (2005). His comic performances in [[comedy film]]s such as ''[[Hera Pheri (2000 film)|Hera Pheri]]'' (2000), ''[[Mujhse Shaadi Karogi]]'' (2004), ''[[Garam Masala (2005 film)|Garam Masala]]'' (2005), ''[[Bhagam Bhag]]'' (2006), ''[[Bhool Bhulaiyaa]]'' (2007), and ''[[Singh Is Kinng]]'' (2008) met with acclaim. In 2007, he starred in four consecutive commercially successful films. Kumar hit a rough patch since 2009 to 2011 but came back with two successes, ''[[Housefull 2]]'' (2012) and ''[[Rowdy Rathore]]'' (2012) with both of them grossing over {{INRConvert|1|b}}. His other films like ''[[OMG – Oh My God!|OMG]]'' (2012), ''[[Special 26]]'' (2013) and ''[[Holiday (2014 film)|Holiday]]'' (2014) were highly successful critically and commercially. Apart from acting, Kumar has also worked as a [[stunt actor]]; he has often performed many dangerous stunts in his films, which has earned him the sobriquet "Indian [[Jackie Chan]]".<ref name="Chan">{{cite news|title=Akshay Kumar meets Jackie Chan in Hong Kong|date=20 May 2004 |publisher=[[Bollywood Hungama]]|url=http://www.bollywoodhungama.com/news/2004/05/20/1112/index.html|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081210042549/http://www.bollywoodhungama.com/news/2004/05/20/1112/index.html |archivedate=10 December 2008|accessdate=11 April 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Mahipal|first=Nikita|url=http://www.koimoi.com/box-office/will-akshay-kumar-s-100th-film-make-100-crores/|title=Will Akshay Kumar's 100th Film Make 100 Crores?|publisher=[[Koimoi]]|date=19 July 2012|accessdate=31 July 2014}}</ref> In 2008, he hosted the show ''[[Fear Factor&nbsp;– Khatron Ke Khiladi]]''. The next year, he founded the [[Hari Om Entertainment]] production company.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2009-10-09/news-interviews/28090093_1_akshay-kumar-producer-taare-zameen-par |title=Main bhi producer!|date=9 October 2009|work=[[The Times of India]]|publisher=[[The Times Group]]|accessdate=18 November 2010|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121106051324/http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2009-10-09/news-interviews/28090093_1_akshay-kumar-producer-taare-zameen-par|archivedate=6 November 2012}}</ref>

In 2008, the [[University of Windsor]] conferred an [[Honorary Doctorate]] of Law on Kumar in recognition of his contribution to Indian cinema. The following year he was awarded the [[Padma Shri]] by the [[Government of India]]. In 2011 [[The Asian Awards]] honoured Kumar for his outstanding achievement in Cinema. In 2012 he founded another production company called [[Grazing Goat Pictures]].

In 2014, Kumar launched TV realty show [[Dare 2 Dance]]. He also owns the team Khalsa Warriors in the [[World Kabaddi League]].

=== 2007–2011 ===

[[File:Akshay & Katrina on the sets of Welcome.jpg|thumb|upright=1.1|Kumar with [[Katrina Kaif]] on the sets of ''[[Welcome (2007 film)|Welcome]]'' (2007); the duo received appreciation for their pairing and appeared in several successful films together]]

2007 proved to be Kumar's most successful year during his career in the industry, and as described by box office analysts, "probably the best ever recorded by an actor, with four outright hits and no flops."<ref name="BO 2007">{{cite news|url=http://www.boxofficeindia.com/npages.php?page=shownews&articleid=42&nCat=news|title=The Toppers Of 2007|publisher=Box Office India|accessdate=14 March 2007|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080115082127/http://www.boxofficeindia.com/npages.php?page=shownews&articleid=42&nCat=news|archivedate=15 January 2008}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=I am kind to everyone, but trust no one: Akshay Kumar|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/entertainment/hindi/bollywood/news/I-am-kind-to-everyone-but-trust-no-one-Akshay-Kumar/articleshow/9779766.cms|work=[[The Times of India]]|publisher=[[The Times Group]]|accessdate=21 August 2014|date=29 August 2011}}</ref> His first release, [[Vipul Amrutlal Shah]]-directed ''[[Namastey London]]'', was critically and commercially successful, and his performance earned him a Best Actor nomination at the Filmfare. Critic [[Taran Adarsh]] wrote of his performance in the film, "he's sure to win the hearts of millions of moviegoers with a terrific portrayal in this film."<ref>{{cite news|author=Adarsh, Taran|url=http://indiafm.com/movies/review/12835/index.html|title=Review of Namastey London|date=23 March 2007|accessdate=5 April 2007|publisher=IndiaFM| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20070405202710/http://www.indiafm.com/movies/review/12835/index.html| archivedate= 5 April 2007}}</ref> Kumar's chemistry with lead actress Katrina Kaif also generated immense appreciation, with [[Nikhat Kazmi]] of ''[[The Times of India]]'' describing their pairing as "refreshing."<ref>{{cite news|last=Kazmi|first=Nikhat|title=Namastey London|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/entertainment/movie-reviews/hindi/Namastey-London/movie-review/1802925.cms|accessdate=21 August 2014|work=[[The Times of India]]|publisher=[[The Times Group]]|date=24 March 2007}}</ref> His next two releases, [[Sajid Khan]]-directed ''[[Heyy Babyy]]'' and Priyadarshan's ''[[Bhool Bhulaiyaa]]'', were box office successes as well.<ref>{{cite web|author=Adarsh, Taran|title=Top 5: 'Dhamaal' average, 'Darling' slumps!|url=http://www.indiafm.com/trade/top5/380.html|date=15 September 2007|accessdate=14 March 2008|publisher=IndiaFM|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20071015033244/http://indiafm.com/trade/top5/380.html|archivedate=15 October 2008}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Adarsh, Taran|title=Top 5: 'J.W.M.' steady, despite pre-Diwali dull phase|url=http://www.indiafm.com/trade/top5/396.html|date=10 November 2007|publisher=IndiaFM|accessdate=10 November 2007| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20071115221205/http://www.indiafm.com/trade/top5/396.html| archivedate= 15 November 2007}}</ref> In both of these films he starred opposite [[Vidya Balan]]. Kumar's last release of the year, the [[Anees Bazmee]]-directed ''[[Welcome (2007 film)|Welcome]]'', did extremely well at the box office, receiving a blockbuster status and simultaneously becoming his fifth successive hit.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=IndiaFM|author=Adarsh, Taran|title=Midweek: 'Welcome', 'TZP' continue to rock!|url=http://www.indiafm.com/trade/top5/411.html|date=1 January 2008|accessdate=1 January 2008| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20080108131929/http://www.indiafm.com/trade/top5/411.html| archivedate= 8 January 2008}}</ref> All of Kumar's films which released that year did well in the overseas market as well.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.boxofficeindia.com/cpages.php?pageName=overseas_earners|title=Overseas Earnings (Figures in Ind Rs)|publisher=Box Office India|accessdate=14 March 2008| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20080317191849/http://www.boxofficeindia.com/cpages.php?pageName=overseas_earners&PHPSESSID=b754628e151e869b2426dfd035eb7581| archivedate= 17 March 2008}}</ref> Kumar appeared in a cameo role in [[Farah Khan]] directed ''[[Om Shanti Om]]''. His role was listed as no. 3 on the Top 10 Cameos In Bollywood list of [[MensXP.com]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Saikia|first1=Priyankee|title=Top 10 Cameos In Bollywood|url=http://www.mensxp.com/entertainment/bollywood/9378-top-10-cameos-in-bollywood.html|publisher=[[MensXP.com]]|accessdate=28 August 2014}}</ref>

Kumar's first film of 2008, [[Vijay Krishna Acharya]]-directed action thriller ''[[Tashan (film)|Tashan]]'', marked his comeback to the [[Yash Raj Films]] banner after 11 years. Although a poll (conducted by Bollywood Hungama) named it the most anticipated release of the year,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://oldbh.bollywoodhungama.com/features/2008/02/08/3550/index.html|title=The Most Awaited movies of 2008|accessdate=19 May 2009|author=Bollywood Hungama News Network|date=8 February 2008|publisher=[[Bollywood Hungama]]|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20090126145213/http://www.bollywoodhungama.com/features/2008/02/08/3550/index.html|archivedate=26 January 2009}}</ref> the film under-performed at the box office grossing {{INRConvert|279|m}} in India.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://boxofficeindia.com/showProd.php?itemCat=215&catName=MjAwOA==|title=Box Office 2008|accessdate=20 January 2009|publisher=Box Office India|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20130115223107/http://boxofficeindia.com/showProd.php?itemCat=215&catName=MjAwOA==|archivedate=15 January 2013}}</ref> His second film, Bazmee-directed ''[[Singh Is Kinng]]'' in which he starred opposite Kaif was a huge success at the box office and broke the first-week worldwide record of ''[[Om Shanti Om (film)|Om Shanti Om]]'', the previous highest figure.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.boxofficeindia.com/showProd.php?itemCat=215&catName=MjAwOA==|title=Box Office 2008|publisher=Box Office India|accessdate=28 January 2009|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080115083431/http://www.boxofficeindia.com/showProd.php?itemCat=215&catName=MjAwOA==|archivedate=15 January 2008}}</ref> His next film was the animated film ''[[Jumbo (film)|Jumbo]]'', directed by Kompin Kemgumnird. He later returned to host the show's [[Fear Factor&nbsp;– Khatron Ke Khiladi Level 2|second season]] in 2009.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Khanna|first1=Niyati|title=Tracing The Journey of Fear Factor – Khatron Ke Khiladi; Season 5 Back with a Bang|url=http://businessofcinema.com/tele/tracing-journey-fear-factor-khatron-ke-khiladi-season-5-back-bang/131637|work=Business of Cinema|accessdate=28 August 2014|date=31 January 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Priyanka Chopra to host 'Fear Factor, Season 3'|url=http://www.newindianexpress.com/entertainment/television/article202006.ece|work=[[The New Indian Express]]|publisher=Express Publications|accessdate=28 August 2014|date=8 August 2010}}</ref>
