'''Virginia Marshall''' (born c. 1919) was a child motion picture actress in [[silent film]] from 1924 through 1928.

==Brief stardom==
Marshall appeared in a ''[[Los Angeles Times]]'' feature entitled ''Stars Tomorrow?'' in April 1925.<ref>Los Angeles Times, ''Stars Tomorrow?'', April 1, 1925, Page C4.</ref> She was among a group of children that included [[Vonda Phelps]] and [[Virginia Davis]]. The article questioned whether their stardom would continue or fade away with time. In fact the nine youth performers profiled only remained popular with theatre and film audiences for a brief time, Marshall's motion picture career lasted from 1924â€“1928. She performed in thirteen screenplays. The films include comedy shorts like ''Little Robinson Corkscrew'' (1924), the [[melodrama]], ''[[East Lynne (1925 film)|East Lynne]]'' (1925), and the western, ''[[The Arizona Wildcat]]'' (1927). Marshall was given the opportunity to act with many stars of her era, such as [[Tom Mix]], [[Alma Rubens]], [[Edmund Lowe]], and [[Dorothy Revier]].
