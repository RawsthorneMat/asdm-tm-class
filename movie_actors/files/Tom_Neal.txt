'''Thomas Carroll "Tom" Neal, Jr.''' (January 28, 1914 – August 7, 1972), known professionally as '''''Tom Neal''''', was an American actor and boxer best known for appearing in the critically lauded film ''[[Detour (1945 film)|Detour]]'', having a tryst with [[Barbara Payton]] and later committing [[manslaughter]].

==Career==
Born in [[Evanston, Illinois]], Neal was one of three children born to banker Thomas, Sr. and Mayme Neal (''née'' Martin). He had two older sisters, Mary Elizabeth and Dorothy Helen.<ref name=odowdbio>{{cite book|last=O'Dowd|first=John |title=Kiss Tomorrow Goodbye: The Barbara Payton Story|year=2007|publisher=BearManor Media|isbn=1-593-93063-1|pages=147-148}}</ref> 
His great uncle was [[John Drew (actor)|John Drew]].<ref>{{cite book|last1=Morton|first1=Lisa|last2=Adamson|first2=Kent|title=Savage Detours: The Life and Work of Ann Savage|year=2009|publisher=McFarland|isbn=0-786-45706-6|pages=227–228}}</ref> Neal and his sisters were raised in a spacious ten-room home in [[Chicago]]. He attended [[Lake Forest Academy]] and [[Evanston Township High School]] before enrolling at [[Northwestern University]] where he majored in [[mathematics]].<ref name=odowd>O'Dowd 2007 p.148</ref> During college, Neal played several sports and, for a time, competed in amateur boxing matches.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Morton|first1=Lisa|last2=Adamson|first2=Kent|title=Savage Detours: The Life and Work of Ann Savage|year=2009|publisher=McFarland|isbn=0-786-45706-6|pages=227–228}}</ref> He was also a member the [[Sigma Chi]] fraternity and was active in the drama club.<ref name=odowd/>

Neal dropped out of Northwestern after a year, and moved back to Chicago. He appeared in various stage productions in [[Summer stock theatre|summer stock]] before making his way to New York City in 1933.<ref name=odowd/> Neal made his [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] debut in 1935. In 1938, he first appeared in film in ''Out West with the Hardys'', part of the [[Mickey Rooney]] "Hardy family" movie series. 

Neal appeared in many low budget [[B-movies]] in the 1940s/1950s. In 1941 he starred with [[Frances Gifford]] in the [[Republic Pictures]] 15 episode [[Serial (film)|serial]], ''[[Jungle Girl (Serial)|Jungle Girl]].'' Perhaps his most memorable role was that of Al Roberts in the classic [[film noir]] ''[[Detour (1945 film)|Detour]]'' alongside [[Ann Savage (actress)|Ann Savage]]. They went on to make five movies together.

==Personal life==
Neal was married three times and had one child. His first marriage was to actress and singer Vicky Lane whom he married in 1944. Lane divorced Neal in 1949 citing "mental and physical cruelty".<ref>{{cite book|last=Rainey|first=Buck|title=Serial Film Stars: A Biographical Dictionary, 1912-1956|year=2005|publisher=McFarland|isbn=0-786-42010-3|page=555}}</ref>

In the early 1950s, Neal met actress [[Barbara Payton]] at a party. The two began dating but Payton ended the relationship after meeting and becoming engaged to actor [[Franchot Tone]].<ref>{{cite book|last=Polito|first=Robert|title=Hollywood & God|year=2009|publisher=University of Chicago Press|isbn=0-226-67341-3|page=7}}</ref> Despite her engagement, Payton began seeing Neal again.<ref name="bulletin">{{cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1243&dat=19510929&id=Ru8sAAAAIBAJ&sjid=8PcDAAAAIBAJ&pg=6640,801620|title=Actor Tone, Barbara Payton, Wed In 'Quickie' Ceremony|date=September 29, 1951|work=The Bulletin|page=8|accessdate=December 7, 2014}}</ref> On September 14, 1951, Neal, Payton and Tone made headlines after Neal got into a physical altercation with Tone over Payton in her front yard.<ref name=PSLife>{{cite web |url=http://www.palmspringslife.com/Palm-Springs-Life/Whispering-Palms/Killer-Career-Actor-Tom-Neal/ |title=Killer Career – Actor Tom Neal |author=Arthur Lyons |publisher=''Palm Springs Life'' magazine |accessdate=November 4, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=110&dat=19510915&id=mZpOAAAAIBAJ&sjid=oUIDAAAAIBAJ&pg=4870,620644|title=Actor Tom Neal Ko's Love Rival Franchot Tone|date=September 15, 1951|work=Ludington Daily News|page=1|accessdate=December 7, 2014}}</ref> Neal beat Tone severely while Payton reportedly watched the fight. Tone suffered severe injuries, including a smashed cheekbone, a broken nose and a brain [[concussion]] for which he was hospitalized.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1298&dat=19510915&id=j91LAAAAIBAJ&sjid=tYoDAAAAIBAJ&pg=3992,1354357|title=Franchot Tone Still in Semi-coma After Beating Over Miss Payton|date=September 15, 1951|work=The Free Lance-Star|page=1|accessdate=December 7, 2014}}</ref> After he recovered, Tone and Payton married on September 28, 1951.<ref name="bulletin"/> Payton left Tone after 53 days and returned to Neal.<ref name="beaver">{{cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=2002&dat=19720808&id=x7IiAAAAIBAJ&sjid=abMFAAAAIBAJ&pg=927,1993234|title=Ex-Movie Star Tom Neal Dies|date=August 8, 1972|work=Beaver County Times|pages=A–4|accessdate=December 7, 2014}}</ref> Tone filed for divorce in March 1952 citing Payton with [[adultery]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1876&dat=19520426&id=iVssAAAAIBAJ&sjid=O8sEAAAAIBAJ&pg=7113,6127395|title=Franchot Says Wife, Neal Had Relations|date=April 25, 1952|work=Herald-Journal|page=32|accessdate=December 7, 2014}}</ref> Neal and Payton announced their engagement in May 1953 but eventually ended their relationship later that year.<ref>O'Dowd 2007 pp.243, 248</ref> 

Shortly after their breakup, Neal married Patricia Fenton. His only child, Patrick Thomas Neal, was born in 1957. Fenton died the following year from cancer.<ref name=hannsberry>{{cite book|last=Burroughs Hannsberry|first=Karen|title=Bad Boys: The Actors of Film Noir|year=2003|publisher=McFarland|isbn=0-786-41484-7|page=485}}</ref> In 1992, Patrick Neal (who goes by the name Tom Neal, Jr.) appeared in one film, playing the role of Al Roberts in a 1992 independent remake of ''[[Detour (1945 film)#Remake|Detour]].''<ref>O'Dowd 2007 p.156</ref><ref>{{cite book|last=Marcus|first=Greil |title=The Shape of Things to Come: Prophecy and the American Voice|year=2007|publisher=Macmillian|isbn=0-312-42642-9|page=130}}</ref>

==Later years and death==
After his much publicized fight with Franchot Tone, Neal was blacklisted in Hollywood as was Payton.<ref name="beaver"/> He acted sporadically but became more known for his tumultuous on-and-off relationship with Payton. Neal and Payton attempted to capitalize on the interest in their relationship by starring together in the low budget Western ''The Great Jesse James Raid'', in 1953. The film did reasonably well but did nothing to revitalize the couple's careers.<ref>O'Dowd 2007 p.242</ref> In June 1953, Neal and Payton accepted an offer to star in the touring production of ''The Postman Always Rings Twice''. Their performances were largely panned and the tour ended in September 1953. Neal and Payton broke up for the final time in November 1953.<ref>O'Dowd 2007 p.242</ref>

With his acting career over, Neal moved to [[Palm Springs, California]] and became a gardener. He later started his own landscaping business.<ref name="beaver"/><ref name="daytona">{{cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1873&dat=19650403&id=CKgoAAAAIBAJ&sjid=ossEAAAAIBAJ&pg=4555,454755|title=Tom Neal Quizzed In Killing|date=April 3, 1965|work=Daytona Beach Morning Journal|page=1|accessdate=December 7, 2014}}</ref> In 1961, Neal married receptionist Gale Bennett in Las Vegas. On April 2, 1965, police were summoned to the couple's Palm Springs home by Neal's attorney.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1876&dat=19650403&id=Tn0sAAAAIBAJ&sjid=4swEAAAAIBAJ&pg=6041,468901|title=Ex-Actor Tom Neal Jailed, Wife Found Shot To Death|date=April 3, 1965|work=Herald-Journal|page=5|accessdate=December 7, 2014}}</ref> They discovered Bennett's body on the couch partially covered by a blanket with a gunshot wound in the back of her head. It was later determined that Bennett had been shot with a .45 caliber gun on April 1.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=2245&dat=19650402&id=ojgzAAAAIBAJ&sjid=0jIHAAAAIBAJ&pg=5423,296266|title=Former Actor Tom Neal Charged In Wife's Murder|date=April 3, 1965|work=Lodi News-Sentinel|page=12|accessdate=December 7, 2014}}</ref> Neal, who was not at the home when police arrived, became an immediate suspect. He surrendered to police on April 3 and was [[indicted]] on one charge of murder on April 10.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=757&dat=19650405&id=5oMwAAAAIBAJ&sjid=zkQDAAAAIBAJ&pg=3524,273804|title=Tom Neal Jailed On Suspicion|date=April 5, 1965|work=The Virgin Islands Daily News|page=2|accessdate=December 7, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1350&dat=19650409&id=aSYxAAAAIBAJ&sjid=VwEEAAAAIBAJ&pg=3494,3388621|title=Former Actor Indicted In Slaying Of Wife|date=April 10, 1965|work=Toledo Blade|page=15|accessdate=December 7, 2014}}</ref> 

At his trial, Neal admitted that he and Bennett were separated at the time of her death but that her death was accidental. He testified that on April 1, he had returned to the couple's Palm Springs home from Chicago where he had been living to see if a reconciliation was possible.<ref name="miami">{{cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=2206&dat=19651109&id=TLoyAAAAIBAJ&sjid=HusFAAAAIBAJ&pg=3140,2833695|title=Tom Neal Tells Of Wife's Dating|date=November 9, 1965|work=The Miami News|page=16A|accessdate=December 7, 2014}}</ref> Neal said the two began fighting after he accused Bennett of sleeping with other men. He claimed that Bennett pulled out a gun, held it to his head and the two began to struggle. During the ensuing struggle, Neal said that the gun accidentally discharged, killing Bennett. Although prosecutors sought the [[death penalty]], a jury convicted Neal of [[involuntary manslaughter]] on November 18, 1965.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=348&dat=19651119&id=5uVMAAAAIBAJ&sjid=wjUDAAAAIBAJ&pg=3200,1978161|title=Former Actor Found Guilty|date=November 19, 1965|work=Rome News-Tribune|page=1|accessdate=December 7, 2014}}</ref> On December 10, he was sentenced to one-to-fifteen years in prison, of which he served six. On December 6, 1971, he was released on [[parole]].<ref name=PSLife/><ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1454&dat=19651211&id=0xNiAAAAIBAJ&sjid=WXQNAAAAIBAJ&pg=3541,2071151|title=Actor Imprisoned For Manslaughter|date=December 11, 1965|work=Star-News|page=17|accessdate=December 7, 2014}}</ref> After his release, Neal went back to working as a landscaper and gardener. 

On August 7, 1972, Neal was found dead in his bed by his son at his home in [[North Hollywood, California]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1310&dat=19720808&id=jN1VAAAAIBAJ&sjid=5eADAAAAIBAJ&pg=5440,1816707|title=Ex-film actor Tom Neal found dead at 59|date=August 8, 1952|work=Eugene Register-Guard|page=4A|accessdate=December 7, 2014}}</ref> His death was later attributed to [[heart failure]].<ref name=hannsberry/> He was cremated, and his ashes were scattered in the Pacific Ocean.<ref>O'Dowd 2007 p.420</ref>
