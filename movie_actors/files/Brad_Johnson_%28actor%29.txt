'''Brad Johnson''' (born October 24, 1959) is an [[United States|American]] actor and former [[Marlboro Man]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Sanz |first=Cynthia |url=http://www.people.com/people/archive/article/0,,20116874,00.html |title=An Ex-Marlboro Man Who Can Really Ride, Brad Johnson Adds Sigh Appeal to Always |publisher=People.com |date=1990-02-19 |accessdate=2012-05-08}}</ref>

Johnson was born in [[Tucson]], [[Arizona]], the son of Virginia and Grove Johnson.<ref>http://www.filmreference.com/film/99/Brad-Johnson.html</ref> Johnson's first acting credit was in the low-budget biker film ''The Nam Angels'' (1989), and shortly after he had his first co-starring role in a major film when he played the role of Ted Baker in the 1989 film ''[[Always (1989 film)|Always]]'', directed by [[Steven Spielberg]]. His other movie credits include ''[[Flight of the Intruder]]'', ''[[Philadelphia Experiment II]]'', ''[[The Birds II: Land's End]]'', ''[[Copperhead (2008 film)|Copperhead]]'' and ''[[Supergator]]''.

He also played the role of [[Rayford Steele]] in the [[Left Behind film series|''Left Behind'' film series]], and played Dr. Dominick O'Malley on ''[[Melrose Place]]''. In 2001, he played a hired killer who tangled with [[Tom Selleck]]'s Rafe Covington character in [[Turner Network Television]]'s ''[[Crossfire Trail (film)|Crossfire Trail]]''.

He has six daughters and four sons.
