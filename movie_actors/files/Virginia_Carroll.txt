'''Virginia Carroll''' (December 2, 1913 – July 23, 2009) was an [[United States|American]] [[actress]]. She was best known for her appearance in a number of [[western films]]

Carroll was born '''Virginia Broberg''' in [[Los Angeles]] on December 2, 1913.<ref name=lat>{{cite news |first=|last=|title=Passings: Virginia Carroll, Character actress and leading lady
|url=http://www.latimes.com/news/obituaries/la-me-passings30-2009jul30,0,6400846.story|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|publisher=|date=2009-07-30 |accessdate=2009-08-01}}</ref>  Her brother, Frank Carroll, became a Los Angeles newscaster.<ref name=variety>{{cite news |first=Pat|last=Saperstein|title=Virginia Carroll dies at 95|url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1118006682.html?categoryId=13&cs=1 |work=[[Variety Magazine]]|publisher=|date=2009-07-30 |accessdate=2009-08-01}}</ref> She worked as a model at a department store in Los Angeles until she began her film career in 1935.

Carroll was initially cast in a small part as a fashion model in the 1935 film, ''[[Roberta (1935 film)|Roberta]]''.<ref name=lat/> She appeared in her first western film less than a year later in 1936's ''A Tenderfoot Goes West'', co-starring [[Jack La Rue]].<ref name=lat/>

Carroll became a staple in B-listed western [[feature film]]s.  She appeared on screen in these westerns opposite [[Tex Ritter]], [[Don "Red" Barry]], [[Roy Rogers]], [[Johnny Mack Brown]], [[Bill Elliott]], [[Gene Autry]] and [[Whip Wilson]].<ref name=lat/>  Her roles included the 1942 film, ''Prairie Gunsmoke''.

Carroll was also cast in television roles later in her acting career, including roles on ''[[The Roy Rogers Show]]'', ''[[Dragnet (series)|Dragnet]]'', ''[[The Adventures of Wild Bill Hickok]]'' and ''[[Perry Mason]]''.<ref name=lat/>

Carroll married her first husband, actor [[Ralph Byrd]], in 1936.<ref name=lat/> Byrd would become best known for his role as [[comic strip]] hero, [[Dick Tracy]].<ref name=lat/> Carroll and Byrd remained married until his death in 1952.<ref name=lat/> She was married to her second husband, Lloyd McLean, a [[20th Century Fox]] film [[projectionist]], in 1957.<ref name=lat/> The couple remained married until McLean's death in 1969, leaving Carroll a [[widow]] for the second time.<ref name=lat/>

Virginia Carroll died at a [[Santa Barbara, California]], [[retirement community]] on July 23, 2009, of natural causes at the age of 95.<ref name=lat/> She is survived by one daughter<ref name=variety/> from her marriage to Ralph Byrd, Carroll Byrd Evangeline.<ref name=lat/>
