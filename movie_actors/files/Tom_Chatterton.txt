'''Tom Chatterton''' (February 12, 1881 â€“ August 17, 1952) was an American actor.

Born in [[Geneva, New York]], Chatterton began his film career in 1913 at the [[New York Motion Picture Company]] under director [[Thomas H. Ince]]. Although never a major star, Chatterton had several leading roles in early [[silent film]]s. He appeared in a large number of [[Western film|westerns]] and was able to adapt to [[talkies]] allowing him to have a successful career lasting five decades.

He died in Hollywood in 1952 and was interred in the Glenwood Cemetery in his hometown of Geneva.
