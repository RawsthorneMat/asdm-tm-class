'''Kipp Hamilton''' (August 16, 1934 &ndash; January 29, 1981) was an American [[actress]], singer and model. She was the younger sister of producer [[Joe Hamilton (producer)|Joe Hamilton]] and the [[sister-in-law]] of [[Carol Burnett]].

==Early life and family==
She was born '''Rita Marie Hamilton''' in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]], the youngest of six children. One her older brothers was producer and actor [[Joe Hamilton (producer)|Joe Hamilton]] who later married comedian [[Carol Burnett]].<ref name="cloud">{{cite news|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1144&dat=19640505&id=WEcqAAAAIBAJ&sjid=Bk8EAAAAIBAJ&pg=7263,1906441&hl=en|title=Kipp Hamilton Dislikes Being Called 'Starlet'|last=Cloud|first=Barbara|date=May 6, 1964|work=The Pittsburgh Press|page=27|accessdate=April 14, 2015|location=Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania}}</ref>

==Career==
Hamilton made her film debut in a supporting role in the [[RKO Radio Pictures]] drama ''[[Our Very Own (1950 film)|On Our Very Own]]''. In March 1953, she was named "Miss Optometry" by the New York State Association of Optometrists.<ref name="glamour">{{cite web|url=http://www.glamourgirlsofthesilverscreen.com/show.php?id=116|title=Kipp Hamilton Bio|publisher=glamourgirlsofthesilverscreen.com}}</ref> In mid-1955, Hamilton signed a seven-year contract with [[20th Century Fox]].<ref name="scott">{{cite news|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1917&dat=19550816&id=RnQuAAAAIBAJ&sjid=pYAFAAAAIBAJ&pg=3116,2222884&hl=en|title=Kipp Hamilton's Allergic, Of All Things, Mink|last=Scott|first=Vernon|date=August 16, 1955|work=Schenectady Gazette|page=8|accessdate=April 14, 2015|location=Schenectady, New York}}</ref> Shortly after signing with Fox, Hamilton (along with nine other up-and-coming actresses including [[Anita Ekberg]] and [[Lori Nelson]]) was named a "Deb Star of 1955".<ref>{{cite journal|date=October 3, 1955|title=Makeup Artists Pick 10 Stars of Future|journal=Motion Picture Daily|publisher=Quigley Publishing Co.|location=New York City|volume=78|issue=65|page=2|issn=0027-1594}}</ref> That same year, Hamilton was cast in her first major role in the drama ''[[Good Morning, Miss Dove]]'', playing Jincey Baker.<ref name="scott"/>

The following year, Hamilton asked to be released from her contract with Fox.<ref name="petersburg">{{cite news|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=888&dat=19590701&id=tTtSAAAAIBAJ&sjid=_3YDAAAAIBAJ&pg=5867,345125&hl=en|title=Rising Actress Admits She Is Now a Spinster|date=July 1, 1959|work=St. Petersburg Times|pages=8–C|accessdate=April 14, 2015|location=St. Petersburg, Florida}}</ref> Throughout the remainder of the 1950s, Hamilton appeared in guest roles on ''[[Perry Mason (TV series)|Perry Mason]]'', ''[[Meet McGraw]]'', ''[[Richard Diamond, Private Detective]]'', ''[[The Life and Legend of Wyatt Earp]]'', ''[[The Texan (TV series)|The Texan]]'' and ''[[77 Sunset Strip]]''. In 1959, Hamilton appeared in a supporting role opposite [[Frank Sinatra]] in ''[[Never So Few]]''. Later that same year, she signed with [[Hecht Hill Lancaster]] (the production company partially owned by actor [[Burt Lancaster]]) and was cast in the Western ''[[The Unforgiven (1960 film)|The Unforgiven]]'' (1960).<ref name="petersburg"/> In addition to television work, Hamilton also had a nightclub act that she performed at Lou Black's Living Room, a club in [[Montreal]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1946&dat=19610727&id=BYwtAAAAIBAJ&sjid=P50FAAAAIBAJ&pg=6959,4308635&hl=en|title=Get Career Out Of Your System Actress Tells Marriage-Bound|date=July 7, 1961|work=The Montreal Gazette|page=18|accessdate=April 14, 2015|location=Montreal, Canada}}</ref>

In November 1963, Hamilton began touring in the road production of the musical ''[[How to Succeed in Business Without Really Trying]]''. She remained with the production until mid-1964.<ref name="cloud"/> In 1965, she returned to television with the guest starring role on the ''[[Bewitched]]'' episode "Pleasure O'Reilly" in which she played the title character.<ref>{{cite book|last=Pilato|first=Herbie J. |title=The Essential Elizabeth Montgomery: A Guide to Her Magical Performances|year=2013|publisher=Taylor Trade Publishing|isbn=1-589-79825-2|page=44}}</ref> The following year, [[Henry G. Saperstein]] cast Hamilton in what would be her final film role, a singer in the Japanese [[Kaiju]] movie, ''[[The War of the Gargantuas]]''. Billed as a "Special Guest Star", Hamilton performs the song "The Words Get Stuck In My Throat" which was later covered by [[Devo]].<ref>{{cite book|last=Macias|first=Patrick |title=Tokyo Scope|year=2001|publisher=Cadence Books|isbn=1-569-31681-3|page=22}}</ref> Hamilton's final onscreen role was in a 1967 episode of the Western ''[[The Virginian (TV series)|The Virginian]]''.

==Personal life==
In 1958, Hamilton dated and later became engaged to film mogul [[Adolph Zukor]]'s son Eugene (also known as Adolph Zukor III), an executive at [[Paramount Pictures]]. Hamilton called off the engagement in March 1959 while she was filming ''The Unforgiven'' in [[Durango]], [[Mexico]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1368&dat=19590320&id=RH1QAAAAIBAJ&sjid=lg8EAAAAIBAJ&pg=7064,4686697&hl=en|title=Louella Parsons In Hollywood: Niven Favored|last=Parsons|first=Louella|date=March 20, 1959|work=The Milwaukee Sentinel|page=3|accessdate=April 14, 2015|location=Milwaukee, Wisconsin}}</ref>

In February 1962, Hamilton married director David Geisel whom she had met on the set of ''[[The Garry Moore Show#Second version (1958–1964)|The Garry Moore Show]]'' which her brother Joe produced. They had a daughter, Marie, in 1963 and separated the following year. They were divorced in June 1965.<ref name="glamour"/>  

==Later years and death==
After retiring from acting, Hamilton married Beverly Hills lawyer Donald Thorman Rosenfeld in February 1968. They had a daughter, Dana, in October 1968. They remained married until Hamilton's death on January 29, 1981.<ref name=glamour /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://articles.latimes.com/1986-09-22/local/me-8656_1_beverly-hills|title=Beverly Hills Attorney Donald T. Rosenfeld Dies|date=September 22, 1986|publisher=latimes.com|accessdate=April 14, 2015}}</ref>
