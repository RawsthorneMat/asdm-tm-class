'''Jon Locke''' (October 10, 1927 â€“ October 19, 2013) was an [[United States|American]] [[actor]], who often specialized in television and film [[Western (genre)|westerns]].<ref name=latimes>{{cite news|first=|last=||title=Passings: Noel Harrison, Jon Locke, Jamalul Kiram III  |url=http://articles.latimes.com/2013/oct/22/local/la-me-passings-20131023 |work=[[Los Angeles Times]] |date=2013-10-22 |accessdate=2013-11-13}}</ref> His television credits included westerns, including ''[[Bonanza]]'', ''[[Gunsmoke]]'', and ''[[The Virginian (TV series)|The Virginian]]'', as well as non-western series such as ''[[The Bionic Woman]]'',''[[The Dukes of Hazzard]]'', ''[[The Mary Tyler Moore Show]]'', and ''[[Perry Mason (TV series)|Perry Mason]]''.<ref name=latimes/> Locke had recurring roles as Officer Garvey on the 1950s  [[Police procedural|crime drama series]], ''[[Highway Patrol (TV series)|Highway Patrol]]'', and as a [[Sleestak]] leader in the [[NBC]] [[television series]], ''[[Land of the Lost (1974 TV series)|Land of the Lost]]'', during the 1970s.<ref name=latimes/>

Locke was born '''Joseph Lockey Yon''' on October 10, 1927, in [[Orlando, Florida]].<ref name=latimes/> He became involved in theater while earning his [[bachelor's degree]] at [[Florida State University]].<ref name=latimes/> Locke enlisted in the [[United States Air Force]], where he began performing in a play about Korean War pilots called "Flame-Out."<ref name=latimes/>

Outside of television, Locke played the [[banjo]] for an annual western film festival held in [[Lone Pine, California]].<ref name=latimes/> He belonged to the Reel Cowboy, which are a social group of western actors who meet in the [[San Fernando Valley]].<ref name=latimes/> In addition to acting, Locke worked as an office manager for a [[real estate]] firm in San Fernando.<ref name=latimes/>

Jon Locke, a longtime resident of [[Van Nuys, California]], died from complications of a stroke at Holy Cross Medical Center in [[Mission Hills, California]], on October 19, 2013, at the age of 86.<ref name=latimes/>
