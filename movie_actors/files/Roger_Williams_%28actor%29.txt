'''Roger Williams''' (February 8, 1898 - December 18, 1964) was an American actor of the 1930s.  Born on February 8, 1898, in Denver Colorado, his family would move to the Los Angeles area during the early 1900s. He served in the US Army during World War I, being awarded several decorations. Married several times, he would enter the film industry in the early 1930s, where he would work for several years.  Married three times, he had a short film career during the 1930s, after which not much is known of his life, until his death in Los Angeles in 1964.

==Early life==
Williams was born in Denver, Colorado, on February 8, 1898, to Charles H. Williams and Evangeline Lloyd.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://familysearch.org/pal:/MM9.1.1/JGWX-968 | publisher=FamilySearch | title=Roger Williams, United States Social Security Death Index | accessdate=January 15, 2015}}</ref><ref name=Marriage>{{cite web | url=https://familysearch.org/pal:/MM9.3.1/TH-1942-29949-29793-59?cc=1804002&wc=M9MJ-QJV:n1782320201 | publisher=FamilySearch | title=California, County Marriages, 1850–1952 | accessdate=January 15, 2015}}</ref> He was the oldest of seven children.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://familysearch.org/pal:/MM9.1.1/MHQD-HTZ | publisher=FamilySearch | title=United States Census, 1920 | accessdate=January 15, 2015}}</ref> By 1910 the family had moved to California, and were living Belvedere, which was in Los Angeles County.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://familysearch.org/pal:/MM9.1.1/MV2L-WXQ | publisher=FamilySearch | title=United States Census, 1910 | accessdate=January 15, 2015}}</ref> He enlisted in the army during the mid-1910s, lying about his age, which would later cause some confusion about his true date of birth, which several sources still incorrectly report.<ref name=RegCard>{{cite web | url=https://familysearch.org/pal:/MM9.3.1/TH-1971-26178-37677-11?cc=1968530&wc=M9W1-Q2Q:1020872091 | publisher=FamilySearch | title=United States World War I Registration Cards | accessdate=January 15, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0931572/?ref_=fn_nm_nm_4 | publisher=IMDB | title=Roger Williams (I) | accessdate=January 15, 2015}}</ref> Press releases during his film career claim that he was awarded the [[Purple Heart]] and the [[Croix de Guerre]] during World War I.<ref>{{cite journal | journal=Song of the Trail Pressbook | publisher=Ambassador Pictures | year=1936}}</ref> He was married for the first time on June 6, 1916, to Vera Paloma Bennett of Utah.  At the time, he claimed to be a motion picture actor, however no records can be found of his involvement in the film industry at this time.<ref name=Marriage /> By the time he registered for the draft for World War I, he was already divorced from his first wife.<ref name=RegCard />  In 1920 he would marry his second wife, Ruby B. Noe, in Salt Lake City, Utah, on February 7, 1920.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://familysearch.org/pal:/MM9.1.1/F8GT-BRX | publisher=FamilySearch | title=Utah, Marriages, 1887–1966 | accessdate=January 15, 2015}}</ref> By 1930, he had divorced and remarried for the third and final time, to his wife, Ellen, by which point the couple had two children.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://familysearch.org/pal:/MM9.1.1/XCVM-WLF | publisher=FamilySearch | title=United States Census, 1930 | accessdate=January 15, 2015}}</ref> During the 1920s he would work as a tradesman in Los Angeles.<ref name=BW />

==Film career==
Williams made his film debut in 1933, in a supporting role in ''[[Trouble Busters]]''.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.afi.com/members/catalog/DetailView.aspx?s=&Movie=6930 | publisher=American Film Institute | title=Trouble Busters | accessdate=January 15, 2015}}</ref><ref name=BW2>{{cite web | url=http://www.b-westerns.com/villan51.htm | publisher=b-westerns.com | title=Roger Williams Filmography | accessdate=January 15, 2015}}</ref> During his time in films, he would be given roles across the gamut of acting, from bit roles as in 1935's ''[[Saddle Aces]]'',<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.afi.com/members/catalog/DetailView.aspx?s=&Movie=4489 | publisher=American Film Institute | title=Saddle Aces | accessdate=January 15, 2015}}</ref>  to supporting parts, as in ''[[The Riders of the Whistling Skull]]'',<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.afi.com/members/catalog/DetailView.aspx?s=&Movie=1172 | publisher=American Film Institute | title=The Riders of the Whistling Skull | accessdate=January 15, 2015}}</ref> to starring roles as in ''[[Heroes of the Alamo]]''.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.afi.com/members/catalog/DetailView.aspx?s=&Movie=5694 | publisher=American Film Institute | title=Heroes of the Alamo | accessdate=January 15, 2015}}</ref> During his short 7 year career he appeared in over 100 films, the vast majority of them [[Western (genre)#Film|westerns]].<ref name=BW />

==Personal life==
Williams and his third wife, Ellen, would have four children, two daughters (Waneta and Delores) and two sons (Roger and Arthur);<ref name=Census1940>{{cite web | url=https://familysearch.org/pal:/MM9.3.1/TH-1971-27797-1145-86?cc=2000219&wc=M9Q8-Y6D:1125289482 | publisher=FamilySearch | title=United States Census, 1940 | accessdate=January 15, 2015}}</ref> he would remain married to Ellen until his death in 1964.<ref name=FaG>{{cite web| url=http://www.findagrave.com/cgi-bin/fg.cgi?page=gr&GSln=williams&GSfn=roger&GSiman=1&GScid=8437&GRid=59335002& | publisher=Find a Grave | title=Roger G Williams | accessdate=January 15, 2015}}</ref> After leaving the film industry, he spent some time as an engineer for Douglas Aircraft.<ref name=Census1940 /> Williams died on December 18, 1964, in Los Angeles, California.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://familysearch.org/pal:/MM9.1.1/VP4F-467 | publisher=FamilySearch | title=California, Death Index | accessdate=January 15, 2015}}</ref>
