'''Douglas Osborne''' "'''Doug'''" '''McClure''' (May 11, 1935 – February 5, 1995) was an American actor whose career in film and television extended from the 1950s to the 1990s. He is best known for his role as the [[cowboy]] Trampas during the entire run from 1962 to 1971 of the [[NBC]] [[western (genre)|western]] [[television series]], ''[[The Virginian (TV series)|The Virginian]]'', loosely based on the [[Owen Wister]] [[novel]].

==Background==
McClure was born in [[Glendale, California|Glendale]], [[California]]. His [[English people|English]] mother, Clara Elsie ([[Married and maiden names|née]] Barker; 1907–1997), had moved to the United States from her native [[United Kingdom]] in 1915, when her widowed mother married an American, Frank S Artman. Clara Barker was naturalised as an American citizen in 1918, and married [[Irish people|Irish]]-American Donald Reed McClure (1904–1965) in 1929. Donald and Clara were parents to Donald Reed McClure, Jr. (1931–2003) and then Doug. The widow Clara would marry Frank Clapp in 1971.

==Career==
McClure's acting career included such films as ''[[The Enemy Below]]'', ''[[The Unforgiven (1960 film)|The Unforgiven]]'', and ''[[Because They're Young]]'', before landing the part of Trampas on ''The Virginian'' – a part that would make him famous. He also starred in four other series:

# as Frank "Flip" Flippen on NBC's western, ''[[Overland Trail (TV series)|Overland Trail]]'' (1960), with co-star [[William Bendix]]
# as Jed Sills on the [[Columbia Broadcasting System|CBS]] [[detective]] series ''[[Checkmate (TV series)|Checkmate]]'' (1960–62) opposite [[Anthony George]] as Don Corey and [[Sebastian Cabot (actor)|Sebastian Cabot]] as Dr. Carl Hyatt
# as C.R. (Christopher Robin) Grover in the sci-fi/detective series ''[[Search (TV series)|Search]]'' (1972–1973) in which he rotated the lead with [[Hugh O'Brian]] and [[Anthony Franciosa]] as a high-tech PROBE agent, and
# in the series ''[[Out of This World (TV series)|Out of This World]]'' (1987–1991) as Mayor Kyle Applegate.

McClure had a minor part in 1957 as an [[United States Army|Army]] officer in "California Gold Rush in Reverse" on the [[Broadcast syndication|syndicated]] [[anthology]] series ''[[Death Valley Days]]''. The episode is a dramatization of the race in 1848 between the Army and the [[United States Navy|Navy]] to be the first to deliver gold nuggets from California to [[Washington, D.C.]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0556568/fullcredits#cast|title=''Death Valley Days'': "California Gold Rush in Reverse", 1957|publisher=[[Internet Movie Data Base]]|accessdate=October 29, 2012}}</ref>

In 1958 and 1959 McClure appeared in three episodes of the syndicated western series ''[[26 Men]]'', stories of the [[Arizona Rangers]]. 

He appeared as Adam Davis in 1959 in the episode "The Court Martial of Trooper Davis" of another syndicated western series, ''[[Mackenzie's Raiders]]'', starring [[Richard Carlson (actor)|Richard Carlson]] and set in southwest [[Texas]].<ref>Billy Hathorn, "Roy Bean, Temple Houston, Bill Longley, Ranald Mackenzie, Buffalo Bill, Jr., and the Texas Rangers: Depictions of West Texans in Series Television, 1955 to 1967", ''[[West Texas Historical Association|West Texas Historical Review]]'', Vol. 89 (2013), p. 113</ref>
 
McClure was in the third episode "[[Mr. Denton on Doomsday]]", of CBS's ''[[The Twilight Zone (1959 TV series)|The Twilight Zone]]''. 

Then in 1962, he was cast as Trampas in NBC's ''The Virginian''. His co-stars throughout the series were [[James Drury]], [[Roberta Shore]], [[Lee J. Cobb]], [[Randy Boone]], [[Gary Clarke]], [[Clu Gulager]], [[Diane Roter]], [[Charles Bickford]], Sara Lane, [[Tim Matheson]], [[Jeanette Nolan]], and [[John McIntire]].

After the show ended in 1971, McClure was slated to co-star with [[Bette Davis]] in a series about a parolee assisting a judge, played by Davis, by doing detective work.  The pilot, produced and written by the prolific team of [[Richard Levinson]] and [[William Link]], failed to generate interest in the series and was released as a [[Television film|telemovie]] under the title "[[The Judge and Jake Wyler]]".<ref>{{cite news|last=Shipman|first=David|title=OBITUARY: Doug McClure|url=http://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/obituary-doug-mcclure-1571880.html|accessdate=13 January 2013|newspaper=The Independent|date=7 Feb 1995}}</ref>   McClure starred in [[science fiction]] [[film]]s such as ''[[At the Earth's Core (film)|At the Earth's Core]]'', ''[[The Land That Time Forgot (1975 film)|The Land That Time Forgot]]'' and ''[[The People That Time Forgot (film)|The People That Time Forgot]]'', all three based on the novels of [[Edgar Rice Burroughs]]. In 1967, he played the [[Errol Flynn]] role in a re-make of ''Against All Flags'' titled ''[[The King's Pirate]]''. He was cast in the lead in three [[World War II]] adventures, ''The Longest Hundred Miles'',''The Birdmen'' and "State Of Division" Aka "Death Race". In the 1970s and 1980s, McClure appeared in commercials for [[Miller Brewing#Hamm's Family|Hamms Beer]].<ref>[http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1356&dat=19821029&id=4-sTAAAAIBAJ&sjid=-wUEAAAAIBAJ&pg=2679,7730738 Doug McClure Played Trampas and commercial] [[Dick Kleiner]] syndicated column, ''[[Ocala Star-Banner]]'', Oct 29, 1982.</ref>

In 1994, McClure was awarded a star on the [[Hollywood Walk of Fame]] for Television at 7065 Hollywood Blvd. It was unveiled in what was his final public appearance. == Death ==
On February 5, 1995, McClure died from [[lung cancer]] in [[Sherman Oaks, California|Sherman Oaks]], California. Only fifty-nine, McClure had been a heavy drinker and a lifelong smoker. He is interred at [[Woodlawn Memorial Cemetery]], in [[Santa Monica, California|Santa Monica]], California.  McClure was married to his fifth wife at the time of his death, and was also survived by daughter [[Tané McClure|Tané]] from his first marriage, and daughter Valerie from his fourth marriage. McClure was divorced four times, including twice while he was performing on ''The Virginian''.

== Other information ==
The character of [[Troy McClure]] on ''[[The Simpsons]]'' was designed after him along with the late [[Troy Donahue]]. [[Mike Reiss]], executive producer of ''The Simpsons'', said that Doug McClure's daughter informed him that Doug was a big fan of ''The Simpsons''. She said that while watching an episode Doug saw the character Troy McClure on the show and said, "Are they making fun of me?" Doug said he thought the parody was funny and his daughters would call him Troy McClure behind his back as a joke.<ref>''The Simpsons'', Season 2 DVD, Episode: "[[Homer vs. Lisa and the 8th Commandment]]"</ref> He also had a cameo role as a poker player in the 1994 movie remake of ''[[Maverick (film)|Maverick]]''.
