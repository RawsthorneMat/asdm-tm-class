'''Paul Stephen Rudd''' (born April 6, 1969)<ref>{{cite web|title=Paul Rudd biography|url=http://www.biography.com/people/paul-rudd-9542374|work=bio.|publisher=A+E Television Networks, LLC|accessdate=September 1, 2012|author=bio.|year=1996–2012}}</ref> is an American actor, comedian, writer, and producer. He has primarily appeared in comedies, and is known for his roles in the films ''[[Clueless (film)|Clueless]]'' (1995), ''[[Wet Hot American Summer]]'' (2001), ''[[Anchorman: The Legend of Ron Burgundy]]'' (2004), ''[[The 40-Year-Old Virgin]]'' (2005), ''[[Knocked Up]]'' (2007), ''[[I Love You, Man]]'' (2009), ''[[This Is 40]]'' (2012), and ''[[Anchorman 2: The Legend Continues]]'' (2013). In television, he appeared in the early 1990s on the NBC drama ''[[Sisters (TV series)|Sisters]]'' and some years later he appeared as [[List of recurring characters in Friends#Mike Hannigan|Mike Hannigan]] on the NBC sitcom ''[[Friends]]''. Other guest television roles have included an appearance on the ''[[Tim and Eric Awesome Show, Great Job!|Tim and Eric]]'' show, a recurring role on ''[[Parks and Recreation]]'' as politician [[Characters of Parks and Recreation#Bobby Newport|Bobby Newport]],<ref name=PnRS7>http://insidetv.ew.com/2014/12/29/parks-and-recreation-paul-rudd/</ref> and hosting ''[[Saturday Night Live]]''. Rudd has been cast as [[Ant-Man (Scott Lang)|Scott Lang / Ant-Man]] in [[Marvel Studios]]' ''[[Ant-Man (film)|Ant-Man]]'', to be released July 17, 2015 and will be reprising the character in ''[[Captain America: Civil War]]'', set for release May 6, 2016.

==Early life==
Rudd was born on April 6, 1969 in [[Passaic, New Jersey]]. His father, Michael, was a historical tour guide who was formerly the vice president of [[Trans World Airlines]], while his mother, Gloria, was a sales manager at the television station [[KSMO-TV]] in [[Kansas City, Missouri]].<ref name=jjournal1/><ref name="nytimes">{{cite news| last=Weiner| first=Caren| title=FILM; Suddenly the Object Of Much Attention| work=[[The New York Times]]| date=April 26, 1998| publisher=NYTimes.com| url=http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9F04E7D6163FF935A15757C0A96E958260| accessdate=December 10, 2007}}</ref><ref name="ref1108">{{cite news| last=Garron| first=Barry| title=Actor takes his career in stride For Paul Rudd, the cancellation of 'Wild Oats is merely a bend in the road| publisher=[[The Kansas City Star]]| date=October 23, 1994| url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=KC&p_theme=kc&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=0EAF40FAE443789D&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM| accessdate=November 10, 2008}}</ref> His parents were [[British people|British]], his father from [[Edgware]] and his mother from [[Surbiton]].<ref name="NJ">{{cite news| author=Stephen Whitty| url=http://www.nj.com/entertainment/tv/index.ssf/2009/03/behind_paul_rudd_theres_a_seri.html| title=Paul Rudd interview: He's happy to have a job – any job| work=[[The Star-Ledger]]| location=Newark| publisher=Nj.com| date=March 22, 2009| accessdate=July 30, 2011}}</ref><ref>Stated on ''[[The Graham Norton Show]]'', February 1, 2013</ref> Rudd's parents were both [[History of the Jews in England|Jewish]] (descended from Jewish immigrants who moved from [[History of the Jews in Russia|Russia]] and [[History of the Jews in Poland|Poland]] to England).<ref name="bang">{{cite web| url=http://www.bangitout.com/reviews50.html| title=Movies that Bang! his family also spent three years living in [[Anaheim, California]], because of his father's occupation.<ref name="ew">{{cite news| last=Karpel| first=Ari| url=http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,20040158,00.html| title=Spotlight on Paul Rudd: Comedy's go-to guy improvises his next career move after '&#39;Knocked Up'&#39| work=[[Entertainment Weekly]]| publisher=Ew.com| date=May 25, 2007| accessdate=July 30, 2011}}</ref><ref name=elgae>{{cite news| last=Mills| first=Nancy| title=Paul Rudd's offbeat 'Dinner' date| publisher=Reading Eagle| date=July 26, 2010| url=http://readingeagle.com/article.aspx?id=236952| accessdate=July 3, 2010}}</ref><ref name="playboy">{{cite web| title=Playboy Interview: Paul Rudd| url=http://www.playboy.com/magazine/playboy-interview-paul-rudd| author=Eric Spitznagel| date=October 2011| work=[[Playboy]]| publisher=Playboy.com| accessdate=January 29, 2012}}</ref>

Rudd attended [[Shawnee Mission School District#Former schools|Broadmoor Junior High]] and [[Shawnee Mission West High School]]. ==Career==

===Film and television===
Rudd made his acting debut in 1992 with the television drama ''[[Sisters (TV series)|Sisters]]'' where he played Kirby Quimby Philby. During that time, in 1994 he appeared in ''[[Wild Oats (TV series)|Wild Oats]]'' for 6 episodes. Rudd left ''Sisters'' in 1995 to then appear in the comedy film ''[[Clueless (film)|Clueless]]'' with [[Alicia Silverstone]].<ref name=Telegraph1>{{cite web|last=Billson|first=Anne|title=Paul Rudd's 10 best roles|url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/film/film-blog/10383663/Paul-Rudds-10-best-roles.html|publisher=Telegraph|accessdate=December 20, 2013|date=October 17, 2013}}</ref> He also appeared in ''[[Halloween: The Curse of Michael Myers]]'', ''[[William Shakespeare's Romeo + Juliet]]'', ''[[The Locusts (film)|The Locusts]]'', ''[[Overnight Delivery]]'', ''[[The Object of My Affection]]'', and ''[[200 Cigarettes]]''.<ref name=Telegraph1 /> He was then in the 1999 film ''[[The Cider House Rules (film)|The Cider House Rules]]'' that earned him and the cast a [[Screen Actors Guild|SAG]] nomination for [[Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture|Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture]].

He played FBI Agent Ian Curtis in [[Benny Chan (film director)|Benny Chan]]'s 2000 Hong Kong action film ''[[Gen-Y Cops]]''. In 2002, he was cast as [[List of Friends characters|Mike Hannigan]] on the [[sitcom]] ''[[Friends]]'' who dates and then marries Phoebe Buffay, played by [[Lisa Kudrow]]. In 2006, he appeared in several episodes of ''[[Reno 911!]]'' as "Guy Gerricault" (pronounced "jericho"), the coach of a [[lamaze]] class, and then portrayed a [[drug lord]] in the ''[[Reno 911!: Miami]]'' film. He guest-starred as a hasbeen 1990s rock star, Desmond Fellows, on the television series ''[[Veronica Mars]]'', in the 2007 episode "[[Debasement Tapes]]".

2004 marked the start of his work with director/producer [[Judd Apatow]], first on the film ''[[Anchorman: The Legend of Ron Burgundy]]'' as Brian Fantana with [[Steve Carell]], [[David Koechner]] and [[Will Ferrell]], produced by Apatow and again in 2005 in ''[[The 40-Year-Old Virgin]]'' with Steve Carell and [[Seth Rogen]], directed by Apatow. He subsequently worked with Apatow in 2007's ''[[Knocked Up]]'', as frustrated husband Pete, married to [[Leslie Mann]]'s character.<ref name=HWR1>{{cite web|last=Zakarin|first=Jordan|title=Judd Apatow: I'm 'Disgusted' When Wife Leslie Mann Fools Around Onscreen With Other Men|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/judd-apatow-im-disgusted-wife-409103|publisher=Hollywood Reporter|accessdate=December 20, 2013}}</ref> In that film, he co-starred with [[Jason Segel]], [[Jonah Hill]], [[Seth Rogan]], and [[Jay Baruchel]]. Rudd appeared as [[John Lennon]] in the comedy film ''[[Walk Hard: The Dewey Cox Story]]'' in 2007<ref name=Telegraph1 /> and as the drug-addled surf instructor in [[Nicholas Stoller]]'s ''[[Forgetting Sarah Marshall]]'' in 2008 with [[Jason Segel]] and [[Jonah Hill]], both of which Apatow produced.<ref>{{cite web|last=Bonaime|first=Ross|title=The 10 Best Paul Rudd Film Roles|url=http://www.pastemagazine.com/blogs/lists/2011/08/the-10-best-paul-rudd-film-roles.html|publisher=Paste Magazine|accessdate=December 20, 2013|date=August 29, 2011}}</ref> Rudd appeared as an uncredited cameos in ''[[Year One (film)|Year One]]'' (2009) and ''[[Bridesmaids (2011 film)|Bridesmaids]]'' (2011). In 2012, he starred and also co-produced with Apatow on the film ''[[Wanderlust (2012 film)|Wanderlust]]'' with [[Jennifer Aniston]].<ref name=Telegraph1 /> He starred in the comedy film ''[[This Is 40]]'' with Leslie Mann,<ref name=HWR1 /> a spinoff from ''Knocked Up'', which was directed and produced by Apatow. He reprised his role as Brian Fantana in the 2013 sequel ''[[Anchorman 2: The Legend Continues]]''.

In 2007, he starred in both ''[[The Oh in Ohio]]'' and ''[[The Ten]]'', which reunited him with [[David Wain]] and [[Michael Showalter]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Lee|first=Nathan|title=Movie Review The Oh in Ohio (2006)|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2006/07/14/movies/14ohio.html?_r=0|publisher=NY Times|accessdate=December 20, 2013|date=July 14, 2006}}</ref> And then in ''[[Over Her Dead Body]]'' with [[Eva Longoria]] the next year. His next comedy which he also wrote, ''[[Role Models]]'', he and co-star [[Seann William Scott]] portray energy-drink salesmen forced to perform community service in a child mentoring program.<ref>{{cite web|last=Neumaier|first=Joe|title=Paul Rudd and Seann William Scott are no 'Role Models'|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/tv-movies/paul-rudd-seann-william-scott-role-models-article-1.334232|publisher=NY Daily News|accessdate=December 20, 2013|date=November 6, 2008}}</ref>

[[File:SegelJonesRuddILoveYouManMarch09.jpg|thumb|right|Rudd (''right'') with ''I Love You, Man'' co-stars [[Jason Segel]] and [[Rashida Jones]] at the premiere in March 2009]]

In 2009, Rudd again appeared with Jason Segel in ''[[I Love You Man]]'' and co-created the TV series ''[[Party Down]]'' with John Embom, ''[[Rob Thomas (writer)|Rob Thomas]]'' and Dan Etheridge.<ref name=Telegraph1 /> He lent his voice to the [[DreamWorks Animation|DreamWorks]] computer-animated hit movie ''[[Monsters Vs. Aliens]]''. In 2010, he reunited with [[Steve Carell]] for the first time since ''The 40-Year-Old Virgin'' for the [[Jay Roach]]-directed comedy ''[[Dinner for Schmucks]]''. In 2012, he had a supporting role in the teen drama ''[[The Perks of Being a Wallflower (film)|The Perks of Being a Wallflower]]'', playing Mr. Anderson, a teacher of Charlie, played by [[Logan Lerman]].

He starred in the 2011 [[comedy-drama]] film ''[[Our Idiot Brother]]'' with [[Elizabeth Banks]], [[Zooey Deschanel]], and [[Emily Mortimer]].<ref name=IdiotBro>{{cite web|title= 'Our Idiot Brother' premiere: Paul Rudd hits Hollywood with his 'sisters'|url=http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/gossip/2011/08/our-idiot-brother-paul-rudd-zooey-deschanel-rashida-jones.html|publisher=LA Times|accessdate=December 20, 2013|date=August 17, 2011}}</ref> It would be the fifth film that Rudd starred in with Elizabeth Banks. He had previously appeared with her in ''[[Wet Hot American Summer]]'' (2001), ''[[The Baxter]]'' (2005), ''The 40-Year-Old Virgin'' (2005) and ''Role Models'' (2008).<ref name=EW1>{{cite web|last=Collis|first=Clark|title=Elizabeth Banks talks stuffing her bra and slobbering over Paul Rudd for 'Wet Hot American Summer'|url=http://insidemovies.ew.com/2011/07/16/elizabeth-banks-wet-hot-american-summer/|publisher=Entertainment Weekly|accessdate=December 20, 2013|date=July 16, 2011}}</ref>

In 2012, he signed to appear on four episodes of NBC's ''[[Parks and Recreation]]'' as Bobby Newport, a candidate for City Council and a rival of [[Amy Poehler]]'s character Leslie Knope. A role which he won the [[Critics' Choice Television Award]] for Best Guest Performer in a Comedy Series.

In December 2013, Rudd entered early negotiations to star in the upcoming 2015 [[Marvel Entertainment|Marvel]] film ''[[Ant-Man (film)|Ant-Man]]'' as the lead character Scott Lang.<ref name="Ant-ManCast">{{cite web|title=Paul Rudd Set to Star in Marvel's Ant-Man|url=http://bloody-disgusting.com/images/3326133/yellowjacket-ant-man-movie/|work=|publisher=Marvel Studios|accessdate=December 19, 2013|date=December 19, 2013|archivedate=December 19, 2013|deadurl=no}}</ref><ref name="TheWrap">{{cite web |last=Sneider |first=Jeff |date=December 18, 2013 |url=http://www.thewrap.com/paul-rudd-early-talks-play-ant-man-edgar-wrights-marvel-movie-exclusive/ |title=Paul Rudd to Play Ant-Man in Edgar Wright’s Marvel Movie (Exclusive) |publisher=The Wrap |accessdate=December 18, 2013 |archiveurl=http://archive.is/LGNhn |archivedate=December 18, 2013 |deadurl=no}}</ref><ref name="VarietyDec2013">{{cite web |last=Kroll |first=Justin |date=December 18, 2013 |url=http://variety.com/2013/film/news/paul-rudd-marvel-ant-man-1200756440/ |title=Paul Rudd to Star in Marvel’s ‘Ant-Man’ |publisher=Variety |accessdate=December 18, 2013 |archiveurl=http://archive.is/NL7iL |archivedate=December 18, 2013 |deadurl=no}}</ref> Marvel officially confirmed Rudd's casting on December 19, 2013.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.comingsoon.net/news/movienews.php?id=112721 |title=It's Official: Marvel Studios Confirms Paul Rudd Will Play Ant-Man  |publisher=comingsoon.net |date=December 19, 2013 |accessdate=December 19, 2013}}</ref><ref name="RuddCast">{{cite web|title=Paul Rudd Set to Star in Marvel's Ant-Man|url=http://marvel.com/news/movies/2013/12/19/21669/paul_rudd_set_to_star_in_marvels_ant-man|work=Marvel Entertainment|publisher=Marvel Studios|accessdate=December 19, 2013|date=December 19, 2013|archiveurl=http://archive.is/f1JvV|archivedate=December 19, 2013|deadurl=no}}</ref> Rudd also co-wrote the screenplay with [[Adam McKay]]. In May 2015, Marvel Studios confirmed Rudd will reprise his role in ''[[Captain America: Civil War]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://marvel.com/news/movies/24586/marvel_studios_begins_production_on_marvels_captain_america_civil_war |title=Marvel Studios Begins Production on Marvel's 'Captain America: Civil War' |publisher=Marvel.com |date=2015-05-07 |accessdate=2015-05-07}}</ref>

Rudd often stars with other Apatow film regulars like [[Seth Rogen]] (four films), [[Steve Carell]] (four films), [[Jonah Hill]] (three films), [[Leslie Mann]] (three films), [[Kristen Wiig]] (three films), [[Jason Segel]] (three films), [[Elizabeth Banks]] (five films), and [[Joe Lo Truglio]] (seven films), as well as working with director [[David Wain]] (four films).

In 2014 he began providing voiceovers for Hyundai television commercials. He has also voiced the [[audiobook]] recordings of [[John Hodgman]]'s books ''[[The Areas of My Expertise]]'' (2005) and ''[[More Information Than You Require]]'' (2008).<ref>{{cite web|title=The Areas of My Expertise|url=http://www.powells.com/biblio/9780143059097|publisher=Powells.com|accessdate=December 19, 2013|year=2008}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Kuntzman|first=Gersh|title=John Hodgman is big — bigger than Paul Rudd, even|url=http://www.brooklynpaper.com/stories/34/43/24_hodgmania_2011_10_28_bk.html|publisher=BrooklynPaper.com|accessdate=December 19, 2013|date=October 25, 2011}}</ref>

===Theatre===
Rudd has also appeared in [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] plays, the first being ''[[The Last Night of Ballyhoo]]'' as Joe Farkas in 1997.<ref>{{cite web|last=Lefkowitz|first=David|title=How Ballyhoo's Paul Rudd Gets His Exercise|url=http://www.playbill.com/features/article/64568-How-Ballyhoos-Paul-Rudd-Gets-His-Exercise|publisher=Playbill.com|accessdate=December 19, 2013|date=September 1, 1997}}</ref> The next year he appeared in ''[[Twelfth Night]]'' with [[Kyra Sedgwick]] and [[Max Wright]] at the [[Lincoln Center Theatre]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Lincoln Center Theatre : Twelfth Night|url=http://www.lct.org/showMain.htm?id=129|publisher=Lincoln Center Theatre|accessdate=December 19, 2013|year=1998}}</ref> And in 2006, he was in the Broadway production of Richard Greenberg's ''[[Three Days of Rain]]'' with [[Bradley Cooper]] and [[Julia Roberts]] at the Bernard Jacobs Theater.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bradley Cooper & Paul Rudd Join Julia Roberts in B'way's Three Days of Rain|url=http://www.broadway.com/buzz/95025/bradley-cooper-paul-rudd-join-julia-roberts-in-bways-three-days-of-rain/|publisher=Broadway.com|accessdate=December 19, 2013|date=November 28, 2005}}</ref>
