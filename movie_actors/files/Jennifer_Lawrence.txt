'''Jennifer Shrader Lawrence''' (born August 15, 1990) is an American actress. Her first major role was as a lead cast member on the [[TBS (TV channel)|TBS]] sitcom ''[[The Bill Engvall Show]]'' (2007–09). She appeared in the independent dramas ''[[The Burning Plain]]'' (2008) and ''[[Winter's Bone]]'' (2010), for which she received an [[Academy Award for Best Actress]] nomination. Her first commercial success came with the superhero film ''[[X-Men: First Class]]'' (2011).

Lawrence gained international fame for playing heroine [[Katniss Everdeen]] in the [[The Hunger Games (film series)|''Hunger Games'' film series]] (2012–15), which established her as the highest-grossing action heroine {{as of|2015|lc=y}}.<ref name="Highest-grossing action heroine"/> She starred in [[David O. Russell]]'s romantic comedy ''[[Silver Linings Playbook]]'' (2012), for which she won a [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Motion Picture Musical or Comedy|Golden Globe Award]], a [[Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Female Actor in a Leading Role|Screen Actors Guild Award]], and the Academy Award for Best Actress, becoming the second-youngest Best Actress Oscar winner. For her supporting role in Russell's comedy-drama ''[[American Hustle]]'' (2013), she won a [[BAFTA Award for Best Actress in a Supporting Role|BAFTA Award]] and a [[Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actress – Motion Picture|Golden Globe Award]], and received a nomination for the [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress]].

Lawrence's lead role in [[Debra Granik]]'s ''[[Winter's Bone]]'' (2010), which won Best Picture at the [[Sundance Film Festival]], is often cited as her breakout performance.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.blackbookmag.com/jennifer-lawrence-dishes-on-winters-bone-and-stripping-for-esquire|title=Jennifer Lawrence dishes on 'Winter's Bone' and stripping for 'Esquire'|first=Jeremy|last=Medina|work=[[BlackBook (magazine)|BlackBook]]|date=June 28, 2010|accessdate=June 4, 2011}}</ref> She portrays a 17-year-old in the [[Ozark Mountains]] who cares for her mentally ill mother and younger siblings while searching for her missing father.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/two-join-house-at-end-24282|title=Two join 'House at the End of the Street'|first=Borys|last=Kit|work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|date=October 14, 2010|accessdate=February 4, 2015}}</ref> Her performance was highly acclaimed by film critics. [[David Denby (film critic)|David Denby]] of ''[[The New Yorker]]'' said the film "would be unimaginable with anyone less charismatic playing Ree."<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Denby|first1=David|authorlink=David Denby (film critic)|date=July 5, 2010|title=Current Cinema: Thrills and Chills|journal=[[The New Yorker]]|publisher=[[Condé Nast]]|pages=78–79|url=http://www.newyorker.com/arts/critics/cinema/2010/07/05/100705crci_cinema_denby|accessdate=May 12, 2011}}</ref> [[Peter Travers]] from ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' opined that "her performance is more than acting, it's a gathering storm. Lawrence's eyes are a roadmap to what's tearing Ree apart."<ref>{{cite web|title=Winter's Bone Review|url=http://www.rollingstone.com/movies/reviews/winters-bone-20100603|date=June 3, 2010|last=Travers|first=Peter|authorlink=Peter Travers|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|accessdate=April 14, 2012}}</ref> Lawrence was awarded the [[National Board of Review of Motion Pictures|National Board of Review Award for Best Breakthrough Performance]] and received a nomination for the [[Academy Award for Best Actress]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nytimes.com/movies/movie/456874/Winter-s-Bone/awards|title=Winter's Bone awards|work=[[The New York Times]]|accessdate=March 29, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1656582/2011-oscar-nominations.jhtml|title=Oscar Nominations List 2011|publisher=[[MTV]]|date=January 25, 2010|accessdate=June 4, 2011}}</ref>

In 2011, Lawrence co-starred in the independent film ''[[Like Crazy]]'', which premiered at the [[27th Sundance Film Festival]],<ref>{{cite work|last=Zeitchik|first=Steven|url=http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/movies/2011/01/like-crazy-sundance-anton-yelchin-jones-jennifer-lawrence.html|title=Sundance 2011: 'Like Crazy' is bought, and will be released by, Paramount Pictures|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|date=January 23, 2011|accessdate=February 1, 2011}}</ref> and she appeared in ''[[The Beaver (film)|The Beaver]]'', a dark comedy starring [[Jodie Foster]] and [[Mel Gibson]]. ==Achievements==
{{see also|List of awards and nominations received by Jennifer Lawrence}}

Lawrence won an [[Academy Award for Best Actress]] for her performance in ''[[Silver Linings Playbook]]'' (2012). She won two [[Golden Globe Award]]s, [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actress in a Motion Picture – Comedy or Musical|Best Actress – Musical or Comedy]] for ''Silver Linings Playbook'' and [[Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actress – Motion Picture|Best Supporting Actress]] for ''[[American Hustle]]'' (2013). She has won a [[BAFTA Award]] for [[BAFTA Award for Best Actress in a Supporting Role|Best Actress in a Supporting Role]] for ''American Hustle''.<ref name="BAFTA"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.oscars.org/awards/academyawards/85/nominees.html|title=Winners and Nominees for the 85th Academy Awards|publisher=[[Academy of Motion Pictures Arts and Sciences]]|accessdate=January 26, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hfpa.org/golden-globes-2013/|title=2013 Golden Globe Awards|publisher=[[Hollywood Foreign Press Association]]|accessdate=January 26, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goldenglobes.com/awards|title=2014 Golden Globe Awards|publisher=[[Hollywood Foreign Press Association]]|accessdate=January 26, 2014}}</ref>

She has also received numerous awards from other organizations, including the [[Independent Spirit Award for Best Female Lead]], the [[Los Angeles Film Critics Association Award for Best Actress]] and the [[Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Female Actor in a Leading Role]] for ''Silver Linings Playbook'', the [[National Society of Film Critics Award for Best Supporting Actress]], the [[New York Film Critics Circle Award for Best Supporting Actress]] and the [[Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture]] for ''American Hustle''. She received four [[Critics' Choice Movie Award]]s for her work in ''Silver Linings Playbook'', ''[[The Hunger Games]]'', and ''American Hustle''.<ref name="Crit2013">{{cite web|url=http://www.criticschoice.com/movie-awards/18th-annual-critics-choice-movie-awards-2013-best-picture-argo/|title=18th Annual Critics' Choice Movie Awards (2013)|publisher=[[Broadcast Film Critics Association]]|accessdate=January 26, 2014}}</ref><ref name="Crit2014">{{cite web|url=http://www.criticschoice.com/movie-awards/19th-annual-critics%E2%80%99-choice-movie-awards-2014-%E2%80%93-best-picture-12-years-a-slave/|title=19th Annual Critics' Choice Movie Awards (2014)|publisher=[[Broadcast Film Critics Association]]|accessdate=January 26, 2014}}</ref><ref name="Sag2013">{{cite web|url=http://www.sagawards.org/awards/nominees-and-recipients/19th-annual-screen-actors-guild-awards|title=The 19th Annual Screen Actors Guild Awards|publisher=[[SAG-AFTRA]]|accessdate=January 26, 2014}}</ref><ref name="SAG2014">{{cite web|url=http://www.sagawards.org/awards/nominees-and-recipients/20th-annual-screen-actors-guild-awards|title=The 20th Annual Screen Actors Guild Awards|publisher=[[SAG-AFTRA]]|accessdate=January 26, 2014}}</ref>

Lawrence was recognized as the highest-grossing action heroine in the 2015 edition of the ''[[Guinness World Records]]'' for the role of Katniss Everdeen in the ''Hunger Games'' franchise.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://time.com/3268300/jennifer-lawrence-guinness-world-record/|title=Jennifer Lawrence Is the Highest-Grossing Action Heroine|last=Alter|first=Charlotte|work=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]|date=September 4, 2014|accessdate=September 5, 2014}}</ref>
