'''Evelyn Francisco''' (born August 13, 1904, [[Little Rock, Arkansas]] &ndash; died January 27, 1963, [[Corona, California]]) was a [[silent era]] film actress who began as a bathing beauty. Her sister, [[Betty Francisco]], was also a movie star.<ref name=betty>''News Of The Cafes'', [[Los Angeles Times]], August 19, 1925, pg. A6.</ref>

In ''The Goof'' (1924), directed by [[William Beaudine]], she is one of a number of beauties called Spike Malone's diving girls. The seven-reel farce featured former [[William Fox (producer)|William Fox]] actress, [[Alta Allen]], as the head of the troupe. Spike was played by ''Chuck'' Relaner.<ref>''Bathing Beauties Features Of Goof'', Los Angeles Times, April 6, 1924, pg. B14.</ref> In the 1924 ''Hollywood Follies'' Francisco was one of the [[Mack Sennett]]'s [[Sennett Bathing Beauties|bathing girls]] who performed at the Philharmonic Auditorium. Led by [[Harry Langdon]], [[Thelma Parr]] was another of the select group of Sennett females who presented an act called ''All Wet''.<ref>''Hollywood Follies Glistens With Novelty'', Los Angeles Times, October 26, 1924, pg. C19.</ref> In August 1925, the Francisco sisters were presented together at the Greenwich Village Cafe in the Christie Hotel, in Hollywood. At the time Francisco had just completed an important role in the first production of [[Julian Eltinge]] for the [[Christie Studios]],<ref name=betty/> a film entitled ''Madame Behave'' (1925).

She wore a novelty bathing suit of Russian [[ermine]] for a fashion pageant held at the Hotel Vista del Arroyo in [[Pasadena, California]], in February 1927. The garment was designed and made by Colburn's of South Flower Street in [[Los Angeles, California]].<ref>''Fashions'',
Los Angeles Times, February 27, 1927, pg. H5.</ref>

Francisco's husband, Samuel Bernheim, died at 623 North Alta Drive in [[Beverly Hills, California]], on May 16, 1934.<ref>''Deaths, Los Angeles Times, May 19, 1934, pg. 12.</ref>
