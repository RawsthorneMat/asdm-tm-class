'''David Rodman''' "'''Dave'''" '''Annable''' (born September 15, 1979) is an American actor. He played the character of [[Justin Walker (Brothers & Sisters)|Justin Walker]] on the television series ''[[Brothers & Sisters (2006 TV series)|Brothers & Sisters]]'' from 2006 to 2011. He also portrayed Henry Martin on the [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] drama ''[[666 Park Avenue]]''. Annable co-starred as Dr. Adam McAndrew on the [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]] series ''[[Red Band Society]]''.

==Early life==
Annable was born in [[Suffern, New York]]. He grew up in [[Walden, New York|Walden]], a village in New York, where he played baseball, rugby and hockey. His father is Christian and his mother is Jewish, and he was "raised both".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.interfaithfamily.com/arts_and_entertainment/popular_culture/Hollywood_Now_New_Fall_Previews.shtml|title=Hollywood Now: New Fall Previews|publisher=Interfaithfamily.com|accessdate=2 October 2014}}</ref>

Annable attended Valley Central High School and graduated in 1997. He attended college at the [[State University of New York at Plattsburgh]] (SUNY Plattsburgh). There, he became involved with the student-run TV station, [[Plattsburgh State Television]] (PSTV), working both on production behind the scenes and on the air as well. He hosted many shows at PSTV, including ''Late Night with Dave Annable'', ''Cardinal Sports'', and ''The Roommate Game''. Annable credits Plattsburgh State Television for preparing him for his successful career in television.  Annable dropped out of college in 2003 to pursue his acting career.<ref name="Cardinal_Points">{{cite news | last = Bergeron | first = Bryan | title = Annable, 'off to a good start' | publisher = Cardinal Points | date = May 1, 2009 | url = http://www.cardinalpointsonline.com/fuse/annable-off-to-a-good-start-1.1741249 | accessdate = 2009-05-17 }}</ref>

After leaving SUNY Plattsburgh, Annable studied acting at the [[Neighborhood Playhouse]] in New York with Richard Pinter.

==Career==
In 2002, Annable made his acting debut in an episode of the [[NBC]] crime drama ''[[Third Watch]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tvsa.co.za/actorprofile.asp?actorID=5546|title=Dave Annable|publisher=Tvsa.co.za|accessdate=2 October 2014}}</ref> Annable has appeared in numerous commercials for brands such as [[Mountain Dew]], [[Starburst (confectionery)|Starburst]] and [[Abercrombie and Fitch]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm1616349/otherworks|title=Dave Annable - Other works|work=IMDb|accessdate=2 October 2014}}</ref> In 2004 Annable appeared in the films ''[[Little Black Book]]'' and ''Spellbound''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm1616349/|title=Dave Annable|work=IMDb|accessdate=2 October 2014}}</ref> In 2007 Annable was ranked as #7 on ''[[People (magazine)|People]]'' magazines annual list of Sexiest Men Alive.<ref>{{cite journal | title = The Sexiest Men Alive | journal = [[People (magazine)|People]] |date = November 26, 2007 | url = http://www.people.com/people/package/gallery/0,,20154290_20159879_7,00.html }}</ref>

In September 2005 Annable appeared in the [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]] drama series ''[[Reunion (TV series)|Reunion]]''. The series centers on six friends who attend their 20th high school reunion, where one of them is murdered and all of them are suspects.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0460672/|title=Reunion (TV Series 2005– )|work=IMDb|accessdate=2 October 2014}}</ref> Annable portrayed the role of Aaron Lewis, a mastermind behind a multi-million-dollar company. In December 2005 FOX cancelled the series due to low ratings shortly after the ninth episode aired.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tvseriesfinale.com/tv-show/reunion-killers-identity-revealed-for-cancelled-fox-show/|title=Reunion: Killer’s Identity Revealed for Cancelled Fox Show|publisher=Tvseriesfinale.com|accessdate=2 October 2014}}</ref> [[The WB]] reportedly showed interest in picking up the series on their network but these plans never materialized.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tv.com/shows/reunion/forums/wb-picks-up-reunion-14448-176235/|title=Reunion|work=TV.com|accessdate=2 October 2014}}</ref>

In 2006, Annable appeared in [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] drama series ''[[Brothers & Sisters (2006 TV series)|Brothers & Sisters]]'' alongside [[Calista Flockhart]], [[Rachel Griffiths]], and [[Sally Field]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2006/09/03/arts/television/03wyat.html?pagewanted=all|title=‘Brothers & Sisters’ to Make Its Debut on ABC|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|accessdate=2 October 2014}}</ref> In May of the same year ABC picked the series up for a 23-episode first season.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://au.tv.ign.com/articles/708/708855p1.html|title=Upfronts Announcement: NBC's Schedule for 2006-2007 Season|work=IGN|accessdate=2 October 2014}}</ref> The series follows the daily lives of the Walker family in [[Pasadena, California]]. Annable portrayed the role of [[Justin Walker (Brothers & Sisters)|Justin Walker]], a paramedic, war veteran and former med school student. The series fifth season premiered in September 2010 to 9.47 million viewers.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tvbythenumbers.zap2it.com/2010/09/28/sunday-finals-amazing-race-premieres-up-undercover-boss-a-bit-less-bossy-vs-football/65590/|title=Sunday Finals: Amazing Race Premieres Up; Undercover Boss A Bit Less Bossy vs. Football|publisher=Tvbythenumbers.zap2it.com|accessdate=2 October 2014}}</ref> In May 2011, ABC did not renew the series for a sixth season.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tvline.com/2011/05/13/breaking-abc-cancels-brothers-sisters-and-v/|title=Breaking: ABC Cancels Brothers & Sisters and V – TVLine - TVLine|work=TVLine|accessdate=2 October 2014}}</ref> In 2008, Annable's role on the show earned him a [[Prism Award]] for Best Performance in a Drama Series Multi-Episode Storyline.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm1616349/awards|title=Dave Annable|work=IMDb|accessdate=2 October 2014}}</ref>

Five years after dropping out of college, Annable earned his diploma from SUNY Plattsburgh on May 16, 2009. In addition to earning his degree, Annable was awarded the "Off To A Good Start" award and gave a commencement address to his fellow Class of 2009 graduates.  Completing college was a promise he made to his mother.<ref name="Cardinal_Points"/><ref>{{cite web | title = Dave Annable accepts the Off to a Good Start Award from SUNY Plattsburgh | publisher = YouTube | url = http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JucFW_6Q8o&fmt=18 | format = video | accessdate = 2009-05-16}}</ref>

In January 2012, Annable was confirmed to star as the protagonist in the ABC supernatural drama series ''[[666 Park Avenue]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1118048970?refCatId=14|title=Advertisement|work=Variety|accessdate=2 October 2014}}</ref> In May 2012 the series was picked up.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tvbythenumbers.zap2it.com/2012/05/11/abc-picks-up-comedies-the-neighbors-and-comeback-jack-and-dramas-nashville-red-window-and-zero-hour/133721/|title=Updated (5): ABC Picks Up Comedies ‘The Neighbors’ and ‘The Family Tools’ and Dramas ‘Nashville,’ ‘Red Widow’ and ‘Zero Hour’ + More|publisher=Tvbythenumbers.zap2it.com|accessdate=2 October 2014}}</ref> The series follows a couple, who learns that the Manhattan building complex that they just moved into, including its upscale tenants, might be possessed by a mysterious demonic force. [[Rachael Taylor]], [[Vanessa L. Williams|Vanessa Williams]] and [[Robert Buckley]] starred.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/comic-con-2012-666-park-avenue-349077|title=Comic-Con 2012: '666 Park Avenue' Keeps the Mystery Going, Crowd Celebrates Terry O'Quinn's Birthday|date=13 July 2012|work=The Hollywood Reporter|accessdate=2 October 2014}}</ref> The series aired on Sundays at 10:00 pm Eastern/9:00 pm Central after ''[[Revenge (TV series)|Revenge]]'', premiering September 30, 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tvbythenumbers.zap2it.com/2012/05/15/abc-2012-13-primetime-schedule-revenge-to-sunday-happy-endings-dont-trust-the-b-to-tuesday-last-man-standing-to-friday/134231/|title=ABC 2012-13 Primetime Schedule: 'Revenge' To Sunday, 'Happy Endings,' 'Don't Trust The B' To Tuesday, 'Last Man Standing' To Friday|publisher=Tvbythenumbers.zap2it.com|accessdate=2012-06-22}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://blog.zap2it.com/frominsidethebox/2012/07/abc-fall-2012-premiere-dates-revenge-and-modern-family-plus-suburgatory-gets-a-new-timeslot.html|title=ABC fall 2012 premiere dates: 'Revenge' and 'Modern Family,' plus 'Suburgatory' gets a new timeslot|last=Porter|first=Rick|date=July 26, 2012|work=[[Zap2it]]|accessdate=July 26, 2012}}</ref> On November 16, 2012, ABC announced that the series had been canceled after its initial thirteen-episode run.

==Personal life==
Annable married actress [[Odette Yustman|Odette Annable]] (née Yustman) on October 10, 2010.
