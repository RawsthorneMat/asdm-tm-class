'''Merry Anders''' (May 22, 1932 – October 28, 2012) was an American stage, film and television actress and model who appeared in a number of television programs and films from the 1950s until her retirement from the screen in 1972. 

==Early life==
Anders was born '''Mary Helen Anderson''' in [[Chicago]], [[Illinois]]. She was the only child of Charles, a contractor, and Helen Anderson. Anders was of German, Irish and Swedish descent. In 1949, Anders and her mother visited [[Los Angeles]] for two weeks. They decided to remain in Los Angeles permanently while Charles Anderson remained in Chicago.<ref name="woodbine">{{cite web|url=http://www.glamourgirlsofthesilverscreen.com/show/12/merry+anders/register.php|title=Merry Anders|last=Woodbine|first=Paul|publisher=glamourgirlsofthesilverscreen.com|accessdate=March 5, 2015}}</ref> While she was a student at [[John Burroughs Middle School (Los Angeles)|John Burroughs Middle School]], Anders met former actress Rita LeRoy who encouraged her to begin a modeling career. While working as a junior model, Anders began studying acting at the Ben Bard Playhouse. It was there that a talent scout from [[20th Century Fox]] spotted her and signed her to a film contract in 1951.<ref>{{cite book|last=Weaver|first=Tom|title=Double Feature Creature Attack: A Monster Merger of Two More Volumes of Classic Interviews|year=2003|publisher=McFarland|isbn=0-786-48215-X|page=2}}</ref>

==Career==
Anders made her film debut in the 1951 musical ''[[Golden Girl (1951 film)|Golden Girl]]''. For the next two years, she appeared in small and supporting roles in several 20th Century Fox films. In 1954, Fox dropped her. Later that year, Anders joined the cast of ''[[The Stu Erwin Show]]''. She remained with the series until its cancellation in 1955. Anders was then cast in the [[CBS]] sitcom ''[[It's Always Jan]]'', starring [[Janis Paige]].<ref name="koper27">{{cite book|last=Koper|first=Richard |title=Fifties Blondes: Sexbombs, Sirens, Bad Girls and Teen Queens|year=2010|publisher=BearManor Media|isbn=1-593-93521-8|page=27}}</ref> That series canceled after one season.

Shortly after the birth of her daughter in 1956, Anders took over the role of "Rita Malone" (popularized by [[Jayne Mansfield]]) in the West Coast touring production of the Broadway hit ''[[Will Success Spoil Rock Hunter? (play)|Will Success Spoil Rock Hunter?]]''. In 1957, Anders had a much publicized leading role in [[Paramount Pictures]] film ''Hear Me Good'', opposite [[Hal March]].<ref name="koper27"/> In 1957, Anders landed the role of "Mike McCall" in the syndicated sitcom ''[[How to Marry a Millionaire (TV series)|How to Marry a Millionaire]]''. The series was based on the hit 1953 film [[How to Marry a Millionaire|of the same name]] (in which Anders appeared in a bit role)<ref>{{cite book|last1=Brooks|first1=Tim|last2=Marsh|first2=Earle F.|title=The Complete Directory to Prime Time Network and Cable TV Shows, 1946-Present|year=2009|publisher=Random House Publishing Group|isbn=0-307-48320-7|page=643}}</ref> starring [[Betty Grable]], [[Marilyn Monroe]], and [[Lauren Bacall]]. Anders co-starred in the series with [[Barbara Eden]] and [[Lori Nelson]]. The first season of ''How to Marry a Millionaire'' was fairly successful and it was renewed for a second, abbreviated season. It was cancelled in 1959.<ref>{{cite book|last=Tucker|first=David C.|title=Lost Laughs of '50S and '60S Television: Thirty Sitcoms That Faded Off Screen|year=2010|publisher=McFarland|isbn=0-786-45582-9|pages=64–66}}</ref>

During the early to mid 1960s, Anders continued her career with mainly supporting film roles and guest spots on television. In 1960, she appeared in the horror film ''[[The Hypnotic Eye]]'' followed by a role in the Western ''Young Jesse James''. Anders went on to land guest roles on ''[[Surfside 6]]'', ''[[Alfred Hitchcock Presents]]'', ''[[Hawaiian Eye]]'', ''[[Death Valley Days]]'', ''[[77 Sunset Strip]]'', and ''[[Perry Mason (TV series)|Perry Mason]]''. In 1962, Anders was cast in the [[Beauty and the Beast (1962 film)|first live action film adaptation]] of the 18th century French [[fairy tale]] ''[[Beauty and the Beast]]''.<ref>{{cite news|title=Andrew Stone Will Produce War Tale: Civic to Stage 'The Balcony'; 'Beauty and Beast' Refilmed|last=Scheuer|first=Philip K.|date=May 1, 1961|work=Los Angeles Times|page=C11|location=Los Angeles, California}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|editor=Blum, Daniel|title=Daniel Blum's Screen World 1963|year=1963|publisher=Biblo & Tannen Publishers|isbn=0-819-60304-X|page=129}}</ref> In 1965, she appeared opposite [[Elvis Presley]] in the musical comedy ''[[Tickle Me]]''.<ref>{{cite book|last=Lisanti|first=Tom|title=Fantasy Femmes of Sixties Cinema: Interviews with 20 Actresses from Biker, Beach, and Elvis Movies|year=2001|publisher=McFarland|isbn=0-786-40868-5|page=96}}</ref>
The following year, she appeared in the recurring role of "Alice" in the teen soap opera ''[[Never Too Young]]''. Later that year, Anders had a supporting role in the [[B movie]] ''[[Women of the Prehistoric Planet]]''. From 1967-68, she appeared in seven episodes of [[Jack Webb]]'s ''[[Dragnet (series)|Dragnet]]'' series on [[NBC]], in which she played policewoman Dorothy Miller. She appeared in a recurring role on ''[[Lassie (1954 TV series)|Lassie]]''.<ref> {{IMDb name|0026039}}</ref>

===Retirement and later years===
By the late 1960s, Anders' acting career had begun to wane. In 1968, she appeared as uncredited "glorified [[Extra (acting)|extra]]" in the film ''[[Airport (1970 film)|Airport]]'' (1970). To supplement her income between acting jobs, Anders took a job as a receptionist at [[Litton Industries]].<ref name="koper29">{{harv|Koper|2007|p=29}}</ref> Her final acting role was a guest spot in the two part ''[[Gunsmoke]]'' episode "Waste", which aired late September and early October 1971. 

Anders retired from acting in 1972 in order to "live a normal life." She eventually became a customer relations coordinator at Litton Industries, where she remained until her retirement in 1994.<ref name="koper29"/>

==Personal life==
On March 25, 1955, Anders married producer John Stephens. Anders left Stephens after three months and seventeen days due to his physical abuse.<ref name="woodbine"/> Two weeks later, she discovered she was pregnant with the couple's first child.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1817&dat=19570720&id=APQhAAAAIBAJ&sjid=6JkEAAAAIBAJ&pg=7345,2370594|title=Anders Explains Her Divorce|date=July 19, 1957|work=The Tuscaloosa News|page=12|accessdate=March 5, 2015|location=Tuscaloosa, Alabama}}</ref> Their daughter, Tina Beth Paige Anders, was born in March 1956.<ref name="woodbine"/> Anders and Stephens were divorced in June 1956.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1734&dat=19560618&id=cBscAAAAIBAJ&sjid=KVEEAAAAIBAJ&pg=7060,6680254|title=Actress Divorced|date=June 18, 1956|work=The Dispatch|page=3|accessdate=March 5, 2015|location=Lexington, North Carolina}}</ref> In 1986, Anders married engineer Richard Benedict; the couple remained married until his death in 1999.<ref name="woodbine"/>

==Death==
On October 28, 2012, Anders died in [[Encino, California]], aged 80, from undisclosed causes.<ref name="woodbine"/>
