'''Guillermina Jiménez Chabolla''' (born 16 August 1930),<ref name="border crossing">{{cite web|title=Guillermina Jimenez-chabolla, "United States, Border Crossings from Mexico to United States, 1903-1957"|url=https://familysearch.org/pal:/MM9.1.1/XLGT-DZ5|website=[[FamilySearch]]|accessdate=30 May 2013}}</ref> commonly known by her stage name '''Flor Silvestre''' ({{IPA-es|floɾ silˈβestɾe}}), is a Mexican singer and actress whose multifaceted career spans seventy years.<ref name="Flor Silvestre recibe Diosa de Plata especial por su trayectoria">{{cite news|title=Flor Silvestre recibe Diosa de Plata especial por su trayectoria|url=http://www.informador.com.mx/entretenimiento/2013/475518/6/flor-silvestre-recibe-diosa-de-plata-especial-por-su-trayectoria.htm|accessdate=7 August 2014|work=[[El Informador (Mexico)|El Informador]]|date=31 July 2013}}</ref>

Famed for her beauty and sentimental style of singing, she began her career in the 1940s, performing [[ranchera]] music on radio stations.<ref name='Se Inauguró Solemnemente el Teatro "Juárez": El C. Gobernador del Estado pronunció unas palabras – Asistieron artistas de México'>{{cite news|title=Se Inauguró Solemnemente el Teatro "Juárez": El C. Gobernador del Estado pronunció unas palabras – Asistieron artistas de México|accessdate=6 January 2015|work=[[El Informador (Mexico)|El Informador]]|date=22 November 1946}}</ref> In the early 1950s, she signed with [[Columbia Records]] and released her first successful recordings, which include "[[Guadalajara (song)|Guadalajara]]", "Imposible olvidarte", "Pobre corazón", and "Viejo nopal". In 1957, she signed with [[Musart Records]], the label for which she recorded many of her best-selling songs such as "[[Cielo rojo (song)|Cielo rojo]]", "[[Mi destino fue quererte]]", "Renunciación", "Cachito de mi vida", "[[Gaviota traidora]]", "Lágrimas del alma", "Qué bonito amor", and "Desolación".<ref>"[[Cielo rojo]]", "[[Mi destino fue quererte]]", and "Renunciación" were included in three of Flor's greatest hits compilation albums: ''Los éxitos de Flor Silvestre'' (1972), ''[[El disco de oro de Flor Silvestre]]'' (1977), and ''[[15 éxitos (Flor Silvestre album)|15 éxitos]]'' (1989).</ref>

As an actress, she is regarded as one of the folkloric leading ladies of the "[[Golden Age of Mexican cinema|golden age]]" of [[Cinema of Mexico|Mexican cinema]].<ref name="cine confidencial">{{cite web|title=Cine Confidencial: Folklóricas del cine mexicano|url=http://cinemexicano.mty.itesm.mx/libros/confidencial_folcloricas.html|accessdate=20 September 2011}}</ref> Her most prominent performances are featured in ''[[Primero soy mexicano]]'' (1950), ''[[El bolero de Raquel]]'' (1957), ''[[The Soldiers of Pancho Villa|La cucaracha]]'' (1959), and ''[[Ánimas Trujano (film)|Ánimas Trujano]]'' (1962), which was nominated for an [[Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film]].<ref>{{cite web|title=The 34th Academy Awards (1962)|url=http://www.oscars.org/oscars/ceremonies/1962|website=[[Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences]]|accessdate=6 January 2015}}</ref> She also lent her name and likeness to the title character of ''[[La Llanera Vengadora]]'', a [[comic book]] series.<ref name="superhero comic book">{{cite web|title=An International Catalogue of Superheroes|url=http://www.internationalhero.co.uk/l/llanaven.htm|accessdate=30 November 2011}}</ref>

She married twice and has five children. Her second husband was singer and actor [[Antonio Aguilar]], with whom she toured internationally. Her younger sister, [[Enriqueta Jiménez]], is also a singer and actress.

==Early life==
Guillermina Jiménez Chabolla was born on 16 August 1930, in [[Salamanca, Guanajuato|Salamanca]], [[Guanajuato]],<ref name="border crossing" /> the daughter of Jesús Jiménez Cervantes and María de Jesús Chabolla Peña (c. 1907 &ndash; 5 September 1993).<ref name="Maria de Jesus Chabolla Pena Mexico, Distrito Federal, Civil Registration, 1832-2005">{{cite web|title=Maria de Jesus Chabolla Pena Mexico, Distrito Federal, Civil Registration, 1832-2005|url=https://familysearch.org/pal:/MM9.3.1/TH-1951-23158-1717-20?cc=1923424|website=[[FamilySearch]]|accessdate=6 August 2014}}</ref> She is the third of seven children; her siblings are Francisco, Raquel, José Luis, [[Enriqueta Jiménez|Enriqueta]], María de la Luz, and Arturo.<ref name=ranchera>{{cite web|title=Flor Silvestre, estandarte de la música ranchera|url=http://www.oem.com.mx/esto/notas/n1286866.htm|accessdate=9 December 2011}}</ref> Her maternal grandparents were Felipe Chabolla and Inés Peña.<ref name="Maria de Jesus Chabolla Pena Mexico, Distrito Federal, Civil Registration, 1832-2005" />

As a young child, Silvestre showed a passionate interest in singing and acting; she participated in festivals and organized school plays.<ref>Liner notes by Raúl Vieyra for the album ''[[Flor Silvestre, vol. 6]]''.</ref> She completed primary school in Salamanca, and later moved to [[Mexico City]], where she was enrolled in the Escuela Bancaria Comercial Milton.<ref name="Una flor que comenzó cantando">{{cite news|last1=Kühne|first1=Cecilia|title=Una flor que comenzó cantando|url=http://hemeroteca.imagenzac.com.mx/2003/10/23/antonio1.htm|accessdate=21 October 2014|work=Imagen|date=23 October 2003}}</ref>

==Career==
Silvestre made her singing debut at the age of 13 in [[Mexico City]]; she sang the songs "La canción mexicana", "Yo también soy mexicana", and "El herradero" and received an ovation from the audience.<ref name="Una flor que comenzó cantando" /> Shortly after, she was invited to sing on the XFO radio station, where she sang "La soldadera" by popular songwriter Jesús Morales.<ref name="Una flor que comenzó cantando" /> Her first stage names were La Soldadera ("the female soldier"), based on the success of the aforementioned song, and La Amapola ("the poppy"), because an announcer told her that she looked "like a flower", until she was required to choose another name since those were already in use.<ref name="Una flor que comenzó cantando" /> She then chose Flor Silvestre ("wild flower"), the title of a [[Flor silvestre (film)|1943 drama film]] starring [[Dolores del Río]], as her stage name.<ref name="Una flor que comenzó cantando" />

In the late 1940s, Silvestre won a singing contest sponsored by the [[XEW-AM|XEW radio station]] which led to many contracts, including one for her to perform at the important Teatro Colonial, where she caught the attention of showmen who hired her for tours across northern Mexico and [[South America]].<ref name="Una flor que comenzó cantando" /> Upon her return from those tours, she sang at El Patio, where she met producer [[Gregorio Walerstein]], who invited her to participate in films.<ref name="Una flor que comenzó cantando" /> Her musical career in radio started to rise in 1950, as reporter Mónica Fio noticed:

<blockquote>We unreservedly commend the young singer "Flor Silvestre" because her radio career, though quick, is made with effort, perseverance and study. Whenever we hear her programs we confirm that she does not abandon herself to passing and easy successes, but seeks to overcome. This is how one reaches the goal. This is how one creates prestige. This is how one triumphs.<ref name='Microfono: "Flor Silvestre"'>{{cite news|last1=Fio|first1=Mónica|title=Micrófono: "Flor Silvestre"|url=http://h.elsiglodetorreon.com.mx/Default/Scripting/ArticleWin.asp?From=Search&Key=EDT/1950/04/09/19/Ar01900.xml&CollName=EDT_1950_1959&DOCID=719038&PageLabelPrint=19&skin=ElSiglo&AppName=2&sPublication=EDT&sLanguage=English&Content=ALL&selLanguage=&sQuery=flor%2bsilvestre%2belogiamos&rEntityType=&sScopeID=DR&sDateFrom=&sDateTo=&sSorting=Score%252Cdesc&x=32&y=3&ViewMode=GIF&FontSize=l1|accessdate=16 January 2015|work=[[El Siglo de Torreón]]|date=9 April 1950}}</ref></blockquote>

Silvestre made her acting debut alongside [[Joaquín Pardave]], [[Luis Aguilar (actor)|Luis Aguilar]] and fellow film newcomer [[Francisco Avitia]], also a ranchera singer, in the comedy ''[[Primero soy mexicano]]'' (1950), a Walerstein production.<ref name=ranchera /> She worked with Aguilar and [[Francisco Avitia]] again in ''El tigre enmascarado'' (1951). Around the same time, she signed a contract with [[Columbia Records]] (her first record label) and released the successful singles "Llorar amargo" and "Oye morena".<ref>{{cite news|title=Novedades de esta Semana y de más Exito|url=http://h.elsiglodetorreon.com.mx/Default/Scripting/ArticleWin.asp?From=Search&Key=EDT/1951/07/08/14/Ad01408.xml&CollName=EDT_1950_1959&DOCID=184737&PageLabelPrint=14&skin=ElSiglo&AppName=2&sPublication=EDT&sLanguage=English&Content=ALL&selLanguage=&sQuery=flor%2bsilvestre%2bcolombia&rEntityType=&sScopeID=DR&sDateFrom=&sDateTo=&sSorting=Score%252Cdesc&x=34&y=20&ViewMode=GIF&FontSize=l1|accessdate=21 October 2014|work=[[El Siglo de Torreón]]|date=8 July 1951}}</ref> She played the female lead in ''El lobo solitario'', ''La justicia del lobo'', and ''Vuelve el lobo'' (all in 1952). In 1955, she and [[Miguel Aceves Mejía]] starred the radio program ''Su programa Calmex'', sponsored by Calmex Sardines.<ref>{{cite news|title=Advertisement for ''Su programa Calmex''|url=http://h.elsiglodetorreon.com.mx/Default/Scripting/ArticleWin.asp?From=Search&Key=EDT/1955/01/02/7/Ad00710.xml&CollName=EDT_1950_1959&DOCID=460199&PageLabelPrint=7&skin=ElSiglo&AppName=2&sLanguage=English&Content=ALL&selLanguage=&sPublication=EDT&sQuery=su%2bprograma%2bcalmex&sScopeID=DR&sDateFrom=02%252F26%252F1922&sDateTo=12%252F31%252F2000&x=29&y=14&ViewMode=HTML&FontSize=l1|accessdate=21 October 2014|work=[[El Siglo de Torreón]]|date=2 January 1955}}</ref>

[[File:Flor Silvestre in La cucaracha.jpg|thumb|Flor Silvestre and [[María Félix]] in ''[[The Soldiers of Pancho Villa|La cucaracha]]'' (1959)]]
Silvestre's first color film was [[Miguel M. Delgado]]'s ''La doncella de piedra'' (1956). Her first film co-starring [[Antonio Aguilar]], her future husband, was ''La huella del chacal'' (1956). She traveled to [[Nicaragua]] for ''Rapto al sol'' (1956), another color film. On Mother's Day 1957, she participated with great success in the television play ''Secreto de familia''.<ref name="El Cine en México: Flor Silvestre Artista de Cine y TV.">{{cite news|title=El Cine en México: Flor Silvestre Artista de Cine y TV.|url=http://h.elsiglodetorreon.com.mx/Default/Scripting/ArticleWin.asp?From=Search&Key=EDT/1957/06/09/21/Ar02102.xml&CollName=EDT_1950_1959&DOCID=643642&PageLabelPrint=21&skin=ElSiglo&AppName=2&sPublication=EDT&sLanguage=English&Content=ALL&selLanguage=&sQuery=flor%2bsilvestre%2bteleteatro&x=0&y=0&rEntityType=&sScopeID=DR&sDateFrom=&sDateTo=&sSorting=Score%252Cdesc&ViewMode=HTML&FontSize=l1|accessdate=21 October 2014|work=[[El Siglo de Torreón]]|date=9 June 1957}}</ref> One of her famous roles was as Leonor, the mother of [[Cantinflas]]' godson, in the popular [[Eastmancolor]] comedy ''[[El bolero de Raquel]]'' (1957). She received [[Billing (filmmaking)|top billing]] for the first time in ''Pueblo en armas'' (1959) and its sequel ''¡Viva la soldadera!'' (1960), both directed by Miguel Contreras Torres. She had a supporting role opposite [[María Félix]] in [[Ismael Rodríguez]]'s [[Mexican Revolution]] epic ''[[The Soldiers of Pancho Villa|La cucaracha]]'' (1959); she also sang "Te he de querer", "La chancla", and "La Valentina" for the film's soundtrack album, ''La cucaracha: Música de la película'', released by [[Musart Records]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Liner notes for the album ''La cucaracha: Música de la pelicula''.|url=http://i.ebayimg.com/00/s/MTIwMFgxNjAw/z/WHUAAOxyHWJSHU0a/$T2eC16J,!yYFIcLP4RYBBSHU0Zn-W!~~60_57.JPG|accessdate=9 January 2015}}</ref>

In the early 1960s, she starred with popular comedy duo [[Viruta and Capulina]] in ''Dos locos en escena'' (1960). Silvestre was cast along with [[Toshiro Mifune]] and [[Columba Domínguez]] in the award-winning film ''[[Ánimas Trujano (film)|Ánimas Trujano]]'' (1962), another film directed by [[Ismael Rodríguez]]. The film was nominated for an [[Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film]] and contains her best known role among American and international audiences. In 1964, she released the album ''[[La sentimental]]'', which included both [[ranchera]] and [[bolero]] songs; "[[Mi destino fue quererte]]", one of the album's ranchera tracks, became one of Silvestre's best-selling singles. In this decade, she would avert her roles from low-budget westerns and comedies to star in [[Mexican Revolution]] drama films starring her husband [[Antonio Aguilar]], such as ''[[Caballo prieto azabache]]'' (1968), ''[[Lauro Puñales]]'' (1969), and ''[[El ojo de vidrio]]'' (1969).

By the 1970s, Silvestre had created a symbolic persona of a folkloric woman-hero, therefore she became the star of a [[comic book]] entitled ''[[La Llanera Vengadora]]'',<ref name="superhero comic book" /> which translates as "the avenging plainswoman," that used her likeness and name. The magazine featured a female hero who sought justice, and was dressed in brightly colored cowboy garb.<ref name="superhero comic book" />

She made a [[cameo appearance]] as the singer Rafaela in ''[[Valente Quintero]]'' (1973), in which she sang "No me lo tomes a mal", and played the female lead in ''La yegua colorada'' (1973). Her next two films were historical dramas: she played [[Pancho Villa]]'s lover in ''La muerte de Pancho Villa'' (1974) and the wife of [[Felipe Carrillo Puerto]] in ''Peregrina'' (1974). She sang "La palma" in ''Simón Blanco'' (1975) and played the female leads in ''Don Herculano enamorado'' (1975), ''El moro de cumpas'' (1977), and ''Mi caballo el cantador'' (1979). Her last [[soldadera]] role was featured in ''Benjamín Argumedo el rebelde'' (1979) and ''Persecución y muerte de Benjamín Argumedo'' (1980). Her last film was ''Triste recuerdo'' (1991).

On 21 December 2010, she released ''Soledad'', an album dedicated to her late husband.<ref>{{cite web|title=FLOR SILVESTRE: SOLEDAD|url=https://play.google.com/store/music/album/FLOR_SILVESTRE_SOLEDAD?id=Bachiikxvee7ldlidzpfcelacea&hl=en|website=[[Google Play]]|accessdate=9 January 2015}}</ref>

On 9 March 2015, her documentary titled ''Flor Silvestre: Su destino fue querer'' premiered at the Plaza de las Américas as part of the [[Guadalajara International Film Festival]].<ref name="Flor Silvestre, una vida musical">{{cite news|last1=Jiménez|first1=Lorena|title=Flor Silvestre, una vida musical|url=http://www.mural.com/aplicacioneslibre/preacceso/articulo/default.aspx?id=482650&v=3&urlredirect=http://www.mural.com/aplicaciones/articulo/default.aspx?Id=482650&v=3|accessdate=9 March 2015|work=Mural|date=7 March 2015}}</ref><ref name="Flor Silvestre, pilar de la dinastía Aguilar, estrena documental">{{cite news|title=Flor Silvestre, pilar de la dinastía Aguilar, estrena documental|url=http://estoenlinea.oem.com.mx/58145-flor-silvestre-pilar-de-la-dinastia-aguilar-estrena-documental/|accessdate=9 March 2015|work=Esto|date=7 March 2015}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Silvestre gave birth to her first child, Dalia Inés, when she was 16 years old.<ref name="Dalia Ines">{{cite web|title=Muestra Dalia Inés 'orgullo' familiar|url=http://www.lasnoticiasmexico.com/152647.html|publisher=lasnoticiasmexico.com|accessdate=28 May 2013}}</ref> Dalia Inés publicly omits the name of her biological father, who was a radio announcer, since she says that she considers [[Antonio Aguilar]] as her father figure.<ref name="Dalia Ines" /> Silvestre later married [[Presenter#Television|television presenter]] Paco Malgesto.<ref name="Dalia Ines" /> Their children are Marcela Rubiales Jiménez, a singer and actress, and Francisco Rubiales Jiménez, a voice-over actor.<ref name="Dalia Ines" />

In an interview with [[Don Francisco (television host)|Don Francisco]] in ''Don Francisco presenta'', Silvestre confirmed that during her first few films with [[Antonio Aguilar]] she had not been romantically involved with him.<ref name=DFPFS>{{cite web|title=Flor Silvestre recordó a su Charro|url=http://www.univision.com/content/content.jhtml?cid=1805013|publisher=Univision Interactive Media|accessdate=2009-01-15}}</ref> It wasn't until the film ''Heraclio Bernal'' when both decided to divorce their respective spouses (Aguilar being married to actress Otilia Larrañaga and Silvestre being married to television presenter Paco Malgesto) to marry each other.<ref name="Dificiles momentos">{{cite web|title=Dificiles momentos|url=http://www.cronica.com.mx/notas/2007/307481.html|publisher=La Cronica de Hoy|accessdate=28 May 2013}}</ref> Aguilar and Silvestre finally married on 29 October 1959, shortly before the release of their most recent film ''[[The Soldiers of Pancho Villa|La cucaracha]]''. Their marriage produced two sons: Antonio Aguilar hijo and [[Pepe Aguilar]].

On 28 February 2012, Silvestre underwent surgery to extirpate the [[cancer]]-stricken half of her right [[lung]].<ref name="tvynovelas Flor Silvestre cirugia">{{cite web|title=Le extirparon la mitad del pulmón derecho a la mamá de Pepe Aguilar|url=http://www.tvynovelas.com/en-exclusiva/mx/412641/le-extirparon-mitad-del-pulmon-derecho-mama-pepe-aguilar|publisher=TVyNovelas|accessdate=28 May 2013}}</ref> She responded well to the surgery and has since been recuperating.<ref>{{cite web|title=Flor Silvestre fue operada de tumor en el pulmón|url=http://salud.univision.com/es/salud-de-las-estrellas/flor-silvestre-fue-operada-de-tumor-en-el-pulm%C3%B3n|publisher=Univision|accessdate=28 May 2013}}</ref>

==Awards and honors==
*In 1969, [[Musart Records]] awarded her The Golden Clover (''Trébol de Oro'') for being one of the company's best-selling artists.<ref>{{cite news|title=Discos Musart Awards The Golden Clover To Its Best Selling Artists - 1969|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=PCgEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA38-IA7&dq=flor+silvestre+billboard&hl=en&sa=X&ei=71JRVYvBCMqyoQS5jYHwCQ&ved=0CEIQ6AEwCQ#v=onepage&q=Flor%20Silvestre&f=false|accessdate=12 May 2015|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=28 February 1970}}</ref>
*In 2001, the [[National Association of Actors]] awarded her the [[Eduardo Arozamena Medal]] for her 50-year career.<ref name="Reconocen trayectoria de la dinastía Aguilar">{{cite news|last1=Guzmán Frías|first1=Habacuc|title=Reconocen trayectoria de la dinastía Aguilar|url=http://www.eluniversal.com.mx/espectaculos/30483.html|accessdate=11 November 2014|work=[[El Universal (Mexico City)|El Universal]]|date=14 November 2001}}</ref>
*In 2008, she was the grand marshal of the Comité Mexicano Cívico Patriótico's Mexican Independence Parade in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]].<ref name="Celebran el Los Ángeles desfile por la Independencia de México">{{cite news|title=Celebran el Los Ángeles desfile por la Independencia de México|url=http://www.horacero.com.mx/internacional/celebran-en-los-angeles-desfilepor-la-independencia-de-mexico/|accessdate=12 May 2015|work=Horacero|date=7 September 2008}}</ref>
*In 2010, the twenty-first edition of the World Mariachi Day (''Día Mundial del Mariachi'') awarded her the Pedro Infante Medal for her "outstanding work and dissemination of Mexican music".<ref name="Flor Silvestre y Lucha Villa serán homenajeadas">{{cite news|last1=Camacho|first1=Alma Rosa|title=Flor Silvestre y Lucha Villa serán homenajeadas|url=http://www.oem.com.mx/esto/notas/n1341019.htm|accessdate=11 November 2014|work=Esto|date=28 September 2009}}</ref>
*In 2012, the Confederation of Livestock Organizations awarded her a "bull sculpture" for her contribution to Mexican culture.<ref name="Antonio Aguilar y Flor Silvestre, homenajeados">{{cite news|last1=Camacho|first1=Alma Rosa|title=Antonio Aguilar y Flor Silvestre, homenajeados|url=http://www.oem.com.mx/esto/notas/n2773121.htm|accessdate=11 November 2014|work=Esto|date=16 November 2012}}</ref>
*In 2013, the Association of Mexican Cinema Journalists awarded her the Special Silver Goddess for her career.<ref name="Reconocen a histriones del cine mexicano" /> Mexican actor [[Ignacio López Tarso]] presented her with the award and said: "For me it is a great honor and personal satisfaction to give you this award, to a great figure of Mexican cinema who, either walking or on horseback, made the best movies of the Mexican film industry".<ref name="Reconocen a histriones del cine mexicano">{{cite news|last1=Camacho|first1=Alma Rosa|title=Reconocen a histriones del cine mexicano|url=http://www.oem.com.mx/elsoldepuebla/notas/n3072948.htm|accessdate=11 November 2014|work=El Sol de Puebla|date=1 August 2013}}</ref>
*In 2014, the [[Zacatecas|Government of the State of Zacatecas]] paid tribute to her career and gave her a special accolade at the Teatro Calderón in the state capital as part of the First Corrido Festival.<ref name="Emotivo homenaje a Flor Silvestre">{{cite news|last1=Ortiz|first1=Isela|title=Emotivo homenaje a Flor Silvestre|url=http://www.oem.com.mx/elsoldezacatecas/notas/n3433975.htm|accessdate=11 November 2014|work=El Sol de Zacatecas|date=16 June 2014}}</ref>
*In 2015, while promoting the release of her documentary entitled ''Flor Silvestre: Su destino fue querer'', she was honored in [[Lagos de Moreno]], [[Jalisco]];<ref>{{cite news|last1=Casillas Gómez|first1=Carlos|title=Homenajean a Flor Silvestre|url=http://www.cronicajalisco.com/notas/2015/39018.html|accessdate=12 May 2015|work=La Cronica de Hoy - Jalisco|date=10 March 2015}}</ref> [[Los Angeles]], [[California]];<ref>{{cite news|title=Rinden homenaje a Flor Silvestre en Los Ángeles|url=http://www.eluniversal.com.mx/espectaculos/2015/homenaje-flor-silvestre--1087866.html|accessdate=12 May 2015|work=[[El Universal (Mexico City)|El Universal]]|date=26 March 2015}}</ref> and [[Aguascalientes, Aguascalientes|Aguascalientes]], [[Aguascalientes]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Homenaje de Aguascalientes a la Señora Flor Silvestre|url=http://www.palestraaguascalientes.com/homenaje-de-aguascalientes-a-la-senora-flor-silvestre/|accessdate=12 May 2015|work=Palestra Aguascalientes|date=7 May 2015}}</ref>
