'''Marc Preston Webb'''<ref name=dimeeht>{{cite book|last=Lott Clark|first=Susan|authorlink=|title=Southern letters and life in the mid 1800s|publisher=the University of Wisconsin - Madison|year=1993|location=Wisconsin|pages=|url=|id=}}</ref> (born August 31, 1974) is an American [[music video]], [[short film]], and [[film]] [[Film director|director]]. He made his [[List of directorial debuts|feature film directorial debut]] with the 2009 romantic comedy-drama ''[[(500) Days of Summer]]'' and went on to direct the 2012 [[Spider-Man in film|''Spider-Man'']] [[Reboot (fiction)|reboot]] ''[[The Amazing Spider-Man (2012 film)|The Amazing Spider-Man]]'' and its sequel ''[[The Amazing Spider-Man 2]]'' in 2014.

==Life and career==
Webb was born in [[Bloomington, Indiana]], the son of Margaret Ruth (née Stocker) and Norman Lott Webb, who works in math education at the [[University of Wisconsin–Madison|University of Wisconsin]].<ref name=dimeeht/><ref name=gni>{{cite news|last=Webb|first=Marc|title=Marc Webb's 500 Days of Blogging #1 - Dudes in Love|pages=|publisher=FirstShowing.net|date=2009-06-24|url=http://www.firstshowing.net/2009/06/24/marc-webbs-500-days-of-blogging-1-dudes-in-love/|accessdate=2010-07-06}}</ref> When he was eighteen months old, Webb's family moved to [[Madison, Wisconsin]], where he was raised.<ref name=ref1>{{cite news|last=Thomas|first=Rob|title=Summer in the Spotlight|pages=|publisher=The Capital Times|date=2009-08-09|url=http://host.madison.com/77squaresunday/article_07e737d9-2f06-5316-a8f0-ef560e11ecc6.html|accessdate=2010-07-06}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Dudek|first=Duane|title=Director returning for Madison film debut|pages=|publisher=Milwaukee-Wisconsin Journal Sentinel|date=2009-04-01|url=http://www.jsonline.com/entertainment/movies/42303567.html|accessdate=2010-07-06}}</ref> He graduated from [[Madison West High School]] in 1992, and subsequently attended [[Colorado College]],<ref name=ref1/> and the University of Wisconsin–Madison, from which he graduated with an English degree.<ref name=aila>{{cite news|last=Osterndorf|first=Chris|title=Director Marc Webb moves on from "(500) Days of Summer" and visits DePaul|pages=|work=The Depaulia|date=2009-10-26|url=http://media.www.depauliaonline.com/media/storage/paper1414/news/2009/10/26/Entertainment/Director.Marc.Webb.Moves.On.From.500.Days.Of.Summer.And.Visits.Depaul-3812027.shtml|accessdate=2010-07-06 | archiveurl =https://web.archive.org/web/20091103090148/http://media.www.depauliaonline.com/media/storage/paper1414/news/2009/10/26/Entertainment/Director.Marc.Webb.Moves.On.From.500.Days.Of.Summer.And.Visits.Depaul-3812027.shtml | archivedate=November 3, 2009}}</ref>

His feature length debut, ''[[(500) Days of Summer]]'', starring [[Joseph Gordon-Levitt]] and [[Zooey Deschanel]], was released in July 2009 to a positive critical reception.<ref>{{cite web | title = 500 Days of Summer (2009) | url = http://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/500_days_of_summer/ | work = [[Rotten Tomatoes]] | publisher = [[Flixster]] | accessdate = September 7, 2009}}</ref> In January 2010, [[Columbia Pictures]] hired Webb to direct ''[[The Amazing Spider-Man (2012 film)|The Amazing Spider-Man]]'', a [[reboot (fiction)|reboot]] of the ''[[Spider-Man in film|Spider-Man film franchise]]'', released in July 2012 and starring [[Andrew Garfield]] and [[Emma Stone]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.deadline.com/hollywood/will-sony-spin-spidey-reboot-with-webb/ | title=Will Sony Spin Spidey Reboot With Webb? | publisher=[[Deadline Hollywood]] | author=Fleming, Mike | date=January 13, 2010 | accessdate= January 20, 2010}}</ref> He returned to direct the sequel, ''[[The Amazing Spider-Man 2]]'', which was released on May 2, 2014.

In 2015, Marc Webb directed promotional ads for a partnership between Samsung and Avengers: Age of Ultron.

Webb is signed to DNA in [[Hollywood, California]] and Academy Productions Ltd in the [[United Kingdom]].

==Webb's lamb==
Webb's signature is a white lamb, which has appeared in a few of his videos. In [[Brand New (band)|Brand New]]'s "[[Sic Transit Gloria... Glory Fades]]" video, the lamb appears on the door before [[Jesse Lacey]] enters the bar. It also appears on the shirt of a girl in the bar. In [[Yellowcard]]'s "Ocean Avenue" and "Rough Landing, Holly" videos, the lamb appears on the briefcase [[Ryan Key]] carries around.
