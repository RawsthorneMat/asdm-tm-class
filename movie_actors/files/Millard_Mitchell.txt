'''Millard Mitchell''' (August 14, 1903 â€“ October 13, 1953) was an American [[character actor]] whose credits include roughly thirty feature films and two television appearances.

Born in [[Havana]], [[Cuba]], he appeared as a bit player in eight films between 1931 and 1936. Mitchell returned to film work in 1942 after a six-year absence. Between 1942 and 1953, he was a successful supporting actor.

For his performance in the [[1952 in film|1952]] film, ''[[My Six Convicts]]'', Mitchell won the [[Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actor - Motion Picture|Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actor]]. He is also known for his role as Col. Rufus Plummer in [[Billy Wilder]]'s ''[[A Foreign Affair]]'' (1948), as [[Gregory Peck]]'s commanding officer in the war drama ''[[Twelve O'Clock High]]'' (1949), and as movie mogul "R. F. Simpson" in the musical comedy ''[[Singin' in the Rain]]'' (1952).

Mitchell died at the age of fifty from [[lung cancer]] at St. John's Hospital in [[Santa Monica, California|Santa Monica]],
