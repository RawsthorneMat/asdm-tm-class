[[Image:LorenzoRobledo.jpg|thumb|right|150px|Robledo portraying the tortured character in ''[[For a Few Dollars More]]'' in 1965]]
'''Lorenzo Robledo''' (1921 - September 2006 in [[Madrid]]) was a [[Spain|Spanish]] [[film actor]], who made over 85 appearances in film between 1956 and 1982{{Citation needed|date=November 2014}}. He is a familiar face in Italian westerns appearing in a total of 32 [[Spaghetti Western]] films throughout the 1960s and early 1970s{{Citation needed|date=November 2014}}.

Robledo is probably best known in world cinema for his roles in [[Sergio Leone]]'s Spaghetti Western films of the 1960s and 1970s, portraying minor characters in the trilogy of films ''[[A Fistful of Dollars]]'' (1964), ''[[For a Few Dollars More]]'', (1965) and the ''[[The Good, the Bad and the Ugly]]'' in 1966 and in ''[[Once Upon a Time in the West]]'' in 1968{{Citation needed|date=November 2014}}. He acted in many other westerns prolifically including the tortured sheriff in ''[[Four of the Apocalypse]]'' (1975){{Citation needed|date=November 2014}}.
 
His most notable role in Leone's films where he is more recognizable is ''[[For a Few Dollars More]]'' when he plays the character of a cornered enemy of the main evil villain who tortures him and murders his family before killing him{{Citation needed|date=November 2014}}.

Robledo died in 2006{{Citation needed|date=November 2014}}.
