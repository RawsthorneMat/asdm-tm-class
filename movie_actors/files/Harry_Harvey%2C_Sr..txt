'''Harry W. Harvey, Sr.''' (January 10, 1901 &ndash; November 27, 1985), was an [[United States|American]] [[actor]] of stage, film, and television.

== Career ==
Born in [[Indian Territory]], now [[Oklahoma]], Harvey appeared in [[vaudeville]] and the [[Broadway (theatre)|Broadway]] stage but is best remembered as a [[character actor]] who appeared in more than three hundred films and episodes of [[television series]]. He co-starred in ''[[The Oregon Trail (1936 film)|The Oregon Trail]]'' (1936), with [[John Wayne]], ''Old Overland Trail'' (1953), ''[[Wyoming Renegades]]'' (1954), ''[[Ride Beyond Vengeance]]'' (1966) with [[Chuck Connors]], and many other [[Western (genre)|westerns]]. 

Harvey was cast from 1951 to 1957 in the role of Sheriff Tom Blodgett in fifty-three episodes of ''[[The Roy Rogers Show]]''. He played Mayor George Dixon of fictitious Yellowstone in twenty-one episodes from 1957 to 1959 of the [[Television syndication|syndicated]] western series, ''[[Man Without a Gun]]''. He was cast in different roles in eleven episodes of ''[[The Lone Ranger]]'' from 1949 to 1955. In 1962, he appeared on the short-lived [[NBC]] drama series, ''[[It's a Man's World (TV series)|It's a Man's World]]'' as the recurring character, Houghton Stott, owner of Stott's Service Station. 

In the 1950s, 1960s, and 1970s, he guest-starred in such series as ''[[Branded (TV series)|Branded]]'', ''[[Lassie (1954 TV series)|Lassie]]'', ''[[Hazel (TV series)|Hazel]]'', ''[[The Wild Wild West]]'', ''[[Mannix]]'', ''[[Alias Smith and Jones]]'', ''[[Bonanza]]'', and ''[[Columbo (TV series)|Columbo]]''. His last appearance was in ''[[Adam-12]]'' (1974) with [[Martin Milner]].
