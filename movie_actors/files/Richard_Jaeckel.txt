'''Richard Hanley Jaeckel''' (October 10, 1926 &ndash; June 14, 1997) was an [[United States|American]] [[actor]] of [[film]] and [[television]].<ref>{{cite news|title= Richard Jaeckel Is Dead at 70; A Durable Movie Tough Guy|publisher= The New York Times|date=June 17, 1997|url= http://www.nytimes.com/1997/06/17/movies/richard-jaeckel-is-dead-at-70-a-durable-movie-tough-guy.html|accessdate=2010-09-13|first=Ralph|last=Blumenthal}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title= Obituary: Richard Jaeckel|publisher= Independent|date=June 18, 1997|url= http://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/obituary-richard-jaeckel-1256582.html|accessdate=2010-09-13|location=London|first=Tom|last=Vallance}}</ref> Jaeckel became a highly respected and in-demand [[character actor]] in his career, which spanned six decades. He was honored with a [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor|Best Supporting Actor Oscar]] nomination for his role in the 1971 adaptation of [[Ken Kesey]]'s ''[[Sometimes a Great Notion (film)|Sometimes a Great Notion]]''.

==Life and career==

Jaeckel was born in [[Long Beach, New York|Long Beach]], [[New York (state)|New York]]. A short, tough guy, he played a variety of characters during his fifty years in movies and television and became one of the best known [[character actor]]s in [[Hollywood, California|Hollywood]]. Jaeckel got his start in the business at the age of seventeen while he was employed as a mailboy at [[20th Century Fox]] studios in Hollywood. A casting director auditioned him for a key role in the 1943 film ''[[Guadalcanal Diary (film)|Guadalcanal Diary]]'', Jaeckel won the role and settled into a lengthy career in supporting parts.

He served in the [[United States Merchant Marine]] from 1944 to 1949, then starred in two of the most remembered war films of 1949: ''[[Battleground (film)|Battleground]]'' and ''[[Sands of Iwo Jima]]'' with [[John Wayne]]. One of Jaeckel's shortest film roles was in ''[[The Gunfighter (film)|The Gunfighter]]'', in which his character is killed by [[Gregory Peck]]'s character in the opening scene. He played the role of Turk, the roomer's boyfriend, in the [[Academy Award]]-winning 1952 film ''[[Come Back, Little Sheba (1952 film)|Come Back, Little Sheba]]'', with [[Shirley Booth]], [[Burt Lancaster]], and [[Terry Moore (actress)|Terry Moore]]. In 1960, he appeared as Angus Pierce in the western, ''[[Flaming Star]]'', starring [[Elvis Presley]]. He played [[Lee Marvin]]'s able second-in-command in ''[[The Dirty Dozen]]'' for director [[Robert Aldrich]]. Jaeckel appeared in several other Aldrich films, including ''[[Attack (1956 film)|Attack]]'', ''[[Ulzana's Raid]]'', and ''[[Twilight's Last Gleaming]]''.

Jaeckel also guest-starred on many television programs, one episode of ''[[Bonanza]]'' Between Heaven and Earth. He was also cast as a boxer in a 1954 episode of [[Reed Hadley]]'s [[CBS]] [[legal drama]], ''[[The Public Defender (TV series)|The Public Defender]]''. In 1954, Jaeckel portrayed [[Billy the Kid]] in an episode of the [[Broadcast syndication|syndicated]] [[Western (genre)|western]] [[anthology series]], ''[[Stories of the Century]]'', with [[Jim Davis (actor)|Jim Davis]] as the fictitious Southwest Railroad [[detective]] Matt Clark.

Seven years later, on January 21, 1961, Jaeckel played the character Denver in "The Grudge Fight" of the [[NBC]] western series ''[[The Tall Man (TV series)|The Tall Man]]'', opposite [[Barry Sullivan (actor)|Barry Sullivan]] as [[Pat Garrett]] and [[Clu Gulager]] as [[Billy the Kid]], a fictionalized account of the friendship between Garrett and the Kid. In the storyline, Denver and Billy are thrown out of [[Lincoln, New Mexico|Lincoln]], [[New Mexico]], for persistent brawling and decide to settle their dispute in a shootout in a remote mountainous area. Garrett vows to see the survivor of the match hanged if the other dies. During their journey, the two decide they have much in common, as Billy nurses Denver back to health after Denver breaks a leg.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0717615/|title="The Grudge Fight", ''The Tall Man'', January 21, 1961|publisher=[[Internet Movie Data Base]]|accessdate=February 13, 2013}}</ref>

In 1957, he appeared as Mort Claffey in two episodes, "Paratroop Padre" and "The Light," of the syndicated  religion anthology series, ''[[Crossroads (1955 TV series)|Crossroads]]''. That same year, he portrayed Lieutenant Bradshaw in "War of the Whale Boats" of the military drama, ''[[Navy Log]]''. In 1956 and 1957, he appeared in three episodes of another military drama, ''[[The West Point Story (TV series)|The West Point Story]].''<ref name=jaeckel>{{cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0001395/?ref_=fn_al_nm_1|title=Richard Jaeckel|publisher=Internet Movie Data Base|accessdate=February 16, 2013}}</ref> 

In 1955 and 1958, Jaeckel appeared in different roles on two episodes of CBS's fantasy drama, ''[[The Millionaire (TV series)|The Millionaire]]''.<ref name=jaeckel/> In 1958, Jaeckel guest starred as Webb Martin in the episode "The Bloodline" of NBC's western series ''[[Cimarron City (TV series)|Cimarron City]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ctva.biz/US/Western/CimarronCity.htm|title=''Cimarron City''|publisher=ctva.biz|accessdate=September 9, 2012}}</ref> That same year, he appeared in the syndicated drama of the [[American Civil War]], ''[[Gray Ghost (TV series)|Gray Ghost]]'' in the episode entitled "The Hero". In 1959, Jaeckel was cast as Clint Gleason in "The Man Behind the Star" of CBS's ''[[The Texan (TV series)|The Texan]]'' western series, starring [[Rory Calhoun]].<ref name=jaeckel/> 

In 1960, Jaeckel appeared twice on [[Nick Adams (actor)|Nick Adams]]'s ABC western series, ''[[The Rebel (TV series)|The Rebel]]'', as Marshal Roader in "The Rattler" and as Clyde Traskel in "Run, Killer, Run". 
 
In 1963, Jaeckel played Willie the murderer in "The Case of the Lover's Leap" on CBS's ''[[Perry Mason (TV series)|Perry Mason]]'', starring [[Raymond Burr]].<ref name=jaeckel/> That same year he was among the guest stars on the short-lived [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]]/[[Warner Brothers]] western series, ''[[The Dakotas (TV series)|The Dakotas]]''.  Also in 1963, Jaeckel, speaking in German, played the role of [[Wehrmacht]] Sgt. Buxman in the ''[[Combat!]]'' TV series episode, "Gideon's Army."

In 1966 Jaeckel made a second guest appearance on ''[[Perry Mason (TV series)|Perry Mason]]'' as Mike Woods in the episode, "The Case of the Bogus Buccaneers." 

In 1972, Jaeckel received an [[Academy Awards|Academy Award]] nomination for Best Supporting Actor for his role in ''[[Sometimes a Great Notion (film)|Sometimes a Great Notion]]''. He had a recurring role in the short-lived [[Andy Griffith]] vehicle ''[[Salvage 1]]''. 

In 1977, Jaeckel appeared with [[Donna Mills]], [[Bill Bixby]], and [[William Shatner]] in the last episode, entitled "The Scarlet Ribbon", of NBC's western series ''[[The Oregon Trail (TV series)|The Oregon Trail]]'', starring [[Rod Taylor]] and [[Andrew Stevens]].

In his later years, Jaeckel was known to television audiences as Lt. Ben Edwards on the NBC series ''[[Baywatch]]''. He also co-starred on [[Robert Urich]]'s [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] series ''[[Spenser: For Hire]]'' in the role of Lieutenant Martin Quirk.

[[Barry Jaeckel|His son, Barry]], is a professional [[golf]]er who has won on the [[PGA Tour]].

==Death==

Jaeckel died at the age of seventy after a three-year battle with [[melanoma]], at the [[Motion Picture & Television Country House and Hospital|Motion Picture and Television Hospital]] in [[Woodland Hills, California|Woodland Hills]], [[California]].
