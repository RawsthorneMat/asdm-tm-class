'''Peggy Stewart''' (born '''Margaret O'Rourke'''; June 5, 1923) is an American actress known for her roles in [[Western (genre)|Western]] [[B movie]]s and television series.<ref name="b-westerns">{{cite web|last= |first= |title=Peggy Stewart |publisher=B-Westerns |url=http://www.b-westerns.com/ladies10.htm |accessdate=August 11, 2014}}</ref>

==Biography==
Peggy Stewart was born Margaret O'Rourke on June 5, 1923 in West Palm Beach, Florida, were she was raised.<ref name="nytimes">{{cite news|last=Erickson |first=Hal |title=Peggy Stewart |newspaper=The New York Times |date= |url=http://www.nytimes.com/movies/person/68268/Peggy-Stewart/biography |accessdate=August 11, 2014}}</ref> She distinguished herself as a swimming champion in high school;<ref name="nytimes"/> her sister, Patricia O'Rourke, was an [[Olympic Games|Olympic]] swimmer.<ref name="b-westerns"/> In the 1930s, her family moved to California, where she met character actor Henry O'Neill. He recommended her to Paramount Pictures executives who were looking for a new actress for the part of [[Joel McCrea]]'s teenage daughter in ''[[Wells Fargo (film)|Wells Fargo]]'' (1937).<ref name="nytimes"/> Her work in the film led to numerous other film roles.<ref name="nytimes"/>

In April 1944, Stewart signed a contract with [[Republic Pictures]] and began starring in [[Western (genre)|Western]] [[B movie]]s opposite such actors as [[Allan Lane]], [[Sunset Carson]], and [[Wild Bill Elliott]].<ref name="b-westerns"/> During that time she played in several episodes of ''[[Adventures of Red Ryder]]''. She usually played the part of the tough [[heroine]], rather than a passive girl needing to be saved.<ref name="b-westerns"/> From 1944 to 1951 she starred in 35 films, most of which were Westerns and film serials. She also starred with [[Gene Autry]] several times during that period as well as appearing on several episodes of ''[[The Cisco Kid]]'', including "Oil Land", which first aired on October 10, 1950.

In 1949, she played alongside [[Jim Bannon]] in ''Ride, Ryder, Ride''. She again played the part of heroine to Bannon in 1949, starring in ''[[The Fighting Redhead]]''. In 1952 she starred with Bill Elliott in ''Kansas Territory''. In 1957, she had a minor role on CBS's ''[[Have Gun-Will Travel]]'' episode "The Outlaw". In 1958, she was cast as Etta Jackson, the romantic interest of the painter Hurley Abbott, played by [[Brad Johnson (actor)|Brad Johnson]], in the episode "The Underdog" of the [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]]/[[Desilu]] western series, ''[[The Life and Legend of Wyatt Earp]]'', with [[Hugh O'Brian]] in the title role.

Her career slowed in the 1960s, and by the 1970s she was residing in [[Studio City, California]]. Stewart won the [[Golden Boot Awards]] in 1984. Semi-retired, Stewart still continued to act on occasion. She played a bit role on one episode of ''[[Seinfeld]]'' in 1990 titled "The Implant"; in that episode she played the part of the aunt of [[George Costanza]]'s temporary girlfriend.

In 2009 through 2010, Stewart played [[Pam Beesly]]'s "old-fashioned" grandmother Mee-Maw on [[The Office (US TV series)|The Office]] episode "[[Niagara (The Office)|Niagara]]", and once more in the episode when they baptise Jim's and Pam's daughter Cece. In 2012, she played Grandma Delores in [[Adam Sandler]]'s comedy film, ''[[That's My Boy (2012 film)|That's My Boy]]''.

In recent decades, Stewart has appeared regularly as a guest speaker at Western film festivals.<ref name="nytimes"/> Since 1993, Stewart has appeared as a special guest at the annual Lone Pine Film Festival.<ref name="lpff-25">{{cite web|title=Lone Pine Film Festival Celebrates 25 Years |publisher=Lone Pine Flm Festival |date=August 4, 2014 |url=http://www.lonepinefilmfestival.org/index.php/component/content/article/429 |accessdate=August 11, 2014}}</ref>

Stewart was married twice. In 1940 she married actor [[Don "Red" Barry]]; the marriage ended in divorce in 1944.<ref name="nytimes"/> She was married to actor [[Buck Young]] from 1953 until his death on February 9, 2000.<ref name="tcm-companions">{{cite web|title=Peggy Stewart: Companions |publisher=Turner Classic Movies |url=http://www.tcm.com/tcmdb/person/184766%7C123244/Peggy-Stewart/companions.html |accessdate=August 11, 2014}}</ref>
