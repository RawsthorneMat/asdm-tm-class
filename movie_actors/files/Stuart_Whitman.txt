'''Stuart Maxwell Whitman''' (born February 1, 1928)<ref>According to the State of California. ''California Birth Index, 1905-1995''. Center for Health Statistics, California Department of Health Services, [[Sacramento, California]]. At Ancestry.com</ref> is an [[United States|American]] [[actor]]. He is arguably best known for playing Marshal Jim Crown in the [[Western (genre)|Western]] television series ''[[Cimarron Strip]]'' in 1967. Whitman also starred with [[John Wayne]] in the Western film, ''[[The Comancheros (film)|The Comancheros]]'', in 1961, and received top billing as the romantic lead in the extravagant aerial epic ''[[Those Magnificent Men in Their Flying Machines]]'' in 1965.

== Early life ==
Whitman was born in [[San Francisco]], [[California]], the elder of two sons. His parents, Cecilia (née Gold) and Joseph Whitman, traveled frequently during his childhood, and as a result, he attended over twenty schools.<ref>{{cite web|last=Meyer|first=Jim|title=Stuart Whitman: Dedicated Professional|url=http://www.classicimages.com/articles/2009/10/02/current_issue/whitmanstuart.txt|publisher=Classic Images}}</ref><ref name="bookref1">{{cite book|last=|first=|authorlink=|title=Films and filming|publisher=Hansom Books|year=1958|location=|pages=|url=|id=}}</ref> His family was Jewish.<ref>http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=883&dat=19590828&id=ZTshAAAAIBAJ&sjid=kGEEAAAAIBAJ&pg=5934,780067</ref> He graduated from [[high school]] and spent three years in the [[United States Army Corps of Engineers|Army Corps of Engineers]]. After leaving the army, he enrolled in [[Los Angeles City College]] and the Los Angeles Academy of Dramatic Art.

== Film career ==
[[File:Stewart Whitman Victoria Shaw Cimarron Strip 1968.JPG|thumb|left|Whitman and [[Victoria Shaw (actress)|Victoria Shaw]] in ''[[Cimarron Strip]]'' (1967)]]
Whitman was a [[supporting actor]] in ''[[When Worlds Collide (1951 film)|When Worlds Collide]]'', ''All American'', ''[[Brigadoon]]'', ''[[Silver Lode (1954 film)|Silver Lode]]'', ''[[Ten North Frederick (film)|Ten North Frederick]]'', ''[[The Decks Ran Red]]'' starring [[Dorothy Dandridge]] (on whose face Whitman planted Hollywood's first interrracial kiss), ''[[These Thousand Hills]]'', and ''[[The Sound and the Fury (1959 film)|The Sound and the Fury]]''.

When [[Charlton Heston]], who had originally been signed to play the lead in 1958's ''[[Darby's Rangers (film)|Darby's Rangers]]'' left the film, [[James Garner]] was given the lead and Whitman wound up with Garner's original role in the film.<ref>''Screen World'' 17 March 1961</ref>

His first [[leading man]] role was in ''[[Murder, Inc. (1960 film)|Murder, Inc.]]'' in 1960 with [[May Britt]] and [[Peter Falk]]. He was signed on as a contract star with [[20th Century Fox]].

In 1961, Whitman was nominated for the [[Academy Award for Best Actor]] for his role as a [[pedophilia|child molester]] in ''[[The Mark (1961 film)|The Mark]]'', a role many other better known actors turned down. He has since appeared in starring and supporting roles in many films, including ''[[Francis of Assisi (film)|Francis of Assisi]]'', ''[[The Fiercest Heart]]'', ''[[The Longest Day (film)|The Longest Day]]'' (with all his scenes opposite [[John Wayne]]), ''[[The Comancheros (film)|The Comancheros]]'' (sharing leading man status with Wayne), ''[[Convicts 4]]'', ''The Day and the Hour'', ''Signpost to Murder'', ''[[Shock Treatment (1964 film)|Shock Treatment]]'', ''[[Rio Conchos (1964 film)|Rio Conchos]]'', ''[[Those Magnificent Men in Their Flying Machines]]'', ''[[Sands of the Kalahari]]'', ''The City Beneath the Sea'', ''[[An American Dream (film)|An American Dream]]'', ''[[The Last Escape]]'', ''[[The Invincible Six]]'', ''[[Night of the Lepus]]'', ''[[Shatter (film)|Shatter]]'', ''[[Captain Apache]]'', ''[[Strange Shadows in an Empty Room]]'',  ''[[Guyana: Crime of the Century]]'', ''[[The Treasure Seekers (1979 film)|Treasure Seekers]]'', ''[[The White Buffalo]]'' and ''[[The Story of Ruth]]''.

== Television ==
{{weasel words|section|date=November 2012}}{{BLP unsourced section|date=November 2012}}{{tone|section|date=November 2012}}
[[File:Stuart Whitman in The Longest Day trailer.jpg|thumb|right|Whitman in ''[[The Longest Day (film)|The Longest Day]]'' (1962)]]
In 1957, Whitman, who frequently appeared as police officer Sgt. Walters on the [[television series]] ''[[Highway Patrol (TV series)|Highway Patrol]]'', was seriously considered for the role of "Bart Maverick" in the smash hit [[television series]] ''[[Maverick (TV series)|Maverick]]''. The studio needed another Maverick to rotate as the series lead with [[James Garner]]. Garner, who had filmed seven episodes, closely resembled Whitman at the time, but [[Jack Kelly (actor)|Jack Kelly]] was chosen for the part.

A decade later, Whitman played heroic Marshal Jim Crown in the lavish western TV series ''[[Cimarron Strip]]'' for a single season. The show, which ran 90 minutes per episode, was highly regarded for its theme music, production values, and Whitman's performance. His principal costars were [[Jill Townsend]] as Dulcey Coopersmith, proprietor of the local inn, and [[Randy Boone]], who played the [[photographer]] Francis Wilder.

Later Whitman portrayed [[Clark Kent]]'s father [[Jonathan Kent (comics)|Jonathan Kent]] on the popular TV series ''[[Superboy (TV series)|Superboy]]''.

Whitman made over two hundred appearances in various movies and television shows over a half-century span between 1951 and 2000. One of his early roles came in 1957 in the [[Television syndication|syndicated]] military dramas, ''[[Harbor Command]]'', a drama about the [[United States Coast Guard]], and ''[[The Silent Service (TV series)|The Silent Service]]'', based on true stories of the [[submarine]] service of the [[United States Navy]]. Whitman's last credited role was in ''The President's Man'', released in 2000 and starring [[Chuck Norris]]. He had previously appeared with Norris in a two-part episode of ''[[Walker, Texas Ranger]]''.

He was also a guest on ''[[Murder, She Wrote]]'' appearing in four different episodes, "Hit, Run and Homicide" (1984), "Powder Keg" (1986), "Trouble in Eden" (1987), and "Incident in Lot 7" (1992).
Whitman also appeared in an episode of the TV series ''Ghost Story'' ("The Concrete Captain," c. 1973).  In "Blood Sweat and Cheers", Series 4, Episode 8 of ''[[The A Team]]'', Whitman played Jack Harman, a friend of Hannibal Smith.

== Personal life ==
His first marriage, to Patricia LaLonde (October 13, 1952 – 1966), ended in divorce. They had four children: Tony (b. 1953), Michael (b. 1954), Linda (b. 1956) and Scott (b. 1958). Stuart was married to the [[France|French-born]] Caroline Boubis (1966–1974). They had one son together, Justin. They divorced in 1974. Since 1993 he is married to Julia Vadimovna Paradiz.
