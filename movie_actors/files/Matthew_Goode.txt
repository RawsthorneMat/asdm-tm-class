'''Matthew William Goode''' (born 3 April 1978) is an English actor. His films include ''[[Chasing Liberty]]'' (2004), ''[[Match Point]]'' (2005), ''[[Imagine Me and You]]'' (2005), ''[[Brideshead Revisited (film)|Brideshead Revisited]]'' (2008), ''[[Watchmen (film)|Watchmen]]'' (2009), ''[[A Single Man (2009 film)|A Single Man]]'' (2009), ''[[Leap Year (2010 film)|Leap Year]]'' (2010),  ''[[Stoker (film)|Stoker]]'' (2013), and ''[[The Imitation Game]]'' (2014). In 2014, he joined the cast of the [[CBS]] legal drama ''[[The Good Wife]]'' as Finley "Finn" Polmar.

==Early life==
Goode was born in [[Exeter]], [[Devon]]. His father is a geologist and his mother, Jennifer, is a nurse and amateur theatre director. Goode is the youngest of five children with a brother, two half brothers and a half sister, television presenter [[Sally Meen]], from his mother's previous marriage. He grew up in the village of [[Clyst St. Mary]], near Exeter.<ref name=rouz>{{cite news | url = http://www.thestar.com/entertainment/article/1051094--the-big-interview-matthew-goode-is-burning-his-way-to-the-top | title = The Big Interview: Matthew Goode is burning his way to the top | last = Ouzounian | first = Richard | date = 8 September 2011 |work=The Toronto Star | accessdate =18 March 2015 }}</ref><ref>http://www.thisisexeter.co.uk/Exeter-pupil-s-Hollywood-hope/story-11845967-detail/story.html</ref>

Goode was educated at [[Exeter School]], an independent school in Exeter, Devon, followed by the [[University of Birmingham]] and London's [[Webber Douglas Academy of Dramatic Art]].

==Career==
Goode played Peter Lynley, the brother of Inspector Lynley in the BBC production ''[[Inspector Lynley Mysteries]]: A Suitable Vengeance'' and co-starred in the TV movie ''Confessions of an Ugly Stepsister'', based on the [[Gregory Maguire]] [[Confessions of an Ugly Stepsister|novel of the same name]], and [[William Shakespeare]]'s play ''[[The Tempest (play)|The Tempest]]''. In 2004, Goode made his American film debut opposite [[Mandy Moore]] in the romantic comedy ''[[Chasing Liberty]]'', as the love interest of Moore's character.

Goode co-starred in [[Woody Allen]]'s thriller ''[[Match Point]]'', opposite [[Jonathan Rhys Meyers]] and [[Scarlett Johansson]], Ol Parker's romantic comedy ''[[Imagine Me & You]]'', opposite [[Piper Perabo]] and [[Lena Headey]], the TV movie ''[[My Family and Other Animals (film)|My Family and Other Animals]]'', opposite [[Imelda Staunton]], the biographical-musical drama ''[[Copying Beethoven]]'', opposite [[Ed Harris]] and [[Diane Kruger]], and Scott Frank's crime drama ''[[The Lookout]]'' opposite [[Joseph Gordon-Levitt]]. He had a whimsical take on Brooke Burgess in the BBC miniseries ''[[He Knew He Was Right (TV serial)|He Knew He Was Right]]'' based upon [[Anthony Trollope]]'s [[He Knew He Was Right|novel of the same name]].

In 2008, Goode starred in the drama film ''[[Brideshead Revisited (film)|Brideshead Revisited]]'' as Charles Ryder. It was based on [[Evelyn Waugh]]'s [[Brideshead Revisited|novel of the same name]]. In 2009, Goode co-starred opposite [[Malin Akerman]] in [[Zack Snyder]]'s superhero film ''[[Watchmen (film)|Watchmen]]'' as [[Ozymandias (comics)|Ozymandias/Adrian Veidt]], and co-starred opposite [[Colin Firth]] in the drama film ''[[A Single Man (2009 film)|A Single Man]]'', based on [[Christopher Isherwood]]'s [[A Single Man (novel)|novel of the same name]]. In 2010, he co-starred opposite [[Amy Adams]] in the romantic comedy ''[[Leap Year (2010 film)|Leap Year]]''.

In 2013, Goode played the lead in the critically acclaimed, but short-lived [[BBC Two]] drama [[Dancing on the Edge (TV series)|''Dancing on the Edge'']], as magazine columnist Stanley Mitchell. That same year, he co-starred opposite [[Mia Wasikowska]] and [[Nicole Kidman]] in the [[psychological thriller]] film ''[[Stoker (film)|Stoker]]''.

Also in 2013, Goode co-starred as George Wickham opposite [[Matthew Rhys]] in the three-part BBC murder mystery miniseries ''[[Death Comes to Pemberley (TV series)|Death Comes to Pemberley]]'', based on P. D. James' [[Death Comes to Pemberley|novel of the same name]], and a spin-off of the 1995 drama miniseries ''[[Pride and Prejudice (1995 TV series)|Pride and Prejudice]]'' to commemorate the 200th anniversary of the publication of [[Jane Austen]]'s [[Pride and Prejudice|novel of the same name]].<ref>http://www.bbc.co.uk/mediacentre/latestnews/2013/death-comes-to-pemberley.html</ref>

In March 2014, Goode joined the cast of the [[CBS]] legal drama ''[[The Good Wife]]'' during the fifth season replacing [[Josh Charles]], who played Will Gardner<ref name="insidetv.ew.com">{{cite web|url=http://insidetv.ew.com/2014/03/27/good-wife-josh-charles-julianna-margulies/|title='The Good Wife': Julianna Margulies explains how she negotiated Josh Charles' season 5 contract|first1=Mandi|last1=Bierly|publisher=[[Entertainment Weekly]]|date=27 March 2014}}</ref><ref name="tvline.com">{{cite web|url=http://tvline.com/2014/03/28/the-good-wife-season-5-matthew-goode-series-regular/|title=TVLine Items: Good Wife Ups Goode, Neighbors Star Books Pilot, Clinton Visits Kimmel and More|first1=Vlada|last1=Gelman|publisher=[[TVLine]]|date=28 March 2014}}</ref> as Finley "Finn" Polmar, a State prosecutor. He made his debut in the fifteenth episode titled "Dramatics, Your Honor" in which Will was killed off.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tvguide.com/news/matthew-goode-good-wife-1074153.aspx|title=Matthew Goode Heads to The Good Wife|first1=Natalie|last1=Abrams|publisher=[[TV Guide]]|date=3 December 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://tvline.com/2013/12/03/the-good-wife-season-5-matthew-goode-cast/|title=The Good Wife Exclusive: Matthew Goode to 'Befriend' Alicia in Big Recurring Role|first1=Michael|last1=Ausiello|publisher=TVLine|date=3 December 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tv.com/shows/the-good-wife/community/post/news-briefs-the-good-wife-promotes-matthew-goode-to-series-regular-139604577029/|title=News Briefs: The Good Wife Promotes Matthew Goode to Series Regular|first1=Kaitlin|last1=Thomas|publisher=TVLine|date=29 March 2014}}</ref> That same year, he co-starred in the historical thriller ''[[The Imitation Game]]'' as [[Conel Hugh O'Donel Alexander|Hugh Alexander]], a British Second World War [[cryptanalyst]] and chess champion.<ref name=DB2014>{{cite news|last1=Fallon|first1=Kevin|title=From ‘The Good Wife’ to ‘The Imitation Game’: Matthew Goode Wages His Charm Offensive|url=http://www.thedailybeast.com/articles/2014/11/24/from-the-good-wife-to-the-imitation-game-matthew-goode-wages-his-charm-offensive.html|accessdate=November 24, 2014|publisher=The Daily Beast|date=November 24, 2014}}</ref>

In 2014, Goode co-starred in the [[ITV (TV network)|ITV]] drama ''[[Downton Abbey]]'''s Christmas special titled "A Moorland Holiday" as Henry Talbot.<ref>http://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/tv/reviews/downton-abbey-christmas-special-2014-tv-review-love-is-everywhere-actually-9944878.html</ref>
