'''Asghar Farhadi''' ({{lang-fa|اصغر فرهادی}}, {{IPA-fa|æsɢæɾ ɛ fæɾhɑdiː}}; born 7 May 1972) is an <!-- OSCAR-WINNING DOES NOT GET PLACED HERE -->[[Iranian peoples|Iranian]] film director and screenwriter. For his work as a director, he has received a [[Golden Globe Award]] and an [[Academy Award]] for [[Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film|Best Foreign Language Film]], among many other awards. He was named one of the 100 Most Influential People in the world by ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' magazine in 2012.

==Life and career==
Farhadi was born in Homāyūnshahr (Khomeyni Shahr), [[Isfahan Province|Province of Isfahan]]. He is a graduate of [[theatre]], with a [[Bachelor of Arts|BA]] in Dramatic Arts and [[Master of Arts|MA]] in [[Blocking (stage)|Stage Direction]] from [[University of Tehran]] and [[Tarbiat Modares University]], respectively. Farhadi made short [[8 mm film|8mm]] and [[16 mm film|16mm]] films in the [[Isfahan]] branch of the Iranian Young Cinema Society, before moving on to writing plays and screenplays for [[Islamic Republic of Iran Broadcasting|IRIB]]. He also directed such TV series as ''A Tale of a City'' and co-wrote the screenplay for [[Ebrahim Hatamikia|Ebrahim Hatamikia’s]] ''Low Heights''. ''Dancing in the Dust'' was his feature film debut,<ref>http://www.neshane.com/product-dancing%20in%20the%20dust.htm</ref> which he followed with ''A Beautiful City.''

His third film, ''[[Fireworks Wednesday]]'', won the Gold Hugo at the 2006 [[Chicago International Film Festival]]. His fourth film, ''[[About Elly]]'', won him the [[Silver Bear for Best Director]] at the 59th International [[Berlin International Film Festival|Berlin Film Festival]] and also Best Picture at the [[Tribeca Film Festival]]. The latter film is about a group of Iranians who take a trip to the [[Caspian Sea]] that turns tragic. Famous [[Film theory|film theorist]] and film critic [[David Bordwell]] has called ''About Elly'' a masterpiece.<ref>http://www.davidbordwell.net/blog/2009/03/30/a-masterpiece-and-others-not-to-be-neglected/</ref>

His film ''[[A Separation]]'' premiered on 9 February 2011 at the 29th [[Fajr International Film Festival]] in [[Tehran]] and received critical acclaim from the Iran Society of Film Critics. It won Farhadi four awards including Best Director (for the third time after ''[[Fireworks Wednesday]]'' and ''[[About Elly]]''). On 15 February 2011, it also played in competition at the [[61st Berlin International Film Festival]], which earned him a [[Golden Bear]] for best film, becoming the first Iranian film to win that award. In June 2011, ''A Separation'' won the [[Sydney Film Festival#Winners of the Sydney Film Prize|Sydney Film Prize]] in competition with [[2011 Cannes Film Festival|Cannes Festival's]] winner ''[[The Tree of Life (film)|The Tree of Life]]'', directed by [[Terrence Malick]].<ref name=sff2011-news1>{{cite web |url=http://www.sff.org.au/public/news/2011-sff-official-competition-winner-is/ |title=2011 SFF Official Competition winner is... |work=News |publisher=Sydney Film Festival |date=19 June 2011 |accessdate=5 July 2011 }}</ref>

On 19 December 2011, Farhadi was announced as being on the jury for the [[62nd Berlin International Film Festival]], held in February 2012.<ref name="BerlinJury">{{cite web|url=http://www.berlinale.de/en/presse/pressemitteilungen/alle/Alle-Detail_11861.html |title=Berlinale 2012: International Jury |date=19 December 2011 |accessdate=2011-12-21 |work=berlinale.de}}</ref>

On 15 January 2012, ''A Separation'' won the [[69th Golden Globe Awards|Golden Globe]] for the Best Foreign Language Film.<ref name="Golden Globe">{{cite web|url=http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/movies/2012/01/separation-golden-globes-best-foreign-language-film.html|title=Golden Globes: 'A Separation' wins best foreign language film |date=15 January 2012 |accessdate=16 January 2011 |work=latimes.com}}</ref> 

The film was also the official Iranian submission for the [[Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film|Best Foreign Language Film]] at the [[84th Academy Awards|2012 Academy Awards]], where apart from getting [[List of Academy Award winners and nominees for Best Foreign Language Film|nominated]]<ref>{{cite news|title="A Separation" nominated for foreign-language film Oscar|url=http://www.tehrantimes.com/arts-and-culture/94758-a-separation-nominated-for-foreign-language-film-oscar|publisher=[[Tehran Times]]|date=25 January 2012}}</ref> in this category, it also received a nomination in the [[Academy Award for Best Writing (Original Screenplay)|Best Original Screenplay]] category. On 26 February 2012, ''A Separation'' became the first Iranian movie to have received an [[Academy Award|Oscar]] for the best foreign language film at the [[84th Academy Awards|84th edition of the Academy Awards]]. This marked Farhadi as the first Iranian to have won an [[Academy Award]] in any of the competitive categories.<ref>{{cite web |author=Ronald Grover |title=Iran wins first Oscar with "A Separation |url=http://www.reuters.com/article/2012/02/27/us-oscars-aseparation-idUSTRE81Q06X20120227 |publisher=[[Reuters]] |accessdate=27 February 2012}}</ref> He was invited to join the [[Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences]] in June 2012 along with 175 other individuals.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.oscars.org/press/pressreleases/2012/20120629a.html|title=Academy Invites 176 to Membership|publisher=The Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences|date=29 June 2012|accessdate=19 July 2013}}</ref>

His 2013 film ''[[The Past (film)|The Past]]'', starring [[Bérénice Bejo]] and [[Tahar Rahim]], was nominated for the [[Palme d'Or]] at the [[2013 Cannes Film Festival]].<ref name="Cannes2013">{{cite web|url=http://www.festival-cannes.fr/en/article/59652.html |title=2013 Official Selection|date=18 April 2013|accessdate=18 April 2013|work=Cannes}}</ref> Bejo won the [[Best Actress Award (Cannes Film Festival)|Best Actress Award]] at Cannes for her performance in the film.<ref name="CannesAwards">{{cite web|url=http://www.festival-cannes.fr/en/archives/2013/awardCompetition.html |title=Cannes Film Festival: Awards 2013 |date=26 May 2013|accessdate=26 May 2013|work=Cannes}}</ref><ref name="Chang">{{cite news|url=http://variety.com/2013/film/news/cannes-blue-is-the-warmest-color-wins-palme-d-or-1200488202/|title=Cannes: 'Blue Is the Warmest Color' Wins Palme d' Or|last=Chang|first=Justin|date=26 May 2013|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|accessdate=26 May 2013}}</ref>
