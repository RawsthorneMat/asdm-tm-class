'''Justus D. Barnes''' (October 2, 1862 &ndash; February 6, 1946) was an [[United States|American]] stage and silent film [[actor]]. Barnes is best known for his role as an [[outlaw]] in the 1903  short silent [[Western (genre)|Western]], ''[[The Great Train Robbery (1903 film)|The Great Train Robbery]]''.

==Career==
Barnes was born in [[Little Falls, New York]].<ref>{{cite book|last=Vazzana|first=Eugene Michael |title=Silent Film Necrology|edition=2|year=2001|publisher=McFarland|isbn=0-786-41059-0|page=30}}</ref> He appeared in his best known role, a bandit in the twelve minute silent Western ''The Great Train Robbery'', in 1903.<ref name="nyt_obit">{{cite news|title=Justus D. Barnes; Stage and Screen Actor, 84, Dies in Weedsport, N.Y.|date =February 8, 1946|work=The New York Times|page=19}}</ref> He began acting on the stage before making his film debut in 1903 in ''The Great Train Robbery''. The film was one of the first American films to emphasize narrative. In a memorable scene, Barnes' character points his gun directly at the camera and fires a shot. The film went on to become one of the most successful and best known commercial films in the early era of silent films.<ref>{{cite book|last=Erish|first=Andrew A.|title=Col. William N. Selig, the Man Who Invented Hollywood|year=2012|publisher=University of Texas Press|isbn=0-292-74269-X|page=35}}</ref>

In late July 1908, Barnes was hired as an actor in the stock company for the [[Edison Manufacturing Company]], a film production company owned by [[Thomas Edison]].<ref>{{cite book|last=Musser|first=Charles |title=Before the Nickelodeon: Edwin S. Porter and the Edison Manufacturing Company|year=1991|publisher=University of California Press|isbn=0-520-06080-6|page=417}}</ref> In 1910, he signed on with the [[Thanhouser Company]] in [[New Rochelle, New York]]. From 1910 to 1917, Justus appeared in more than seventy films for the Thanhouser. He portrayed Ham Peggotty in ''[[David Copperfield (1911 film)|David Copperfield]]'', the oldest known film adaption of the [[David Copperfield|1850 novel]] by [[Charles Dickens]].<ref name="nyt_obit"/> He also played supporting roles in ''[[Nicholas Nickleby (1912 film)|Nicholas Nickleby]]'' (1912), ''[[Aurora Floyd (film)|Aurora Floyd]]'' (1912), and ''[[A Dog of Flanders (1914 film)|A Dog of Flanders]]'' (1914). He made his final onscreen appearance in 1917.

==Death==
Barnes died on February 6, 1946 in Weedsport, New York at the age of 83.<ref>{{cite journal|date=February 16, 1949|title=The Final Curtain|journal=Billboard|publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc.|volume=58|issue=7|page=87|issn=0006-2510|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=fBgEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PT86&dq=justus+d+barnes+weedsport&hl=en&sa=X&ei=hlBlVL6eFZTXoATe54CgBw&ved=0CB0Q6AEwAA#v=onepage&q=justus%20d%20barnes%20weedsport&f=false}}</ref>
