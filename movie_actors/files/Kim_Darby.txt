'''Kim Darby''' (born July 8, 1947) is an American [[actress]] best known for her role as Mattie Ross in the film ''[[True Grit]]'' (1969).

==Early life and film career==
Darby was born '''Deborah Zerby''' in [[Los Angeles, California]], the daughter of professional dancers Inga (Wiere) and Jon Zerby. Her mother was from Budapest.<ref>[http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=LA&p_theme=la&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=0EF7C2892E52D8E1&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM NewsLibrary]</ref> Her mother's siblings were comedians who performed as the [[Wiere Brothers]].

Darby began acting at age fifteen. Her first appearance was as a dancer in the film ''[[Bye Bye Birdie (film)|Bye Bye Birdie]]'' (1963). Among her best-known roles are ''True Grit'', in which she played a fourteen-year-old when she was twenty-one years of age; ''[[Gunsmoke (TV series)|Gunsmoke]]'' (1967 episodes "The Lure" and "Vengeance"); ''[[Bonanza]] (TV Series)'' (1967 episode "The Sure Thing"); ''[[The One and Only (1978 film)|The One and Only]]'' (1978); ''[[Better Off Dead (film)|Better Off Dead]]'' (1985); and ''[[Halloween: The Curse of Michael Myers]]'' (1995).

==Television roles==
Darby's television roles included two appearances on the [[NBC]] series ''[[Mr. Novak]]'', starring [[James Franciscus]]; she was cast as Julie Dean in "To Lodge and Dislodge" (1963) and as Judy Wheeler in "The Silent Dissuaders" (1965). Darby also appeared about this time on ''[[The Eleventh Hour (1962 TV series)|The Eleventh Hour]]'', ''[[The Fugitive (TV series)|The Fugitive]]'', ''[[The Donna Reed Show]]'', ''[[Ironside (TV series)|Ironside]]'', and in the first season of ''[[Star Trek]]'' as the title character in [[Miri (Star Trek: The Original Series)|"Miri"]].
 
Darby was cast in an episode of the NBC sitcom ''[[The John Forsythe Show]]'' ("'Tis Better Have Loved and Lost", 1965). and as Angel in the two-part ''Gunsmoke'' episode "Vengeance." She appeared in the episode "Faire Ladies of France" (1967) of the NBC [[Western (genre)|western]] series ''[[The Road West]]'' starring [[Barry Sullivan (actor)|Barry Sullivan]] and a ''[[Bonanza]]'' episode "A Sure Thing" (1967) as Trudy Loughlin, guest starring [[Tom Tully]] as Burt Loughlin, her father. She also appeared in 3 episodes of ''[[Gunsmoke (TV series)|Gunsmoke]]'': "The Lure" (1967) as Carrie Neely, "Vengeance: Part 1" (1967) as Angel, and "Vengeance: Part 2" (1967) again as Angel. She was cast in the 1972 movie, ''[[The People (1972 film)|The People]],'' which also starred [[William Shatner]], reuniting them from their ''Star Trek'' appearance. She also played the unhinged Virginia Caulderwood in the first television [[miniseries]], ''[[Rich Man, Poor Man (TV miniseries)|Rich Man, Poor Man]]'' with [[Nick Nolte]], [[Peter Strauss]], and [[Susan Blakely]] in 1976.

Darby also had the central role of Sally Farnham in the made-for-TV horror film ''[[Don't Be Afraid of the Dark (1973 film)|Don't Be Afraid of the Dark]]'' (1973). Her subsequent television roles included guest appearances on ''[[Crazy Like a Fox (TV series)|Crazy Like a Fox]]'', ''[[The Love Boat]]'', ''[[The Streets of San Francisco]]'', ''[[Riptide (American TV series)|Riptide]]'', and ''[[Becker (TV series)|Becker]]''.

In the late 1980s, she began to teach acting in the Los Angeles area and has been an instructor in the Extension Program at the [[University of California, Los Angeles]] since 1992. Darby also appeared as a female convict in an episode of ''[[The X-Files]]'' ("[[Sein und Zeit (The X-Files)|Sein und Zeit]]", 1999) who falsely confesses to the murder of her son who disappeared under mysterious circumstances related to a current occurrence being investigated by [[Fox Mulder]] and [[Dana Scully]].

In 2014, she played Stacia Clairborne, a partially blind witness to a crime, in the episode "Prologue" of the show ''[[Perception (U.S. TV series)|Perception]]''.

Darby continues to make guest appearances on television and to make occasional films.
