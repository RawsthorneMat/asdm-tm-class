'''Sue Ane Langdon''' (born '''Sue Lookhoff'''; March 8, 1936) is a retired American actress. She began her performing career singing at [[Radio City Music Hall]] and acting in stage productions. In the mid-1960s she appeared in the Broadway musical ''The Apple Tree'',<ref name="The Apple Tree ">{{cite web|title=The Apple Tree|url=http://www.ibdb.com/person.php?id=101904|publisher=[[Internet Broadway Database]]|accessdate=June 2, 2010}}</ref> which starred [[Alan Alda]]. 

In 1976, she appeared in ''[[Hello, Dolly! (musical)|Hello Dolly]]'' at [[The Little Theatre on the Square]].<ref name="The Little Theatre on the Square: Four Decades of a Small-Town Equity Theatre">{{cite book|last=Shervey PhD|first=Beth|title=The Little Theatre on the Square: Four Decades of a Small-Town Equity Theatre|year=2000|publisher=[[Southern Illinois University Press]]|isbn=978-0-8093-2354-8|pages=108|author2=Palmer, Peter}}</ref>  She was featured in many comedies as well as the occasional dramatic film. She appeared in a pair of [[Elvis Presley]] movies, ''[[Roustabout (film)|Roustabout]]'' and ''[[Frankie and Johnny (1966 film)|Frankie and Johnny]]''. Her starring role on ''[[Arnie (TV series)|Arnie]]'' won her a [[Golden Globe Award]] for Best Supporting Actress - Television.

==Biography==
===Early life===
Langdon was born in Paterson, New Jersey to Albert G. Lookhoff (February 26, 1901 – May 1, 1938) and Grace (née Huddle; January 12, 1908 – November 21, 1980). Grace Lookhoff, an operatic soprano, studied music at [[Washington University in Saint Louis]] and [[Juilliard]]. Her opera performances, beginning with her New York debut in [[Lewisohn Stadium]], included appearances with the [[Cleveland Orchestra]], the [[Three Choirs Festival]] ([[Worcester, Massachusetts]]), the [[Elizabeth Sprague Coolidge|Coolidge Festival]] (Washington, D.C.), and the [http://diglib.princeton.edu/ead/getEad?eadid=TC143&kw= Saint Louis Municipal Opera]. Grace's teaching career indicates a timeline of where her daughter grew up.

:* 1941–1947 &ndash; Instructor of Voice and director of the Vesper Choir, [[Coe College]], [[Cedar Rapids, Iowa]]
:* 1947–1951 &ndash; Instructor of Voice, [[Texas A&M University–Kingsville|Texas A&I]], [[Kingsville, Texas|Kingsville]]

Sue Ane was enrolled at the [[University of North Texas College of Music|University of North Texas]]. She was also briefly enrolled full-time at [[Idaho State University]].<ref>Alumni Records, Idaho State University</ref>

===Career===
Langdon's film debut came in ''[[The Great Impostor]]'' (1961), which starred [[Tony Curtis]]. Langdon went on to have leading roles in films such as ''[[The Rounders (1965 film)|The Rounders]]'' (1965), ''[[A Guide for the Married Man]]'' (1967), ''[[A Man Called Dagger]]'', ''[[The Cheyenne Social Club]]'', ''[[A Fine Madness]]''<ref name="Fantasy Femmes of 60's Cinema: Interviews with 20 Actresses from Biker, Beach, and Elvis Movies">{{cite book|last=Lisanti|first=Tom|title=Fantasy Femmes of 60's Cinema: Interviews with 20 Actresses from Biker, Beach, and Elvis Movies|year=2001|publisher=[[McFarland & Company]]|isbn=978-0-7864-0868-9|pages=290–291}}</ref> which led to her posing nude for ''[[Playboy]]'' magazine. In 1966, [[United Artists Pictures]] released ''[[Frankie and Johnny (1966 film)|Frankie and Johnny]]'' in which Langdon co-starred along with [[Elvis Presley]], [[Donna Douglas]] ([[Elly May Clampett]] of ''[[The Beverly Hillbillies]]'' fame) and [[Harry Morgan]] (of ''[[Dragnet (series)|Dragnet]]'' and later ''[[M*A*S*H (TV series)|M*A*S*H]]'' fame).{{cn|date=April 2015}}

[[File:Andy Griffith and Sue Ane Langdon, Andy Griffith Show 1962.jpg|thumb|180px|Langdon with [[Andy Griffith]], 1962.]]
Langdon was the third actress to play Alice Kramden in [[Jackie Gleason]]'s ''[[The Honeymooners]]'' sketches and shows, preceded by [[Pert Kelton]] and [[Audrey Meadows]] and followed by [[Sheila MacRae]] and Meadows again. She shared a ''[[Life (magazine)|Life]]'' magazine cover with Gleason, but played the role only briefly in the 1960s version, during the ''[[The Jackie Gleason Show#American Scene Magazine|American Scene Magazine]]'' era, before MacRae took the role over for the color hour-long musical versions.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/movies/person/40434/Sue-Ane-Langdon|title=Sue Ane Langdon profile|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|accessdate=October 24, 2014}}</ref>

She appeared as Kitty Marsh during the [[NBC]] portion (1959–1961) of ''[[Bachelor Father (U.S. TV series)|Bachelor Father]]''. The next year, she appeared twice on [[Rod Cameron (actor)|Rod Cameron]]'s [[Television syndication|syndicated]] [[crime drama]] ''[[COronado 9]]''. In 1961 she made her first of three appearances on ''[[Perry Mason (TV series)|Perry Mason]]'' as Rowena Leach in "The Case of the Crying Comedian". In 1962, she appeared as nurse Mary Simpson in an episode of CBS's ''[[The Andy Griffith Show]]'' and as Kate Tassel in The "Catawomper" episode of ''[[Gunsmoke]]''. She made her second guest appearance on ''[[Perry Mason]]'' in 1964 as murder victim Bonnie in "The Case of the Scandalous Sculptor." She made guest appearances on ''[[Bourbon Street Beat]]'', ''[[Room for One More (TV series)|Room for One More]]'', ''[[Mannix]]'', ''[[Thriller (U.S. TV series)|Thriller]]'', ''[[Bonanza]]'', ''[[Ironside (TV series)|Ironside]]'', ''[[McHale's Navy]]'', ''[[Banacek]]'', ''[[The Wild Wild West]]'', ''[[The Dick Van Dyke Show]]'', ''[[Three's Company]]'' and ''[[Happy Days]]''.

==Personal life==
Sue Ane Lookhoff married Jack Emrek (born [https://familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:K8YF-ZZP Robert J. Hanusek]; 1920–2010) on April 4, 1959 in [[Las Vegas, Nevada]].<ref>[https://familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:VVKG-LXJ], ''Nevada Marriage Index, 1956–2005'', familysearch.org; accessed April 6, 2015.</ref> The couple remained married until his death on April 27, 2010 in [[Calabasas, California]]. Emrek was a motion picture, stage and television director.<ref>{{cite news|title=Actress Comes Home for Visit in Texas|newspaper=[[The Dallas Morning News]]|date=February 21, 1961}}</ref>
