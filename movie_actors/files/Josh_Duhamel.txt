'''Joshua David''' "'''Josh'''" '''Duhamel''' ({{IPAc-en|d|ə|ˈ|m|ɛ|l}} {{respell|də|MEL|'}}; born November 14, 1972) is an American actor and former fashion model. His first acting role was as [[Leo du Pres]] on ABC's ''[[All My Children]]'' and later as head of security [[Danny McCoy#Cast and characters|Danny McCoy]] on NBC's ''[[Las Vegas (TV series)|Las Vegas]]''. He then began appearing in films, playing as one of the main protagonists in the first three films of the [[Transformers (film series)|''Transformers'' film series]]. He has also starred in romance films including ''[[When in Rome (2010 film)|When in Rome]]'', ''[[Life_as_We_Know_It_(film)|Life as We Know It]]'', ''[[New Year's Eve (2011 film)|New Year's Eve]]'', and ''[[Safe Haven (film)|Safe Haven]]''. He has also starred in the more recent film [[You're Not You]].  Duhamel co-starred with [[Dean Winters]] in one season of the CBS Network series cop drama [[Battle Creek (TV series)|Battle Creek]].

==Early life==
Duhamel was born in [[Minot, North Dakota]]. His mother, Bonnie L. Kemper, is a massage therapist, and his father, Larry Duhamel, is an advertising salesman.<ref name="Bio">{{cite web|title=Josh Duhamel profile at FilmReference.com|publisher=FilmReference.com|url=http://www.filmreference.com/film/64/Josh-Duhamel.html|accessdate=September 20, 2008}}</ref><ref name=ega/> He is of French-Canadian, Irish, English, German, and Norwegian ancestry.<ref name=ega>[http://freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com/~battle/celeb/duhamel.htm Duhamel's genealogy site]</ref> Duhamel was raised Catholic.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.okmagazine.com/2011/05/josh-duhamel-is-a-strong-man-in-tights-on-ellen-degeneres|title=Josh Duhamel Is a (Strong) Man in Tights on ‘Ellen DeGeneres’|publisher=[[OK!]]|accessdate=September 5, 2011}}</ref>

His parents divorced during his youth. Although he remains close to both, he grew up with his mother and his three younger sisters, Ashlee, Mckenzee, and Kassidy. Duhamel attended [[Minot State University]] and played as the quarterback for the [[Minot State Beavers football|university's football team]].<ref name=USAWeekend/> He planned to attend dental school,<ref name=NBC>{{cite web|title=NBC.com|publisher=NBC|url=http://www.nbc.com/Las_Vegas/bios/josh_duhamel.shtml|accessdate=September 20, 2008}}</ref> but dropped out one-and-a-half credits shy of his undergraduate degree.<ref name="People">{{cite web|title=Josh Duhamel Biography|work=[[People (magazine)|People Weekly]]|publisher=[[Time Inc]]|url=http://www.people.com/people/josh_duhamel/biography|accessdate=September 20, 2008}}</ref> He later completed his credits, and he received his degree in 2005.<ref>{{cite journal |last=Rainey |first=Candice |date=July 1, 2005 |title=Everybody's All American |url=http://www.gq.com/style/wear-it-now/200506/josh-duhamel-las-vegas |journal=GQ |location=New York, NY |publisher=Advance Publications}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |date=October 4, 2005 |title=Duhamel Earns A Degree - 13 Years After Starting It |url= http://hub.contactmusic.com/josh-duhamel/news/duhamel-earns-a-degree---13-years-after-starting-it|journal=Contact Music |location=New York, NY |publisher=http://www.contactmusic.com/}}</ref>

==Career==
Duhamel has stated, "After college, I followed an ex-girlfriend to northern [[California]], did a bunch of odd jobs." Duhamel had won the title of Male Model of the Year in an [[International Modeling and Talent Association]] (IMTA) competition in 1997<ref name=IMTA>{{cite web|title=IMTA Alum Josh Duhamel Transforms Into Action Hero|publisher=International Modeling and Talent Agency|date=July 2, 2007|url=http://www.imta.com/pressDetail.aspx?type=joshduhamel070207|accessdate=September 20, 2008}}</ref> (the runner-up was actor [[Ashton Kutcher]]).<ref name=JoshAshton>{{cite web|title=Josh Duhamel: People.com|work=People Weekly|publisher=Time Inc|url=http://www.people.com/people/josh_duhamel|accessdate=September 20, 2008}}</ref>

Duhamel began his acting career as an [[extra (actor)|extra]] in the music videos for [[Donna Summer]]'s "I Will Go With You ([[Con te partirò]])" and [[Christina Aguilera]]'s "Genie in A Bottle" in 1999.<ref name=USAWeekend>{{cite web|last=Hatty|first=Michele|title=Meet up-and-comer Josh Duhamel|work=[[USA Weekend]]|publisher=[[Gannett Company]]|date=February 6, 2005|url=http://www.usaweekend.com/05_issues/050206/050206josh_duhamel.html|accessdate=September 20, 2008}} {{Dead link|date=September 2010|bot=H3llBot}}</ref> Josh trained with [http://www.scottseditaacting.com Scott Sedita Acting Studios]. Later that year, he won the role of [[Leo du Pres]] on the [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] soap opera ''[[All My Children]]''. His work on the show, especially [[Leo du Pres and Greenlee Smythe|his character's pairing]] with [[Greenlee Smythe]] (portrayed by actor [[Rebecca Budig]]), garnered critical acclaim.<ref name="SoapOperaDigest.com">{{cite news|title=Best New Couple|work=[[Soap Opera Digest]]|year=2000|accessdate=September 6, 2007}}</ref><ref name="eyeonsoaps.net">{{cite web| first=Carolyn|last=Aspenson|title=Redefining the Super Couple|publisher=eyeonsoaps.net|date=February 5, 2009|accessdate=March 5, 2009|url=http://replay.web.archive.org/20090627112608/http://eyeonsoaps.net/wp/?p=1765}}</ref> In 2000, he posed fully nude for Greg Gorman's "As I See It" photography book.

In 2003, he earned a [[Daytime Emmy Award]] nomination for the Special Fan Award for America's Favorite Couple in 2002, shared with Budig, and a Daytime Emmy Award for [[Daytime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Drama Series|Outstanding Supporting Actor]] for his portrayal of Leo du Pres.<ref>{{cite web|title=The 29th Annual Daytime Emmy Awards|publisher=SoapCentral.com|url=http://www.soapcentral.com/soapcentral/awards/emmys/2001.php|accessdate=September 20, 2008}}</ref> Duhamel left ''All My Children'' in 2002 to pursue other acting opportunities. In 2003, Duhamel landed a primetime role on the [[NBC]] show ''[[Las Vegas (TV series)|Las Vegas]]'', playing the head of security for the [[Montecito Casino]], Danny McCoy. His character succeeded [[James Caan (actor)|James Caan]]'s as head of the casino after Caan departed the series at the end of the fourth season. The series ended in 2008.

Duhamel made his big-screen acting debut in 2004's ''[[Win a Date with Tad Hamilton!]]'' and continued on to star in the thriller ''[[Turistas]]'' (2006). After watching an episode of ''Las Vegas'' in which Duhamel's character had just returned from the war in Iraq, [[Steven Spielberg]] handpicked him for the role of Captain William Lennox in 2007's summer [[Blockbuster (entertainment)|blockbuster]] film ''[[Transformers (film)|Transformers]]''<ref name=IMTA/> (a film for which Spielberg was the [[Executive producer#Motion pictures and television|executive producer]]). Duhamel reprised the role for the sequels, ''[[Transformers: Revenge of the Fallen]]'', released in June 2009,<ref name=Variety>{{cite web|title='Transformers' sequel sets 2009 date|work=Variety |publisher=[[Reed Business Information]]|url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117972852.html?categoryid=13&cs=1|last=McClintock|first=Pamela|date=September 26, 2007|accessdate=September 20, 2008}}</ref> and ''[[Transformers: Dark of the Moon]]'', released in June 2011.

Duhamel was the official [[pace car]] driver for the [[2009 Indianapolis 500]], driving the 2010 [[Chevrolet Camaro]] Pace Car.<ref name="Josh D">{{cite web|url=http://www.indy500.com/news/13760/Actor_Josh_Duhamel_To_Drive_Chevrolet_Camaro_Pace_Car_At_Indy_500|title=Actor Josh Duhamel To Drive Chevrolet Camaro Pace Car At Indy 500|publisher=indy500.com|date=2009-04-30|accessdate=2009-04-30}}</ref> In 2010, he played the lead role in the film ''When in Rome'' as Nick Beamon. In 2014 Duhamel starred in the touching drama [[You're Not You]] (2014) playing the husband of Kate ([[Hilary Swank]]), who suffers from the degenerative disease [[ALS]].

On March 23, 2013, Duhamel hosted the [[2013 Kids' Choice Awards]], which was broadcast live from [[Los Angeles]], [[California]].<ref>The Associated Press, [http://www.miamiherald.com/2013/01/23/3196090/josh-duhamel-named-host-of-kids.html Josh Duhamel named host of Kids' Choice Awards], ''The Miami Herald'', January 23, 2013</ref> He and his co-star from ''[[Safe Haven (film)|Safe Haven]]'', [[Julianne Hough]], performed a ''Safe Haven''-themed showcase appearance via video on ''[[The Price is Right (U.S. game show)|The Price is Right]]'' with [[Drew Carey]].

==Personal life==
Duhamel met and began dating singer [[Fergie (singer)|Stacy Ann "Fergie" Ferguson]] in September 2004, after Ferguson appeared on Duhamel's show ''[[Las Vegas (TV series)|Las Vegas]]'' with her band [[The Black Eyed Peas]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Josh Duhamel Reveals His Pick-Up Line on Fergie|work=People Weekly|publisher=Time Inc|date=June 19, 2007|url=http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,20043023,00.html|accessdate=September 20, 2008}}</ref> The couple wed on January 10, 2009, in a Roman Catholic ceremony at the Church Estate Vineyards in [[Malibu, California]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.elle.com/Pop-Culture/Movies-TV-Music-Books/Fergie-and-Josh|title=Fergie and Josh|publisher=[[Elle (magazine)|Elle]]|date=March 4, 2009|accessdate=September 12, 2010|first=|last=}}</ref><ref name=Married>{{cite web|title=It's Official: Fergie & Josh Are Married!|work=People Weekly|publisher=Time Inc|date=January 10, 2009|url=http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,20251507,00.html|accessdate=February 4, 2009}}</ref> They have one son, Axl Jack Duhamel, born on August 29, 2013.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://celebritybabies.people.com/2013/08/29/josh-duhamel-fergie-welcome-son-axl-jack/|title=Josh and Fergie Duhamel Welcome Son Axl Jack|work=[[People (magazine)|People]]|date=2013-08-29|accessdate=2013-08-29}}</ref>

Both Duhamel and Fergie are practising Catholics and attend church.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.parade.com/celebrity/2010/09/the-softer-side-of-josh-duhamel.html|title=The Softer Side of Josh Duhamel|publisher=[[Parade (magazine)|Parade]]|accessdate=September 5, 2011}}</ref> In 2005, he became the co-owner of 10 North Main, a restaurant in [[Minot, North Dakota]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Thomas|first=Leeann|title=Three Romantic Restaurants in Minot, North Dakota|url=http://voices.yahoo.com/three-romantic-restaurants-minot-north-dakota-5887780.html|accessdate=July 1, 2013|newspaper=[[Yahoo! Voices]]|date=April 23, 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Squirrel Not On The Menu At Duhamel's Restaurant|url=http://www.cinemablend.com/pop/Squirrel-Not-On-The-Menu-At-Duhamel-s-Restaurant-18370.html|accessdate=July 1, 2013|newspaper=Cinema blend|date=June 26, 2009}}</ref>
