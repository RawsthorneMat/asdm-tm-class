'''Eduardo "Eddie" Verchez García''' (born '''Jesús García Verchez''' on born May 2, 1929)<ref name="birth"/><ref name=Newsflash>{{cite news|last1=Agbayani|first1=Claire|title=The Simple Truth About Eddie Garcia|url=http://www.newsflash.org/2002/11/sb/sb002534.htm|accessdate=28 October 2014|publisher=[[The Philippine Star]]|date=6 December 2002}}</ref>
<ref name=PEP>{{cite news|last1=Mendoza|first1=Abby|title=The longevity of Eddie Garcia|url=http://www.pep.ph/celeb/people-and-lifestyle/hobbies-and-ventures/28556/The-longevity-of-Eddie-Garcia|accessdate=28 October 2014|publisher=Philippine Entertainment Portal (PEP)|date=29 March 2013}}</ref>
popularly known as ''Manoy''<ref name=PEP /><ref name="abs1" /> is a [[Philippines|Filipino]] [[film actor]] and film director.

With over 300 films as of 2011,<ref name=PhilStar3>{{cite news|last1=Tariman|first1=Pablo A.|title=The ageless Eddie Garcia|url=http://www.philstar.com/entertainment/680724/ageless-eddie-garcia|accessdate=29 October 2014|publisher=The Philippine Star|date=30 April 2011}}</ref> he is the Filipino actor who has appeared in the most number of movies.<ref name=MPP>{{cite web|last1=Zulueta|first1=Lito B.|title=EDDIE GARCIA: Actor, director, icon, Philippine cinema’s one-man totem pole|url=http://www.manunuri.com/eddie_garcia_actor_director_icon_philippine_cinema%E2%80%99s_one_man_totem_pole|website=Manunuri ng Pelikulang Pilipino (MPP)|publisher=MPP|accessdate=28 October 2014}}</ref>

He is also the only person in the Philippines inducted into the [[Filipino Academy of Movie Arts and Sciences Award|FAMAS]] ''Hall of Fame'' in three categories: ''[[FAMAS Award for Best Actor|Best Actor]]'', ''Best Supporting Actor'' and ''[[FAMAS Award for Best Director|Best Director]]''.<ref name=PEP />

== Biography ==
Born to Antonio García and Vicenta Verchez on May 2, 1929 in [[Sorsogon City|Sorsogon]], [[Sorsogon]],<ref name="birth"/><ref name=Newsflash /><ref name=PEP /><ref name=Interaksyon>{{cite news|last1=Mateo|first1=Pilar|title=Eddie Garcia: ‘One of these days, I’ll follow Dolphy, and I have everything prepared’|url=http://www.interaksyon.com/entertainment/eddie-garcia-one-of-these-days-ill-follow-dolphy-and-i-have-everything-prepared/|accessdate=28 October 2014|publisher=Interaksyon.com|date=26 July 2012}}</ref> he spent his early life in the municipality of [[Juban, Sorsogon|Juban]].<ref name=PhilStar3 /> He served with the [[Philippine Scouts]] during [[World War II]] and was stationed as a military policeman in [[Okinawa, Japan]] before joining show business.<ref name=Newsflash /><ref name=MPP />

=== Career ===
Garcia started acting in the movie ''Siete Infantes de Lara'' in 1949.<ref name=Newsflash /><ref name=PhilStar3 /><ref name=MPP />

He directed his first movie "''Karugtong ng Kahapon''" in 1961.<ref name=PhilStar3 />

He is known for portraying roles of either the main villain, the lead or a supporting character. He is also known for his trademark humorous lines which he says mostly in action films or comedy films.<ref name="abs1">{{cite news |first=Reyman |last=Buan-Deveza |authorlink= |coauthors= |title=Eddie Garcia stars in indie film 'Fuchsia' |url=http://www.abs-cbnnews.com/storyPage.aspx?storyId=126378 |work= |publisher=ABS-CBN News Online |date=2008-07-25 |accessdate=2008-08-09 }} {{Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot}}</ref>

As of March 2011, he has appeared in approximately 400 movies and TV shows.<ref name=YahooCelebPH />

Since 2013, Garcia is currently a part of [[ABS-CBN]].<ref name=PhilNews>{{cite news|last1=Sarraga|first1=Ed|title=Eddie Garcia Now a Certified Kapamilya Actor|url=http://philnews.ph/2013/02/02/eddie-garcia-now-a-certified-kapamilya-actor/|accessdate=29 October 2014|publisher=Philippine News|date=2 February 2013}}</ref> He first appeared on ''[[Juan dela Cruz (TV series)|Juan dela Cruz]]'',<ref name=BEOnline /> and later on ''[[Honesto]]''. In 2014, he is part of the cast of ''[[Sana Bukas Pa Ang Kahapon]]''.

==Personal life==
Garcia has always been private on his personal life. He considers his acting profession and private life as separate.<ref name=Newsflash /><ref name=PEP /><ref name=PhilStar3 /> A seasoned actor, he lives a relatively simple life without any luxury cars and expensive equipment, enjoying only [[shooting sport|target shooting as a sport]].<ref name=YahooCelebPH>{{cite news|last1=Sicam|first1=Ed|title=Eddie Garcia, 89, works non-stop for 62 years|url=https://ph.celebrity.yahoo.com/blogs/switchingchannels/eddie-garcia-89-works-non-stop-62-years-20110323-000321-549.html|accessdate=28 October 2014|publisher=[[Yahoo!]] Celebrity Philippines|date=23 March 2011}}</ref>
<ref name=YahooNewsMB>{{cite news|title=Eddie Garcia Has Got It All Figured Out|url=https://ph.news.yahoo.com/eddie-garcia-got-figured-063356668.html|accessdate=29 October 2014|publisher=[[Manila Bulletin]]|date=23 July 2012|language=English, [[Tagalog]]}}</ref>
Garcia maintains a healthy lifestyle through exercise,<ref name=Newsflash /> vitamin supplements,<ref name=ABS-CBN>{{cite news|title=Eddie Garcia: I'm still alive|url=http://www.abs-cbnnews.com/entertainment/09/05/12/eddie-garcia-im-still-alive|accessdate=28 October 2014|publisher=[[ABS-CBN]] News|date=5 September 2012}}</ref> and healthy diet, stressing he prefers fish and vegetables more than meat.<ref name=PEP /><ref name=PhilStar3 />

His family is among the [[Spanish settlement in the Philippines|descendants of Spanish Pinaminomons settlers]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.asianjournalusa.com/default.asp?sourceid=&smenu=157&twindow=Default&mad=No&sdetail=7914&wpage=1&skeyword=&sidate=&ccat=&ccatm=&restate=&restatus=&reoption=&retype=&repmin=&repmax=&rebed=&rebath=&subname=&pform=&sc=1028&hn=asianjournalusa&he=.com|title=Asian Journal USA}}</ref> He is a former member of the [[Philippine Scouts]] in [[Okinawa]] during [[World War II]]. The Philippine Military Academy Marangal (Honorable) Class of 1974 also adopted him as one of their honorary classmates, alongside First Gentleman [[Mike Arroyo]].

He has a daughter living in San Diego, California by the name of Lisa Ortega. Not much are known about his spouse or his family. Throughout his career, he has been known for his professionalism, and has evaded intrigues and controversies linking him to any of his fellow co-stars,<ref name=BEOnline>{{cite news|last1=Belen|first1=Walden Sadiri M.|title=Why Eddie Garcia Has No Intrigues Or Controversies|url=http://www.beonline.com.ph/article.php?id=1273&Cid=2&PPid=|accessdate=29 October 2014|publisher=Bulletin Entertainment Online|date=8 November 2013|language=English, [[Tagalog]]}}</ref> unlike his contemporaries who are known for their various relationships with other women.

He was also good friends with fellow veteran actors [[Dolphy]] and [[Fernando Poe Jr.]] He also witnessed the last moment of FPJ. Earlier that year during the 2004 presidential elections, he and Dolphy were campaigning alongside FPJ.

==Awards and nominations==
{{Main|List of awards and nominations received by Eddie Garcia}}

Garcia has received numerous awards throughout his career. Despite this, he remains level-headed, stressing he doesn't make movies aiming for awards.<ref name=YahooNewsMB /><ref name=PEP2 />

===FAMAS Awards===
Garcia is the most awarded and nominated person in the long history of the [[Filipino Academy of Movie Arts and Sciences]] (FAMAS) Awards. He garnered a total of 34 nominations (13 for ''Best Supporting Actor'', 10 for ''Best Actor'' and 11 for ''Best Director''). Out of these, he got 6 ''Best Supporting Actor'' wins, 5 ''Best Actor'' wins and 5 ''Best Director wins'', 3 ''Hall of Fame Awards'', 1 ''Lifetime Achievement Award'' and the ''Fernando Poe, Jr. Memorial Award''. He was awarded his first FAMAS Award in 1957 and his last FAMAS, a ''Hall of Fame for Best Actor'', in 2003.

He is the only performer in Philippine movie history to win three consecutive FAMAS Awards: ''Best Supporting Actor'' Awards for ''Taga sa Bato'' (1957), ''Condenado'' (1958) and ''Tanikalang Apoy'' (1959).<ref name="abs1" />

Garcia is also the first actor to be inducted in the FAMAS ''Best Supporting Actor Hall of Fame'' of the Filipino Academy of Movie Arts and Sciences in 1974.

===Luna Awards===
Three [[Luna Awards]] for ''Best Director'' for the movies ''Magdusa Ka'' in 1986, ''Saan Nagtatago and Pag-ibig'' in 1987, and ''Abakada Ina'' in 2001 were conferred upon him by the [[Film Academy of the Philippines]].<ref name=FAP>{{cite web|title=Awards: Winners|url=http://filmacademyphil.org/fap_awards.php|website=[[Film Academy of the Philippines]]|accessdate=29 October 2014}}</ref>

He was bestowed the ''Best Actor'' award for the movie [[Deathrow (film)|Death Row]] in 2000.<ref name=FAP />

===MMFF-P Awards===
The [[Metro Manila Film Festival]] Philippines Award for Best Director was conferred to him for the film ''Imortal'' in 1989.<ref name=FAP />

He was awarded the ''Best Actor'' for the movie [[Mano Po]] in 2002.<ref name=FAP />

===Gawad Urian Awards===
He earned the ''[[Gawad Urian Award|Urian Award]]'' for ''Pangunahing Aktor'' (Best Actor) for the movie [[Deathrow (film)|Death Row]] in 2000.<ref name=FAP />

The ''Natatanging Gawad Urian'' award was bestowed upon him in 2006.<ref name=FAP />

===Lifetime achievement awards===
In 1998, he earned the [[Film Academy of the Philippines|Film Academy of the Philippines (FAP)]] ''Lifetime Achievement Award''.<ref name=FAP />

He was also awarded the first ''[[Dolphy]] Lifetime Achievement Award - Ulirang Alagad ng Sining'' during the Entertainment Press Society’s ''Golden Screen TV Awards'' in 2013.<ref name=PhilStar2>{{cite news|last1=Calderon|first1=Ricky|title=Gloria Romero, Mel Tiangco, Nova Villa, Eddie Garcia get special lifetime achievement citations in Golden Screen TV Awards|url=http://www.philstar.com/cebu-entertainment/2013/02/23/912204/gloria-romero-mel-tiangco-nova-villa-eddie-garcia-get-special|accessdate=28 October 2014|publisher=[[The Philippine Star]]|date=23 February 2013}}</ref>

===International film awards===
Garcia earned his first international film award at the 55th [[Asia-Pacific Film Festival]] as the lead actor for the movie [[Bwakaw]] in December 15, 2012, garnering the ''[[Asian Film Award for Best Actor|Best Actor]]'' award.<ref name=PEP2>{{cite news|last1=Dimaculangan|first1=Jocelyn|title=Eddie Garcia named Best Actor; gets standing ovation at 55th Asia-Pacific Film Festival|url=http://www.pep.ph/news/36836/eddie-garcia-named-best-actor-gets-standing-ovation-at-55th-asia-pacific-film-festival|accessdate=28 October 2014|publisher=Philippine Entertainment Portal (PEP)|date=18 December 2012}}</ref>
