'''Dan "Danny" Trejo''' ({{IPA-es|ˈtɾexo}}; born May 16, 1944) is an American actor who has appeared in numerous [[Cinema of the United States|Hollywood]] films, often as [[hypermasculinity|hypermasculine]] characters, villains and [[anti-hero]]es. Some of his notable films include ''[[Heat (1995 film)|Heat]]'' (1995), ''[[Con Air]]'' (1997), and ''[[Desperado (film)|Desperado]]'' (1995), the latter with frequent collaborator [[Robert Rodriguez]].  Trejo is perhaps most recognized as the character [[Machete (character)|Machete]], originally developed by Rodriguez for the ''[[Spy Kids (franchise)|Spy Kids]]'' series of movies and later expanded into Trejo's own series of films aimed at a more adult audience. He also appeared in TV shows such as ''[[Breaking Bad]]'' and ''[[Sons of Anarchy]]''.

== Early life ==
Dan Trejo, who is of [[Mexico|Mexican]] descent,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.boxoffice.com/articles/2010-05-danny-trejo-talks-predators-machete-and-the-jonas-brothers|title=Danny Trejo Talks 'Predators,' 'Machete' and The Jonas Brothers|author=Amy Nicholson}}</ref> was born in the [[Echo Park, Los Angeles|Echo Park]] neighborhood of [[Los Angeles]], California.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.nydailynews.com/latino/2007/05/09/2007-05-09_he_aint_that_bad.html | location=New York | work=Daily News | title=He ain't that bad | first=Lewis | last=Beale | date=May 9, 2007}}</ref><ref name="bowles">{{Cite news |first=Scott |last=Bowles |date=September 3, 2010 |title='Machete' star Danny Trejo is an illustrated man, in many ways |newspaper=[[USA Today]] |url=http://www.usatoday.com/life/movies/news/2010-09-03-trejo03_st_N.htm |accessdate=November 4, 2011 }}</ref> He is the son of Alice Rivera and Dan Trejo, a [[construction]] worker,<ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.filmreference.com/film/66/Danny-Trejo.html |title=Danny Trejo Biography (1944–)|website=Film Reference}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://movies.yahoo.com/movie/contributor/1800197572/bio| title= Danny Trejo Biography |website= Yahoo! Movies}}</ref> and a [[second cousin]] of filmmaker [[Robert Rodriguez]], though the two were unaware that they were related until the filming of ''[[Desperado (film)|Desperado]]''.<ref name=swe>{{cite news|last=Strauss|first=Bob|title=ROBERT RODRIGUEZ WEARS A HOST OF HATS, STAYS AWAY FROM TINSELTOWN AND EMBRACES DIGITAL PHOTOGRAPHY WITH GRITTY END TO `MARIACHI' TRILOGY|pages=|publisher=Daily News (Los Angeles, CA)|date=September 11, 2003|url=http://www.thefreelibrary.com/ROBERT+RODRIGUEZ+WEARS+A+HOST+OF+HATS,+STAYS+AWAY+FROM+TINSELTOWN+AND...-a0107651189|accessdate=August 30, 2010}}</ref><ref name=dla>{{Cite news|last=Barlow|first=Helen|title=Robert Rodriguez and Danny Trejo cutting a short story long|pages=|publisher=The New Zealand Herald|date=November 11, 2010|url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/entertainment/news/article.cfm?c_id=1501119&objectid=10686699|accessdate=November 12, 2010}}</ref>

Throughout the 1960s, Trejo was in and out of jail and prison in California; Trejo credits his appearance as contributory to his constantly getting into trouble.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bigissue.com/features/interviews/3505/danny-trejo-interview-my-looks-used-to-get-me-in-trouble-but-now-theyre|title=Danny Trejo interview: "My looks used to get me into trouble but now the're paying off"|first=Steven|last=Mackenzie|date=13 February 2014|work=The Big Issue}}</ref> There are conflicting accounts of his prison chronology. By one account, his final term in custody ended in 1972;<ref name = "bowles"/> by another account, he did time in a juvenile offenders' camp and six California prisons between 1959 and 1969.<ref name="pln_2011_aug">{{Cite news |first=Paul |last=Wright |date=August 2011 |title=Prison Legal News Interviews Former Prisoner and Famous Actor Danny Trejo |newspaper=[[Prison Legal News]] |url=https://www.prisonlegalnews.org/23634_displayArticle.aspx }}</ref><ref name="pln_dbsearch">{{Cite news |date= |title=article listing for Danny Trejo |newspaper=[[Prison Legal News]] |url=https://www.prisonlegalnews.org/Search.aspx?type=articles&term=trejo }}</ref> He recalled that his last prison term was five years. While serving in [[San Quentin Prison]], he became a champion boxer in that prison's lightweight and welterweight divisions.<ref name = "bowles"/> During this time, Trejo became a member of a [[twelve-step program]], which he credits with his success in overcoming drug addiction. In 2011, he recalled that he had been sober for 42 years.<ref name = "pln_2011_aug"/>

Upon his final release, Trejo enrolled in [[Pitzer College]] located in [[Claremont, California]] but subsequently left after one semester of attendance.

==Career==

===Films and television===
While Trejo was working as a youth drug counselor, a teenage patient asked for his assistance dealing with cocaine problems on the set of ''[[Runaway Train (film)|Runaway Train]]'' (1985).<ref name = "bowles"/><ref name="guardian-marks">{{cite news| url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/film/2012/dec/06/danny-trejo-hole-gas-chamber | work=The Guardian | title=Danny Trejo: 'I went to the hole looking at three gas-chamber offences' | first=Lisa| last=Marks | date=6 December 2012}}</ref> While there, Trejo was offered a job as an extra in the film's prison scenes. [[Edward Bunker]], himself a former convict and well-respected crime author who was writing the screenplay for the film, recognized Trejo, with whom he had done time at [[San Quentin]].<ref name = "bowles"/><ref name="guardian-marks"/> Bunker, remembering Trejo's boxing skills, offered him $320 per day to train [[Eric Roberts]], one of the movie's stars, for a boxing scene. Director [[Andrei Konchalovsky]] liked Trejo's work and decided to offer him a small role in the film as a boxer. It has been widely misreported that Trejo was actually serving time in the prison during filming, when in fact he had been released over a decade earlier.{{citation needed|date=October 2014}}

Since then, Trejo has become a prolific actor in both films and television, appearing alongside some of [[Hollywood]]'s most famous actors, including [[Antonio Banderas]], [[Charles Bronson]], [[Nicolas Cage]], [[George Clooney]], [[Robert De Niro]], [[Johnny Depp]], [[Harrison Ford]], [[Val Kilmer]], [[John Malkovich]],  and [[Al Pacino]], and [[Jean-Claude Van Damme]]. Trejo has often appeared in five or more movies per year, in various genres. He has appeared in such major releases as ''[[Marked for Death]]'' (1990), ''[[Blood In Blood Out]]'' (1993), ''[[Desperado (film)|Desperado]]'' (1995), ''[[Heat (1995 film)|Heat]]'' (1995), ''[[From Dusk till Dawn]]'' (1996), ''[[Anaconda (film)|Anaconda]]'' (1997), ''[[Con Air]]'' (1997), ''[[Six Days Seven Nights]]'' (1998), '' [[The Replacement Killers]]'' (1998), [[Point Blank (1998 film)|Point Blank]] (1998), '''[[Animal Factory]]'' (2000), ''[[Bubble Boy (film)|Bubble Boy]]'' (2001), ''[[Spy Kids|SPY kids]]'' (2001), ''[[xXx (film)|xXx]]'' (2002), ''[[Once Upon a Time in Mexico]]'' (2003), ''[[Anchorman: The Legend of Ron Burgundy]]'' (2004), ''[[The Devil's Rejects]]'' (2005), ''[[Snoop Dogg's Hood of Horror]]'' (2006), ''[[Delta Farce]]'' (2007), ''[[Grindhouse (film)|Grindhouse]]'' (2007), ''[[Halloween (2007 film)|Rob Zombie's Halloween]]'' (2007), ''[[Urban Justice]]'' (2007, alongside [[Steven Seagal]]), ''[[Valley of Angels]]'' (2008), ''[[The Haunted World of El Superbeasto]]'' (2009, in which he voice the character of Rico), ''[[Machete (film)|Machete]]'' (2010), ''[[Predators (film)|Predators]]'' (2010), and ''[[Bad Ass (film)|Bad Ass]]'' (2012).

[[File:Danny Trejo 2.jpg|left|thumb|175px|Trejo at [[Muscle Beach]].]]
Trejo is known for his distinctive appearance. In addition to his heavily lined face, scarred from [[Acne vulgaris|cystic acne]] and a brawl, and the long mustache he usually sports, he has displayed the large tattoo on his chest for many roles (the tattoo depicts a woman wearing a [[sombrero]]).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theguardian.com/film/2012/dec/06/danny-trejo-hole-gas-chamber|title=Danny Trejo: "I went to the hole looking at three gas-chamber offences"|first=Lisa|last=Marks|work=The Guardian|date=7 December 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.urbanink.com/view-article.php?serial=329|title=Danny Trejo - Hollywood Badass|first=Ben|last=Westhoff|publisher=Urban Ink}}</ref> He often plays thugs or lowlife characters, although he has also played sympathetic characters in films such as [[Robert Rodriguez]]'s ''[[Spy Kids]]'' trilogy, ''[[Bubble Boy (film)|Bubble Boy]]'', and the [[USA Network]] series ''[[Monk (TV series)|Monk]]'', where art imitated life in his portrayal of a convict roommate to [[Tony Shalhoub]]'s undercover detective.{{citation needed|date=October 2014}} He also appeared in a Season 2 episode of ''[[Desperate Housewives]]'', where, as another good-guy character, he was sent by Carlos to help Gabrielle deal with her grief of losing her unborn baby.{{citation needed|date=October 2014}} He voiced the character Enrique on ''[[King of the Hill]]'',{{citation needed|date=October 2014}} and Octavio's character on that show is based on his appearance.{{citation needed|date=October 2014}} His role on ''[[King of the Hill]]'' was referred to in the HBO series ''[[Unscripted]]'', where he had a cameo appearance.{{citation needed|date=October 2014}}  He also is featured in an episode of ''[[Tim and Eric Awesome Show, Great Job!]]''{{citation needed|date=October 2014}} and an episode of ''Do IT LIVE'' with actors Anna Bennett and Claudia Pickering, shot in Downtown Los Angeles, titled "Sebring feat. Danny Trejo".{{citation needed|date=October 2014}}{{citation needed|date=October 2014}} He also made guest appearances, as "Tortuga''"'' [The Tortoise], in the award-winning series ''[[Breaking Bad]]'' in 2009 and 2010, most notably in the episode "[[Negro y Azul]]" (19 April 2009).

In 2008 Trejo portrayed the Mexican bartender in the daytime soap opera ''[[The Young and the Restless]]''.

Regarding his continued growth as a professional actor, Trejo has stated that he still cannot believe how fortunate he has been: "I'm so blessed. I'm still scared that somebody's going to wake me up and say, 'Hey, we're still in prison. Let's go to chow".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.craveonline.com/film/interviews/157270-danny-trejo-the-illustrated-man|title=Danny Trejo: The illustrated man}}</ref>
Many of Trejo's characters in [[Robert Rodriguez]] movies have been named after knives or sharp instruments: Machete in ''[[Spy Kids]]'', Razor Charlie in the ''[[From Dusk till Dawn]]'' series, Navajas (Spanish for blades) in ''[[Desperado (film)|Desperado]]'', and Cuchillo (Spanish for knife) in ''[[Predators (film)|Predators]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bloody-disgusting.com/news/19992|title=Meet Danny Trejo as the Badass Cuchillo in New 'Predators' Character Profile}}</ref> He also played Machete in a trailer made for Rodriguez's film collaboration with [[Quentin Tarantino]], ''[[Grindhouse (film)|Grindhouse]]''. In 2010, he starred in a full theatrical version of ''[[Machete (film)|Machete]]'', based on the character, followed by a second ''[[Machete Kills]]'' in 2013.

In 2012, Trejo starred alongside [[Ron Perlman]] and [[Charles S. Dutton]] in Craig Moss’s action film ''[[Bad Ass (film)|Bad Ass]]'' (2012). He played the main character of [[Vietnam veteran]] Frank Vega, based on 67-year-old “Epic Beard Man” [[AC Transit Bus fight|Thomas Bruso]]. That same year Trejo again appeared with Perlman in a supporting role in season 4 of the FX network drama ''[[Sons of Anarchy]]''.

Danny Trejo produced his first film, ''Ambition'', in 2014, it was a family film. He will produce an action film ''Bad Ass'' in 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://movie.info/danny-trejo |title=Danny Trejo }}</ref>{{clarify|date=February 2015|reason=is this a separate movie from craig moss's film?}} In 2015, Trejo appeared in a television commercial for [[Snickers]] that aired during [[Super Bowl XLIX]], in which he portrayed [[Marcia Brady]] prior to eating the candy bar.<ref>[http://blogs.wsj.com/cmo/2015/01/29/adwatch-snickers-super-bowl-ad-brings-out-different-side-of-marcia-brady/ AdWatch: Snickers Super Bowl Ad Brings Out Different Side of Marcia Brady]</ref><ref>[http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/style-blog/wp/2015/02/01/the-snickers-brady-bunch-themed-super-bowl-2015-commercial-starring-danny-trejo-and-steve-buscemi/ The Snickers ‘Brady Bunch’-themed Super Bowl 2015 commercial starring Danny Trejo and Steve Buscemi]</ref>

===Biopics===
The award-winning independent film ''Champion'' (2005), which documents Trejo's life, features [[Dennis Hopper]], [[Val Kilmer]], [[Steve Buscemi]], and [[Robert Rodriguez]].{{citation needed|date=October 2014}} In 2013, Trejo shared his tumultuous journey from convict to movie star with [[KTTV]] in Los Angeles in a segment filmed in his own home.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.myfoxla.com/story/23417092/casa-de-machete-a-chat-with-danny-trejo|title=Casa de Machete|publisher=KTTV|accessdate=12 September 2013}}</ref>

===Games===
Trejo appears as a [[player character|playable character]] in ''[[Def Jam: Fight for NY]]'' (2004), a [[versus fighting game|fighting game]] to which the actor lent his voice and likeness. He also lent his voice to ''[[Grand Theft Auto: Vice City]]'' (2002) and ''[[Grand Theft Auto: Vice City Stories]]'' (2006) for the character Umberto Robina, who also resembles Trejo.{{citation needed|date=October 2014}} Additionally, he voiced Raul Tejada, a Ghoul, in ''[[Fallout: New Vegas]]'' (2010).  Trejo appeared in the [[PlayStation Move]] game ''[[The Fight: Lights Out]]'' (2010) as an instructor for the player's character, and as himself in the second map pack for ''[[Call of Duty: Black Ops]]'' (2010); Escalation map pack, on the zombie map ''Call of the Dead''.

==Music videos==
Trejo has made cameo appearances in music videos, such as "[[Got It Twisted]]" by [[Mobb Deep]] (2004); [[Sepultura]]'s video "Attitude" (1996); [[Jay Chou]]'s short movie-music video ''Double Blade'' from his 2003 album ''[[Ye Hui Mei]]'' (2003); Rehab's song "Bartender" (2007); the [[Jonas Brothers]]' song "[[Burnin' Up (Jonas Brothers song)|Burnin' Up]]" from their album ''[[A Little Bit Longer]]'' (2008); rapper [[Tech N9ne]]'s video for the single "Like Yeah" from his release ''[[Killer (Tech N9ne album)|Killer]]'' (2008); southern metalcore band [[Maylene and the Sons of Disaster]]'s video for the single "Open Your Eyes" from their release ''[[IV (Maylene and the Sons of Disaster album)|IV]]'' (2011); and [[Yelawolf]]'s video "Whistle Dixie" (2012). He also appeared in famous adult entertainer [[Lupe Fuentes]]' music video "We Are the Party" (2012) with her band The Ex-Girlfriends.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theexgirlfriends.com/about.html|title=About The Ex-Girlfriends|publisher=The Ex-Girlfriends|accessdate=14 December 2012}}</ref>  On July 15, 2014, Trejo featured as his [[Robert Rodriguez]] character [[Machete (character)|Machete]] in the music video of "Angel In Blue Jeans", which additionally featured Canadian television hostess and actor [[Hannah Simone]].

==In popular culture==

===Comics===
In the comic series ''[[DMZ (comics)|DMZ]]'' a character named Danny with the same appearance as Danny Trejo appears; the character indicates that he was formerly a convicted felon, as well.

===Games===
Trejo is featured in the hip-hop fighting game ''[[Def Jam: Fight for NY]]'' (2004) as a soldier on Crow's side. He can be seen in the intro of Greg Hastings' Tournament Paintball Max'd ("Play for Real", B-Real & DJ Lethal),{{citation needed|date=October 2014}}. He is featured in the downloadable content pack called Escalation Map Pack in the video game ''[[Call of Duty: Black Ops]]'' (2010), on the map "Call of the Dead" as himself.  He provided the voice of Non-player character Raul Alfonso Tejada in ''[[Fallout: New Vegas]]''.

===Literature===
In [[Charlie Higson]]'s third ''The Enemy'' series novel, ''[[The Fear (Higson novel)|The Fear]]'' (2011), Orlando Epstein, nicknamed Einstein, makes fun of DogNut's ignorance by introducing himself as [[Isaac Newton]]. In retaliation, DogNut says, "my real name's Danny Trejo, but you may call me DogNut. Everyone else does." When Einstein responds, "Okay, Danny", DogNut reflects: "He may not know much about scientists, but Einstein clearly didn't know much about hard-faced, ex-con Mexican action-movie stars."<ref>{{cite book|title=The Fear|date=2011|author=Higson, Charlie|page=Chapter 43, page 7}}</ref>

===Music===
[[Plastilina Mosh]], a [[Mexican people|Mexican]] alternative rock band, paid tribute to Trejo with their song "Danny Trejo", featured in their album ''[[All U Need Is Mosh]]'' (2008).
