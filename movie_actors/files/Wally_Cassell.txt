'''Oswaldo Castellano''' (March 3, 1912 &ndash; April 2, 2015), better known as '''Wally Cassell''' was an American character actor. 

== Biography ==
Castellano was born in [[Agrigento]], [[Sicily]], and moved with his family to the United States when he was two years of age. He began his film career in 1942, initially appearing in small, uncredited roles. [[Mickey Rooney]], with whom Cassell appeared in the film noir ''[[Quicksand (1950 film)|Quicksand]]'' (1950), is credited with suggesting the change of name to Wally Cassell.<ref name="auto">[http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/wally-cassell-dead-white-heat-798730 Wally Cassell, Gangster in 'White Heat,' Dies at 103], Hollywood Reporter, May 28, 2015</ref>

His films include ''[[The Thin Man Goes Home]]'' (1945), ''[[The Story of G.I. Joe]]'' (1945), ''[[The Clock (1945 film)|The Clock]]'' (1945), ''[[The Postman Always Rings Twice (1946 film)|The Postman Always Rings Twice]]'' (1946), ''[[The Guilty (1947 film)|Guilty]]'' (1947), ''Loves of Carmen'' (1948), ''[[Saigon (1948 film)|Saigon]]'' (1948), ''[[Sands of Iwo Jima]]'' (1949), ''[[White Heat]]'' (1949),''[[Quicksand (1950 film)|Quicksand]]'' (1950), ''[[City That Never Sleeps]]'' (1953), ''[[Island in the Sky (1953 film)|Island in the Sky]]'' (1953), ''[[Law and Order (1953 film)|Law and Order]]'' (1953), ''[[Princess of the Nile]]'' (1954), ''[[Until They Sail]]'' (1957), and ''[[I, Madman]]'' (1989). 

He later appeared in two [[Broadcast syndication|syndicated]] programs starring [[Jim Davis (actor)|Jim Davis]]: ''[[Stories of the Century]]'', in the role of gunman [[Luke Short]], and ''[[Rescue 8]]'', as Johnny French in "One More Step".  Cassell also guest starred in several television series, including ''[[The Loretta Young Show]]'' (1955), ''[[Gunsmoke]]'' (1956), the 1959 premier episode of ''[[The Untouchables (1959 TV series)|The Untouchables]]'' "The Empty Chair",  ''[[Rawhide (TV series)|Rawhide]]'' (1960), and ''[[The Beverly Hillbillies]]'' (1963). 

He retired from acting in 1964 and became a successful businessman. He was married to actress [[Marcy McGuire]] from 1947 until his death.<ref name="McGuire">{{cite web | url=http://data.desmoinesregister.com/dmr/famous-iowans/MarcyMcGuire | title=Marcy McGuire | publisher=Des Moines Register | accessdate=May 2, 2014}}</ref>

Cassell died aged 103 at his home in [[Palm Desert, California]] on April 2, 2015.<ref name="auto"/>
