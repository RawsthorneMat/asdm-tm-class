'''Hugh Michael Horace Dancy'''<ref name=pehl1>{{cite web|url=http://www.pellew.com/Family%20Tree/Anderson%20Rev%20J%20R.htm|title=Rev. James Richard Anderson profile at|publisher=The Hon. Peter I. Pellew official website|accessdate=19 December 2012}}</ref> (born 19 June 1975)<ref name=pehl1/> is an English actor and model. He is best known for his roles as [[Will Graham]] in the television series ''[[Hannibal (TV series)|Hannibal]]'' and as [[Prince Charmont]] in ''[[Ella Enchanted (film)|Ella Enchanted]]''.
In 2006, he was nominated for a [[Primetime Emmy Award]] for his portrayal of [[Earl of Essex]] in the [[HBO]] miniseries ''[[Elizabeth I (miniseries)|Elizabeth I]]''.

==Early life==
Dancy was born in [[Stoke-on-Trent]], [[Staffordshire]],<ref name=independent>{{cite news|url=http://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/films/features/how-hugh-dancy-became-a-hollywood-hero-464476.html|title=How Hugh Dancy became a Hollywood hero|work=[[The Independent]]|date=20 September 2007|accessdate=10 April 2010|location=London, UK|archivedate=11 May 2010|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20100511074348/http://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/films/features/how-hugh-dancy-became-a-hollywood-hero-464476.html|deadurl=no}}</ref> and raised in [[Newcastle-under-Lyme]].<ref name=telegraph2012>{{cite news|url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/9525455/Hugh-Dancy-I-dont-need-to-be-stinking-rich.html|title=Hugh Dancy: 'I don't need to be stinking rich'|work=[[The Daily Telegraph|The Telegraph]]|location=UK|date=10 September 2012|accessdate=19 December 2012}}</ref> His mother, Sarah Ann (née Birley),<ref name=pehl1/> works in academic publishing.<ref name=telegraph2012/> His father is [[philosophy]] professor [[Jonathan Dancy]], who teaches at the [[University of Reading]] and at the [[University of Texas at Austin]].<ref name=pehl1/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://web.mac.com/jonathandancy/Site/Full_cv.html|title=Jonathan Dancy C.V.|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20100406203619/http://web.mac.com/jonathandancy/Site/Full_cv.html|archivedate=6 April 2010}}</ref>

Hugh is the oldest of three children, followed by brother Jack Dancy (born 25 May 1977), who is co-director of the travel company Trufflepig Travel,<ref name=pehl1/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.trufflepig.com|title=Trufflepig Travel Inc.|publisher=Trufflepig.com|accessdate=10 April 2010}}</ref> and sister Katharine Sarah Redman (born 5 March 1980). From the age of 5 to the age of 10, Dancy was educated at Edenhurst Preparatory School<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.edenhurst.co.uk|title=Edenhurst Preparatory School}}</ref> in Newcastle-under-Lyme. At age 10, Dancy attended boarding school at the [[Dragon School]] in [[Oxford]], then at 13<ref name=independent/> at [[Winchester College]].<ref name=telegraph2012/> At age 18, he acted in the Winchester College Players production of ''[[Twelfth Night]]'' which was performed in both [[Winchester]] and at the [[Minack Theatre]] in [[Cornwall]]. He went on to study English under poet and playwright Francis Warner at [[St Peter's College, Oxford]].<ref name=independent/>

==Career==
After [[graduation]], Dancy moved to [[London]], where a chance conversation in a cafe led to his meeting casting director Ros Hubbard and agent Dallas Smith, who signed him.<ref name=independent/> In 1999, Dancy played the role of Danny, who had a fleeting romance with Rachel, one of the show's main characters, in the second series of ''[[Cold Feet]]''. In 2004, he played Prince Char in the Disney film, [[Ella Enchanted (film)]] alongside [[Anne Hathaway]], [[Cary Elwes]], and [[Aidan McArdle]]. In 2007, Dancy had a starring role on Broadway as Captain Dennis Stanhope in ''[[Journey's End]]'' ([[Belasco Theatre]]). He played "Aiden" in the  2007 film ''[[Blood and Chocolate (film)|Blood and Chocolate]]''. He portrayed the title character, a man with [[Asperger syndrome]], in the 2009 independent film, ''[[Adam (2009 film)|Adam]]''.<!--spending time during pre-production with Aspergers groups and clinical specialists--> Dancy appeared in ''[[Savage Grace]]'', a film adaptation of the novel of the same name by Natalie Robins and Steven M.L. Aronson.

Dancy appeared in [[MCC Theater]]'s ''The Pride'', written by Alexi Kaye Campbell, at the [[Lucille Lortel Theatre]] in [[New York City]]. The off-Broadway production was directed by [[Joe Mantello]] and co-starred [[Ben Whishaw]] and [[Andrea Riseborough]]. In 2009, he appeared in ''[[Confessions of a Shopaholic (film)|Confessions of a Shopaholic]]''. From 2010 until 2011, he starred in [[Manhattan Theatre Club]]'s [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] production of ''[[Venus in Fur]]''. His performance was praised by ''[[The New York Times]]'' theatre critic Charles Isherwood.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://theater.nytimes.com/2011/11/09/theater/reviews/venus-in-fur-by-david-ives-with-nina-arianda-review.html?pagewanted=all|work=[[The New York Times]]|first=Charles|last=Isherwood|title=‘Venus in Fur', by David Ives, With Nina Arianda|date=8 November 2011}}</ref> In March 2012, [[NBC]] announced that Dancy had been cast as [[Will Graham]] in ''[[Hannibal (TV series)|Hannibal]]'', the television adaptation based on the main character of [[Thomas Harris]]' 1981 novel, ''[[Red Dragon (novel)|Red Dragon]]''.<ref>Morgan, Jeffrey. [http://www.digitalspy.com/tv/news/a372800/hannibal-lecter-tv-series-casts-hugh-dancy-as-will-graham.html "Hannibal Lecter TV series casts Hugh Dancy as Will Graham"], digitalspy.com; accessed 23 March 2012.</ref>

==Personal life==

Dancy met American actress [[Claire Danes]] on the set of ''[[Evening (film)|Evening]]'' in [[Newport, Rhode Island]], and they began dating.<ref name=engaged/> In February 2009, they announced their engagement.<ref name=engaged>{{cite news|last=Zuckerman|first=Blaine|url=http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,20257623,00.html|title=Claire Danes & Hugh Dancy Are Engaged!|work=[[People (magazine)|People]]|date=6 February 2009|accessdate=10 April 2010}}</ref> The couple married in France in 2009, privately. They have one child.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,20308634,00.html|title=Claire Danes and Hugh Dancy Wed|date=28 September 2009|work=[[People (magazine)|People]]|quote=...tied the knot in a quiet ceremony in France a few weeks ago...}}</ref><ref>{{cite video|title=Craig Ferguson 6/11/13E Late Late Show Hugh Dancy XD|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sqSUVRdaZT0|accessdate=8 April 2014}}</ref>
