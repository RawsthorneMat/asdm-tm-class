'''Mark Andrew Consuelos''' (born March 30, 1971) is a [[Spain|Spanish]]-born [[Americans|American]] television and film actor.

==Personal life==
Consuelos<ref name="nbcbio">{{cite news | title = Mark Consuelos |work=NBC | url = http://www.nbc.com/Age_of_Love/bios/bio_host.shtml | accessdate = 2007-09-10 }}</ref> was born in Zaragoza, Spain, to an [[Italian people|Italian]] mother, Camilla, and a [[Mexican people|Mexican]] father, Saul Consuelos.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/sptimes/access/22788377.html?dids=22788377:22788377&FMT=ABS&FMTS=ABS:FT&type=current&date=Feb+25%2C+1996&author=ACE+ATKINS&pub=St.+Petersburg+Times&desc=TV+role+brings+dream+to+life&pqatl=google | work=St. Petersburg Times | title=TV role brings dream to life | first=Ace | last=Atkins | date=February 25, 1996}}</ref><ref>http://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/mark-consuelos/bio/192015</ref> He is the youngest of three children, and has a brother, who is a doctor, and a sister, who is a lawyer. Consuelos has lived in [[Italy]] and the United States since childhood.  He grew up in [[Lebanon, Illinois]], and later in [[Tampa, Florida]].<ref name="nbcbio">{{cite news | title = Mark Consuelos |work=NBC | url = http://www.nbc.com/Age_of_Love/bios/bio_host.shtml | accessdate = 2007-09-10 }}</ref> He attended [[Bloomingdale High School]] in [[Valrico, Florida]], and then went to [[University of Notre Dame]] before transferring to and graduating from the [[University of South Florida]] with a degree in marketing in 1994.<ref name="abcbio">{{cite news | title = Mark Consuelos|work=ABC | url = http://www.wchstv.com/abc/allmychildren/markconsuelos.shtml | accessdate = 2007-09-10 }}</ref> During his time at the University of South Florida he worked as a male stripper to pay for his education.

In 1995, Consuelos met [[Kelly Ripa]], his co-star on ''[[All My Children]]''. The two eloped on May 1, 1996. The couple has three children: Michael Joseph (born June 2, 1997), Lola Grace (born June 16, 2001), and Joaquin Antonio (born February 24, 2003).

==Career==
Consuelos had a starring role in the educational serial ''[[Connect With English]]'', which aired on public television stations as part of the [[Annenberg/CPB Project]].{{Citation needed|date=December 2009}}

In February 1995, Consuelos had his breakthrough when he landed the role of [[Mateo Santos]] on the soap opera ''[[All My Children]]''.<ref name="nbcbio"/> There, he fell in love with his on-screen [[love interest]] [[Kelly Ripa]], and secretly married her in [[Las Vegas, Nevada|Las Vegas]], in May 1996. At the time, he lived on co-star [[Winsor Harmon]]'s sofa; Harmon was one of the few to know that co-stars Ripa and Consuelos were not only dating, but married.

Ripa and Consuelos continued to tape episodes of ''All My Children'' until 2002, when Ripa wished to focus more on her other job: taking over for [[Kathie Lee Gifford]] as host of what then became  ''[[Live with Regis and Kelly|Live! with Regis and Kelly]]'' then, ''[[Live! with Kelly]]'' and now, subsequently ''[[Live! with Kelly and Michael]]''. Consuelos is a regular guest co-host.

Since leaving ''All My Children'', Consuelos has starred in the feature film ''[[The Great Raid]]'', which debuted in theatres in 2005. In 2006, he appeared in the movies ''[[My Super Ex-Girlfriend]]'', as Steve, and ''[[Wedding Daze]]''.<ref name="nbcbio"/> He has been seen on the [[Lifetime Television|Lifetime]] series ''[[1-800-Missing|Missing]]'', with [[Vivica A. Fox]].<ref name="nbcbio"/> He also had a part in [[Ugly Betty]].

Consuelos hosted two reality dating shows, ''[[Age of Love (TV series)|Age of Love]]''<ref name="nbcbio"/> and ''[[Science of Love]]'', both airing on [[NBC]] in 2007. He guest-starred on ''[[Third Watch]]'', ''[[Friends]]'', ''American Family,'' ''Fortune Hunter'', ''[[SeaQuest]]'' and ''[[Hope & Faith]]''.<ref name="nbcbio"/>

On October 3, 2008 he performed the marriage ceremony for [[Howard Stern]] and model/actress [[Beth Ostrosky]] at the Le Cirque restaurant in New York City. The two couples had grown close, which was why Stern asked Consuelos to perform the wedding ceremony. Consuelos agreed, and took it upon himself to seek [[ordination]] to make it an official ceremony for Stern and Ostrosky. The guest list for the wedding included [[Joan Rivers]], [[Barbara Walters]], [[Billy Joel]] and wife [[Katie Lee (food critic)|Katie Lee]], [[Jimmy Kimmel]], [[Sarah Silverman]], and Stern's radio co-host [[Robin Quivers]].

A weekly live episode of the "Oprah Winfrey Show" premiered in the show's 23rd season with a panel consisting of Mark Consuelos, Ali Wentworth, Oprah Winfrey, and Gayle King. The panel discussed the week's news and highlighted events in the media and on the show.
In the 2009–10 season, Winfrey hosted this segment on her own. Fridays Live did not return for the show's 25th season.

In 2010 Mark Consuelos attended the ''[[Cop Out (2010 film)|Cop Out]]'' premiere in New York where he had a pleasant conversation with [[Bryan Johnson (filmmaker)|Bryan Johnson]] in view of a spying [[Walt Flanagan]].<ref>Tell 'Em Steve-Dave! Podcast 4: Keeping Up With Consuelos</ref> In 2012 Consuelos guest-starred on an episode of ''[[Law & Order: Special Victims Unit]]'' titled "[[Law & Order: Special Victims Unit (season 13)#ep17|Justice Denied]]". He played Michael, the prime suspect in a brutal rape during [[Fleet Week]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Rizzo|first=Carita|title=Law & Order: SVU: Mariska Will Be Back, A Tense New Case and More!|url=http://www.tvguide.com/News/Law-Order-SVU-Mariska-Hargitay-1044463.aspx|accessdate=March 5, 2012|newspaper=TV Guide|date=March 5, 2012}}</ref> Consuelos is not new to the [[Law & Order (franchise)|''Law & Order'' franchise]], he had guest-starred in the sixth season episode "[[Albatross (Law & Order: Criminal Intent)|Albatross]]" of ''[[Law & Order: Criminal Intent]]'' as the U.S. Attorney involved in a case where the judge's life is threatened.

Consuelos portrayed Spivey, a recurring character, in ''[[American Horror Story (season 2)|American Horror Story: Asylum]]'', the second season of the anthology horror series.<ref>http://insidetv.ew.com/2012/07/16/american-horror-story-mark-consuelos/</ref>

In 2013, Consuelos co-starred as Florida Senator Andy Guzman in Amazon's ''[[Alpha House]]'', a political comedy written by ''[[Doonesbury]]'' creator [[Garry Trudeau]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Goodman|first=Tim|title= Alpha House: TV Review|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/review/alpha-house-tv-review-656034|accessdate=24 July 2014|newspaper=The Hollywood Reporter|date=14 November 2013}}</ref> Along with [[John Goodman]], Consuelos plays one of four Republican senators living together in a house on Capitol Hill. He has spent the summer of 2014 filming the second season.

==Awards and honors==
In 1998 and 1999, Consuelos won the American Latino Media Arts Award for "Outstanding Actor in a Daytime Soap Opera." Consuelos won "Hottest Romance" along with Ripa in 1997 at the Soap Opera Awards.<ref name="abcbio"/>
