'''Aaron Kwok Fu-shing''' (born 26 October 1965) is a [[Hong Kong people|Hong Kong]] singer, dancer, and actor. He has been active since the 1980s and to the present. He is considered one of the "[[Four Heavenly Kings (Hong Kong)|Four Heavenly Kings]]" of Hong Kong.<ref name="X1">Xinhuanet. "[http://news.xinhuanet.com/ent/2006-02/18/content_4195962.htm Xinhuanet]." ''四大天王 蓦然回首十五年.'' Retrieved on 12 April 2009.</ref> Kwok's onstage dancing and displays is influenced by [[Michael Jackson]].<ref name="hkhead1">HKheadline.com. "[http://www.hkheadline.com/ent/ent_content.asp?track=news&contid=81683&srctype=n HKheadline.com]." ''Moonwalk成經典 米高辣舞歌手跟跳.'' Retrieved on 28 June 2009.</ref> While most of his songs are in the [[dance-pop]] genre, he has experimented numerous times with [[rock and roll]], [[ballad]], [[rock music|rock]], [[Rhythm and blues|R&B]], [[soul music|soul]], [[electronica]] and [[List of traditional Chinese musical instruments|traditional Chinese music]].

Kwok earned 130 million HKD in 2014.<ref>{{cite web|title=Louis Koo is 2014's highest-earning celebrity|url=https://sg.entertainment.yahoo.com/news/louis-koo-2014s-highest-earning-celebrity-084700642.html|publisher=Yahoo! News Asia|date=29 December 2014|accessdate=29 December 2014}}</ref>

==Biography==
Kwok graduated from [[St. Joseph's College, Hong Kong|St. Joseph's College]] in Hong Kong.<ref name="ming1" /> After graduating from secondary school, Kwok worked as a junior staff in [[King Fook Holdings|King Fook Gold & Jewellery Co. Ltd]]. His father, who owns a small gold retail store, desired that he gain experience in the business with the view of eventually handing the family business over to him. If not for one of Kwok's brothers taking over the gold business, his father would not have allowed him to join the entertainment industry.<ref name="sina1">Sina.com. "[http://ent.sina.com.cn/s/h/p/2007-10-25/09461763193.shtml Sina.com]." ''郭富城现阶段爱情排末尾 没有结婚念头.'' Retrieved on 28 June 2009.</ref>

==Career==

===Early years===
In 1984 at the age of 19, Kwok joined a dancer training course at [[Television Broadcasts Limited|TVB]], where his talent for dancing was immediately recognised.<ref name="yahoo1">Yahoo.com. "[http://ent.cn.yahoo.com/070301/322/28zkc.html Yahoo.com]." ''郭富城简介.'' Retrieved on 28 June 2009.</ref> Kwok then performed in music videos and variety shows for other singers. In 1987 he was transferred to the acting department of the talent training course and became a TV actor, where he played minor parts in TVB dramas.<ref name="yahoo1" /> In 1990 he did a TV commercial in [[Taiwan]] for the [[Honda]] motorcycle [[Honda Elite|DJ-1RR]].<ref>Youtube.com. "[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B8mt0w8CfF0 Youtube.com]." ''Aaron Kwok commercial.'' Retrieved on 28 June 2009.</ref> The commercial gained him instant popularity with Taiwanese girls, and he immediately burst onto the music scene.<ref name="yahoo1" />

===Music===
{{See also|Aaron Kwok discography}}
Kwok then began his music career with three [[mandopop]] albums including the famous song "Loving You Forever" (對你愛不完) to accompany his dance moves.<ref name="yahoo1" /> After his success in Taiwan, he returned to Hong Kong in 1991 to do [[Cantopop]].<ref name="yahoo1" /> The next few years saw his popularity reach fever-pitch, and he was soon ranked as one of the "[[Four Heavenly Kings (Hong Kong)|Four Heavenly Kings]]".<ref name="yahoo1" /> Kwok became one of Hong Kong and Asia's most prominent pop stars. He won his first major awards with the [[1991 Jade Solid Gold Top 10 Awards]] and [[1991 RTHK Top 10 Gold Songs Awards]].<ref>Tvb.com. "[http://tvcity.tvb.com/jsg/awards/awards_4957574916822816576188250182181.html Tvb.com]." ''1991 additional awards.'' Retrieved on 28 June 2009.</ref><ref>RTHK. "[http://www.rthk.org.hk/classicschannel/goldsong14.htm# RTHK]." ''1991 RTHK awards.'' Retrieved on 28 June 2009.</ref> He would then win a major award every year until 2001.<ref>RTHK. "[http://www.rthk.org.hk/classicschannel/goldsong24.htm# RTHK]." ''Follow the links from 1991 to 2001 RTHK awards.'' Retrieved on 28 June 2009.</ref> As a solo performer, his sell-out concerts in Hong Kong, [[mainland China]], United States, Canada, Singapore, [[Malaysia]] and other countries total over 200 to date.<ref name="long1">Longines.com. "[www.longines.com%2Fdocuments%2Fbiography%2Faaron-kwok-fu-shing-en.pdf Longines.com]." ''Aaron Kwok profile for promotion.'' Retrieved on 28 June 2009.</ref> In 1999 he won his first "Asia Pacific most popular artist" award.<ref>TVB.com. "[http://tvcity.tvb.com/jsg/1999final/1999final_content2.html TVB.com]." ''1999 additional rewards.'' Retrieved on 28 June 2009.</ref>
Aaron Kwok will begin his 2011 tour on 16 April and his first stop is in Shanghai.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://asiapacificarts.usc.edu/article@apa?aaron_kwoks_2011_tour_begins_on_april_16_16636.aspx | title = Aaron Kwok's 2011 tour begins on April 16 | publisher = Asia Pacific Arts | date = 13 April 2011}}</ref> Aaron Kwok has also created the choreography with Sunny Wong for the group [[As One (Chinese Band)|As One]].

[[Janet Jackson]] collaborated with Aaron Kwok and [[Ricky Martin]] for international versions of "[[Ask for More]]", a promotional single and commercial released as part of an advertising campaign for [[Pepsi]]. A full length music video of the version with Kwok was also released in Asian markets.<ref>[http://english.cri.cn/4406/2007/04/23/47@219380.htm Chinese Pop Music History 20 Years Special – Aaron Kwok]<!-- Bot generated title --></ref><ref>[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Lp9BANVUaiE Ask for More (with Aaron Kwok)]<!-- Bot generated title --></ref>

===Dancer===
As soon as Kwok entered the music industry in 1991, he started a fast-dancing trend (勁歌熱舞).<ref name="yahoo1" /> Kwok's onstage dancing and displays has been known to be influenced by [[Michael Jackson]].<ref name="hkhead1" /> Later in his career, he is known to have won a prestigious "Top ten Hong Kong dance award" (十大舞蹈家年獎).<ref>Swiss Privilege. "[http://www.swiss-privilege.com/public/clubsuisse/2008/15feb/big5/06_dancing.html Swiss-privilege]." ''Dancing Aaron Kwok rocked the stage at his de show Reel Live in Concert 2007.'' Retrieved on 28 June 2009.</ref> Of all the performing arts at which Kwok excels, stage appearances remain his perennial favourite.<ref name="long1" /> His dance accomplishments are also matched with stage displays. On 17 February 2008, he held an "Aaron Kwok De Show Reel Extension Live" concert at the Hong Kong [[AsiaWorld Arena]] with the largest revolving stage measured at 10m x 9.44m and created a new entry for the [[Guinness Book of World Records]].<ref name="long1" />

===Acting===
{{see also|Aaron Kwok filmography}}
Over the years, Kwok has also been active in other media such as TV commercials and acting.  He began his acting career with the TVB series ''Rise of Genghis Khan'', and the 1988 series ''[[Twilight of a Nation]]'' about the [[Taiping Rebellion]].<ref>Musicnationgroup.com. "[http://www.musicnationgroup.com/session05_aaron_kwok_archives.html Musicnationgroup.com]." ''Aaron Kwok awards and work list.'' Retrieved on 28 June 2009.</ref> One of his more noticeable role was for the [[List of TVB series (1996)|1996 TVB drama series]] ''[[Wars of Bribery]]'' where he plays an [[Independent Commission Against Corruption (Hong Kong)|ICAC]] special-agent with [[Athena Chu]].

He also starred in various movies. At the Taiwan's 42nd [[Golden Horse Film Festival and Awards|Golden Horse Awards]] ceremony on 13 November 2005, Kwok was the surprise winner of Best Leading Actor award for his role in the film ''[[Divergence (film)|Divergence]]''.<ref name="long1" /> It was Kwok's first Golden Horse nomination and beat veteran Hong Kong star [[Tony Leung Ka-fai]] to win the honour. He won the Best Actor Award again at the 43rd Golden Horse Awards on 24 November 2006 for his role in the film ''[[After This Our Exile]]''.<ref>[http://news.xinhuanet.com/english/2006-11/27/content_5396494.htm Staff (27 November 2006)] "Aaron Kwok crowned best actor again at Golden Horse Awards" ''China View''</ref> He became only the second actor in the history of the Golden Horse Awards to win the Best Actor Award consecutively. [[Jackie Chan]] first accomplished this back in the 1992-3.<ref>[[:zh:金馬獎]]</ref>

Along with [[Zhang Ziyi]], Kwok stars in an AIDS-themed film ''[[Love for Life]]'', which premiered on 10 May 2011.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://asiapacificarts.usc.edu/article@apa?aids-themed_film_starring_zhang_ziyi_and_aaron_kwok_will_open_on_may_10_16477.aspx | title = AIDS-themed film starring Zhang Ziyi and Aaron Kwok will open on May 10 | publisher = Asia Pacific Arts | date = 03/07/2011}}</ref>

===Style promoter===
Among the four heavenly kings in the 1990s, Kwok was generally known to have the best image.<ref name="daisina">Dailynews.sina.com. "[http://dailynews.sina.com/bg/ent/hktwstar/phoenixtv/20090826/2115613060.html Sina.com]." ''當年狂追的偶像髮型 郭富城林志穎引起髮型潮流.'' Retrieved on 2 April 2010.</ref> He was famous for popularising a new type of "center-split hair style" that widely imitated during the 1990s.<ref name="daisina" /> Throughout his career he did change his hair style numerous times including styles such as the five-five split or the four-six split.<ref name="daisina" />

===Community service===
Kwok has also taken part in charitable work. Most notable he received the "Ten Most Outstanding Young Persons Award" in 2003.<ref name="long1" />

===Car promoter, collector, amateur racer===
[[File:2010 Aaron Kwok Renault Clio race car.jpg|thumb|Aaron Kwok's 2010 Clio Cup China race car.]]
Kwok has been known as a collector of sports cars as well as an amateur of motor racing. He participated in a [[Ferrari F355]] race at the [[Macau Grand Prix]], a Formula Campus charity race. Other notable participations include the [[Porsche Carrera Cup Asia]], where he raced with a [[Porsche 911 GT3]].<ref>3weekly.com. "[http://www.3weekly.com/share/threeweekly/0174/ent/20030210ent0048/content.htm 3weekly.com]." ''郭富城樂壇失意車壇得意.'' Retrieved on 28 June 2009.</ref>

He is known as a car fanatic and has a large collection of notable cars.<ref name="auto1">Auto.cn. "[http://www.020auto.cn/Wenhua/Mingren/12117964926348.shtml Auto.cn]." ''郭富城和他的十二辆超级跑车.'' Retrieved on 28 June 2009.</ref> Some of his collection include the [[Audi R8|Audi R8 GT Spyder]], [[Ferrari]] [[Ferrari F50|F50]], [[Ferrari Testarossa|F512M]], [[Ferrari F355|F355 GTS]], [[Ferrari 360|F360 Modena]], [[Ferrari 599 GTB Fiorano]], [[Ferrari California]], [[Ferrari F430|Ferrari F430 Spider]], [[Lamborghini Diablo#Diablo SE30 and Diablo SE30 Jota|Lamborghini Diablo SE30]], [[Mercedes-Benz]] [[Mercedes-Benz SL-Class|SL600]], [[Mercedes-Benz CLK-Class|Mercedes CLK DTM AMG]], [[Porsche 911 Turbo]]. [[Porsche 911 GT3 RS|Porsche 911 GT3 RS mk2]]<ref name="mdb1">Mdbchina.net. "[http://www.mdbchina.net/singer/%E9%83%AD%E5%AF%8C%E5%9F%8E-2337/biography.html Mdbchina.net]." ''郭富城 档案简介.'' Retrieved on 28 June 2009.</ref> Other cars include the [[Enzo Ferrari (car)|Enzo Ferrari]], a Carbon version [[Pagani Zonda F]], [[Lamborghini Reventón|Lamborghini Reventon Roadster]], [[Lamborghini Murciélago]], [[Lamborghini Gallardo|Gallardo]], [[Lamborghini Aventador|Lamborghini Aventador 50th Anniversario Roadster]], [[Lamborghini Gallardo|Lamborghini Gallardo Superleggera]], [[Porsche 996 GT3]], [[Nissan GT-R]].<ref name="auto1" />
