'''Matthew Paige''' "'''Matt'''" '''Damon''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|d|eɪ|m|ən}}; born October 8, 1970)<ref>{{cite news|title=Monitor|newspaper=[[Entertainment Weekly]]|publisher=[[Time Inc.]]|date=Oct 12–19, 2012|issue=1228/1229|page=23}}</ref> is an American actor, screenwriter and producer. His career was launched with the success of his and [[Ben Affleck]]'s  [[Academy Award for Best Writing (Original Screenplay)|Academy Award]]-winning screenplay, ''[[Good Will Hunting]]'' (1997), for which he also received a number of Best Actor nominations. He is among [[Forbes]] magazine's most bankable stars<ref name="ForbesPayback" /> and one of the top-40 highest-grossing actors of all time.<ref name="The Numbers" />{{#tag:ref|In motion pictures that feature him either as a leading actor or as a supporting co-star, his films have grossed a total of $1.94<ref name="BOMlist">{{cite web|publisher=Box Office Mojo|title=Actors #1–50|url=http://www.boxofficemojo.com/people/?view=Actor&sort=sumgross&p=.htm|accessdate=April 5, 2009}}</ref> to $3.12 [[1,000,000,000 (number)|billion]]<ref name="The Numbers">{{cite web|publisher=The Numbers|title=All Time Top 100 Stars at the Domestic Box Office|url=http://www.the-numbers.com/people/records/| accessdate=October 10, 2014}}</ref> (based on counting his roles as strictly lead or including supporting roles, respectively) at the North American box office, placing him in the top forty grossing actors of all time.|group="nb"}} In addition to the [[List of awards and nominations received by Matt Damon|many awards and nominations Damon has received]], such as [[Academy Awards|Academy]], [[Golden Globe Award|Golden Globe]] and other industry awards, for his work in the film industry—in 2007, Damon received a star on the [[Hollywood Walk of Fame]] and was named the [[Sexiest Man Alive]] by ''[[People (magazine)|People]]'' magazine.

Damon has become known for his versatility,<ref name="NYT06"/> starring in commercially and critically successful films such as the rogue secret agent [[Jason Bourne]] in the first three installments of the [[Bourne (film series)|''Bourne'' series]], the youthful thief Linus Caldwell in the ''[[Ocean's Trilogy]]'', the anti-hero in ''[[The Talented Mr. Ripley (film)|The Talented Mr. Ripley]]'' (1999), a fallen angel in ''[[Dogma (film)|Dogma]]'' (1999), a morally divided young soldier in ''[[Saving Private Ryan]]'' (1998),<ref>{{cite web|title=Top 10 Matt Damon Movies| url=http://au.ign.com/articles/2014/01/30/top-10-matt-damon-movies?page=2|publisher=[[IGN]]|accessdate=February 23, 2015}}</ref> and a [[conjoined twin]] in ''[[Stuck on You (film)|Stuck on You]]'' (2003). He won further critical acclaim for his performances in dramas such as ''[[Syriana]]'' (2005) and ''[[The Good Shepherd (film)|The Good Shepherd]]'' (2006), as well as for his role as a villain in the crime drama ''[[The Departed]]'' (2006). Among his critically and commercially unsuccessful films are the romance western ''[[All the Pretty Horses (film)|All the Pretty Horses]]'' and the sports drama ''[[The Legend of Bagger Vance]]'', both made in 2000, the adventure fantasy film ''[[The Brothers Grimm (film)|The Brothers Grimm]]'' (2005) and the war thriller ''[[Green Zone (film)|Green Zone]]'' (2010).

Damon has also performed [[voice-over]] work and established several production companies. Damon has been actively involved in charitable work, including the [[ONE Campaign]], [[H2O Africa Foundation]], [[Feeding America]], and [[Water.org]]. With his wife, Luciana Bozán Barroso, Damon has three daughters and a stepdaughter from Barroso's prior marriage.

== Early life ==
Damon was born in [[Cambridge, Massachusetts]], the second son of Kent Telfer Damon (born 1942), a stockbroker, and Nancy Carlsson-Paige (born 1944), an early childhood education professor at [[Lesley University]].<ref name="KentReal">{{cite news|last=Luscombe|first=Belinda|publisher=''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]''|title=Matt Damon Acts Out|url=http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,36294-1,00.html|date=December 19, 1999|accessdate=April 5, 2009}}</ref><ref name="LiveCambridge">{{cite news|last=Givens|first=Ron|author2=Michele McPhee|publisher=''[[Daily News (New York)|Daily News]]''|title=Two Hollywood Prizefighters 'Hunting' for Stardom Pays Off for Matt Damon|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/archives/entertainment/1998/03/22/1998-03-22_two_hollywood_prizefighters_.html|date=March 22, 1998|accessdate=April 5, 2009}} {{Dead link|date=April 2012|bot=H3llBot}}</ref> His father is of [[Scottish people|Scottish]] and [[English people|English]] descent and his mother is of [[Finns|Finnish]] and [[Swedes|Swedish]] ancestry (his mother's family surname had been changed from the Finnish "Pajari" to "Paige").<ref>{{cite news | url=http://www.businessinsider.com/largest-ethnic-groups-in-america-2013-8 |title=The Largest Ancestry Groups in the United States | author=Liz O'Connor, Gus Lubin, and Dina Spector | date=August 13, 2013 | newspaper=Business Insider | accessdate = October 2, 2014 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news | url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/matt-damon/  |title=Matt Damon | newspaper=Huffington Post | accessdate = October 2, 2014 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hometownfocus.us/news/2014-05-16/Features/Matt_Damon_makes_headlines_in_Finland.html|title=Matt Damon makes headlines in Finland - www.hometownfocus.us - Hometown Focus - Virginia, Minnesota|work=hometownfocus.us}}</ref> Damon and his family moved to [[Newton, Massachusetts|Newton]] for two years. His parents divorced when he was two years old, and Damon and his brother returned with their mother to Cambridge,<ref name="LiveCambridge"/><ref name="ParentsDivorce">{{cite news|publisher=''[[The Independent]]''|title=Matt Damon: A true Hollywood player|url=http://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/profiles/matt-damon-a-true-hollywood-player-418808.html|date=October 4, 2006|accessdate=April 5, 2009 | location=London}}</ref> where they lived in a six-family communal house.<ref name="ParadeProfile03">{{cite news|last=Raider|first=Dotson|publisher=''[[Parade (magazine)|Parade]]''|title="My Goals Have Changed" (Actor Matt Damon)|url=http://www.parade.com/articles/editions/2003/edition_11-30-2003/featured_0|date=November 30, 2003|accessdate=April 21, 2010}}{{Dead link|date=July 2013}}</ref><ref name="NeutralNarrate">{{cite news|last=Crust|first=Kevin|publisher=''[[Los Angeles Times]]''|title='Howard Zinn: You Can't Be Neutral on a Moving Train,' 'Hair Show,' 'The Hillside Strangler,' 'The Dust Factory' and 'Stephen King's Riding the Bullet'|url=http://www.calendarlive.com/movies/reviews/cl-et-capsules15oct15,2,1749015.story|date=October 15, 2004|accessdate=April 5, 2009|archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20080604162332/http://www.calendarlive.com/movies/reviews/cl-et-capsules15oct15,2,1749015.story |archivedate = June 4, 2008|deadurl=yes}}</ref> His brother Kyle is now an accomplished sculptor and artist.<ref name="LiveCambridge"/><ref name="Animation">{{cite news|last=Ball|first=Ryan|publisher=''[[Animation Magazine]]''|title=Matt Damon Animated for Arthur|url=http://www.animationmagazine.net/article/7229|date=August 15, 2007|accessdate=October 10, 2014|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/6TEiBrhJy|archivedate=October 10, 2014}}</ref>

As a lonely adolescent, Damon has said that he didn't feel that he belonged.<ref name="ParadeProfile03"/> Due to his mother's "by the book"<ref name="ParadeProfile03"/> approach to child-rearing, he had a hard time defining a [[Self-concept|self identity]], and as a result gravitated to [[role-playing]].<ref name="ParadeProfile03"/> He attended Cambridge Alternative School (now Graham and Parks) and then [[Cambridge Rindge and Latin School]], where he was a good student,<ref name="WTprofile97"/> but had a "terrifying" first two years due to his short stature.<ref>{{cite news|last=|first=|publisher=''[[Entertainment Weekly]]''|title=Ripley Believe It or Not|url=http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,272093_2,00.html|date=December 17, 1999|accessdate=April 23, 2010}}</ref> Damon performed as an actor in several high school theater productions.<ref name="LiveCambridge"/> He credited his drama teacher, Gerry Speca, as an important artistic influence, even though [[Ben Affleck]],<ref name="WTprofile97" /> his good friend and schoolmate,<ref>{{cite web|author=Share on Facebook |url=http://www.aintitcool.com/node/351 |title=Interview with Matt Damon and Minnie Driver!!! - Ain't It Cool News: The best in movie, TV, DVD, and comic book news |publisher=Aintitcool.com |accessdate=2014-08-13}}</ref> got the "biggest roles and longest speeches".<ref name="WTprofile97">{{cite news|last=Arnold|first=Gary|publisher=''[[The Washington Times]]''|title=Boyhood friends are stars on the rise|date=December 26, 1997}}</ref>{{#tag:ref|Another neighbor of Damon's was [[historian]] and author [[Howard Zinn]],<ref name="UnholyAlliance">{{cite book|last=Horowitz|first=David|title=Unholy Alliance: Radical Islam And The American Left|publisher=Regnery Publishing|year=2004|location=|page=102|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=azE5qoXTgoAC&pg=PA102&lpg=PA102&dq=matt+damon+howard+zinn+neighbor&source=web&ots=L-uGfPq5A5&sig=NaQBpDX55Gn7hFB_DEn2i2cSepY#PPA102,M1|isbn=0-89526-076-X|accessdate=April 5, 2009}}</ref> whose biographical film ''You Can't Be Neutral on a Moving Train'' and audio version of ''[[A People's History of the United States]]'' Damon later narrated.<ref name="NeutralNarrate"/>|group="nb"}} Damon is Affleck's tenth cousin, once removed, through a common [[New England]] ancestor.<ref name="newengland">{{cite web|title=Genealogical Chart Knowlton Line of Ben Affleck and Matt Damon|publisher=[[New England Historic Genealogical Society]]|year=2009|url=http://www.americanancestors.org/uploadedFiles/American_Ancestors/Content/Marketing/PDF_Archive/affleck_damon_connection.pdf|format=|accessdate=2010-02-02}}</ref>

Damon also attended [[Harvard University]] where he was a member of the class of 1992 but left before receiving his degree in order to take a lead role in the film ''School Ties''. While at [[Harvard University|Harvard]] he wrote an early treatment of the screenplay for ''[[Good Will Hunting]]'' as an exercise for an English class. Damon was a member of the [[Delphic Club]], one of the University's select [[Final Clubs]]. In 2013, he was awarded the [[Harvard Arts Medal]].<ref>[http://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2013/04/matt-damon-on-his-craft/  "Matt Damon on His Craft" Colleen Walsh, ''The Harvard Gazette'' April 25, 2013]</ref> Damon received an Academy Award for the screenplay of ''[[Good Will Hunting]]'' in 1998, which was handed to him by fellow [[Harvard University|Harvard]] alumnus [[Jack Lemmon]], who coincidentally had also been a member of Harvard's Delphic Club.<ref>[http://cache.boston.com/globe/living/packages/oscars98/titanicwins.html  "Oscar joins the family" Irene Sege, ''The Boston Globe'' March 25, 1998]</ref>

== Acting career ==

=== Early years: 1988–1996 ===
Damon attended [[Harvard University]] in 1988,<ref>"[http://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2013/08/oscar-winner-matt-damon-on-his-harvard-years/ Oscar winner Matt Damon on his Harvard years]", [[Harvard Gazette]], August 6, 2013</ref>{{#tag:ref|He lived in Matthews Hall and then [[Lowell House]],<ref name="Harvard1992">{{cite news|last=McGrath|first=Charles|publisher=''[[The New York Times]]''|title=6 Degrees of Harvard|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2006/10/01/weekinreview/01mcgrath.html?scp=1&sq=matt%20damon%20harvard%201992&st=cse|date=October 1, 2006|accessdate=April 5, 2009}}</ref>|group="nb"}}  where he appeared in student theater plays, such as ''Burn This in Winthrop House'' and ''A... My Name is Alice''.<ref name="Chainani">{{cite web|last=Chainani|first=Soman S.|publisher=''[[The Harvard Crimson]]''|title=Matt Damon On Life, Acting and Harvard|url=http://www.thecrimson.com/article/1997/12/12/matt-damon-on-life-acting-and/|accessdate=April 21, 2010}}</ref><ref name="HarvardCrimson">{{cite news|last=Siegler|first=Elijah|publisher=''[[The Harvard Crimson]]''|title=Ex Show Safe but Satisfying|url=http://www.thecrimson.com/article/1990/11/2/ex-show-safe-but-satisfying-piamy/|date=November 2, 1990|accessdate=October 11, 2013}}</ref> Later, he made his film debut at the age of 18, with a single line of dialogue in the [[romantic comedy]] ''[[Mystic Pizza]]''.<ref name="People - Bio">{{cite web|url=http://www.people.com/people/matt_damon/biography|title=Matt Damon Biography|accessdate=April 5, 2009|work=[[People (magazine)|People]]}}</ref> As a student at Harvard University, he acted in small roles such as in the [[Turner Network Television|TNT]] original film ''Rising Son'' and the ensemble prep-school drama ''[[School Ties]]''.<ref name="actors">{{citation | url=http://www.bravotv.com/inside-the-actors-studio/season-13/videos/matt-damon | title=Matt Damon - Season 13, Episode 3 | work=[[Inside the Actors Studio]] |publisher=Bravo TV |date = January 8, 2007}}</ref>  He left the university in 1992, a semester - 12 credits - shy of completion of his [[Bachelor of Arts]] in English to feature in ''[[Geronimo: An American Legend]]''<ref name="Chainani" /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://abcnews.go.com/Entertainment/Oscars/matt-damon-stars/story?id=9957289|title=Matt Damon: Before They Were Stars|publisher=ABC News|first=Jennifer|last=Joseph|date=1 March 2010}}</ref> in Los Angeles, mistakenly expecting the movie to become a big success.<ref name="Chainani" />{{#tag:ref|"By the time I figured out I had made the wrong decision, it was too late. I was living out here with a bunch of actors, and we were all scrambling to make ends meet," he has said.<ref>{{cite news|last=Koltnow|first=Barry |publisher='The Orange County Register''|title=Looking for Mr. 'Good' Guy|date=December 5, 1999}}</ref>|group="nb"}} Damon next appeared as an [[opiate]]-addicted soldier in 1996's ''[[Courage Under Fire]]'', for which he lost {{convert|40|lb|kg}} in 100&nbsp;days<ref name="People - Bio"/><ref name="EW40lbs">{{cite news|last=Greenblatt|first=Leah|publisher=''[[Entertainment Weekly]]''|title=Actorexia: A Brief History|url=http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,20057925,00.html|date=September 21, 2007|accessdate=April 5, 2009}}</ref> on a self-prescribed diet and fitness regimen. He took medication for a year and a half afterwards to correct the stress inflicted on his [[adrenal gland]] and was told he was lucky his heart did not shrink. ''Courage Under Fire'' gained him critical notice, when ''[[The Washington Post]]'' labeled his performance "impressive".<ref>{{cite news|last=Kempley|first=Rita|publisher=''[[The Washington Post]]''|title=Hand-to-Heart Combat |url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-srv/style/longterm/review96/courageunderfirekemp.htm|date=November 8, 1998|accessdate=April 21, 2010}}</ref>

=== Breakthrough roles: 1997–2000===
[[File:Rino Barillari and Matt Damon, 1999, Rome, Italy.jpg|thumb|Damon and [[paparazzi|paparazzo]] Rino Barillari at Rome in 1999]]
During the early 1990s, Damon and Affleck wrote ''[[Good Will Hunting]]'' (1997), a screenplay about a young math genius, an extension of a screenplay he wrote for an assignment at Harvard, having integrated advice from director [[Rob Reiner]], screenwriter [[William Goldman]], and writer/director [[Kevin Smith]].<ref>{{cite news | url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/08/15/ben-affleck-birthday_n_3750655.html | title=Ben Affleck's 41st Birthday Recalls Actor's Early Roles, Surprising Hobbies And Political Activism| work=Huffington Post | date=August 15, 2013 | author=Matthew Jacobs | accessdate=October 10, 2014|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/6TEhk8apD|archivedate=October 10, 2014}}</ref> It received nine [[Academy Awards]] nominations, earning Damon and Affleck [[Academy Award for Writing Original Screenplay|Oscars]] <ref name="Hartford9Nominations">{{cite news|authors=Amy Wallace, Robert W. Welkos and Susan King|title='Titanic' Ties Record for Oscar Nominations|publisher=''[[The Hartford Courant]]''|url=http://www.courant.com/topic/cl-env-1998oscarnoms,0,2173092.story?page=1|date=February 11, 1998|accessdate=April 5, 2009}}</ref><ref name="WashPostWin">{{cite news|last=Waxman|first=Sharon|author2=William Booth|title='Titanic's' 11 Oscars Ties Record; Night 'Good' for Nicholson, Hunt|publisher=''[[The Washington Post]]''|url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-srv/style/movies/oscars/oscars98.htm|date=March 23, 1998|accessdate=April 5, 2009}}</ref> and [[Golden Globe Awards|Golden Globes]] for Best Screenplay.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.eonline.com/news/584691/ben-affleck-s-most-awkward-smiles-see-that-one-in-gone-girl-and-9-from-real-life |title=Ben Affleck's Most Awkward Smiles | publisher=E! Entertainment | author=Corrine Heller | date=October 2, 2014 | accessdate=October 3, 2014 }}</ref> Damon was also nominated for an [[Academy Award for Best Actor]] for the same film, which also netted an [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor|Oscar for Best Supporting Actor]] for co-star [[Robin Williams]].<ref name="Hartford9Nominations"/> He and Affleck were each paid salaries of $600,000, while the film grossed over $225&nbsp;million at the worldwide box office.<ref name="bomojo"/><ref name="BioChannel$500K">{{cite web|publisher=[[The Biography Channel]]|title=Matt Damon|url=http://www.thebiographychannel.co.uk/biography_story/1685:2046/1/Matt_Damon.htm|accessdate=April 5, 2009}}{{Dead link|date=July 2013}}</ref> The two later parodied their roles from the film in Kevin Smith's 2001 movie ''[[Jay and Silent Bob Strike Back]]''.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.ew.com/ew/gallery/0,,20483133_20585795_21144739,00.html |title=Perfect Casting: Stars on Screen as Themselves | date=June 12, 2013 | author=EW Staff | publisher=Entertainment Weekly | accessdate=October 3, 2014 }}</ref>

Also in 1997, Damon was the lead in the critically acclaimed drama ''[[The Rainmaker (1997 film)|The Rainmaker]]'', where he was recognized by the ''[[Los Angeles Times]]'' as "a talented young actor on the brink of stardom."<ref>{{cite web|first=Jack|last=Matthews|url=http://www.calendarlive.com/movies/reviews/cl-movie980106-50,0,962161.story|title=John Grisham's The Rainmaker|accessdate=April 21, 2010|date=November 21, 1997|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/5qpFLz7Bh|archivedate=June 28, 2010}}</ref> After meeting Damon on the set of ''Good Will Hunting'', director [[Steven Spielberg]] cast Damon as the titular character in the 1998 [[World War II]] film ''[[Saving Private Ryan]]''.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.cbsnews.com/news/matt-damon-playing-private-ryan/ |title=Matt Damon: Playing Private Ryan | author=CBS News Staff | date=July 23, 1998 | publisher=CBS News | accessdate = October 3, 2014 }}</ref> He portrayed [[Patricia Highsmith]]'s anti-hero [[Tom Ripley]] in ''[[The Talented Mr. Ripley (film)|The Talented Mr. Ripley]]'' (1999),<ref name="People - Bio"/> in which "Damon outstandingly conveys his character's slide from innocent enthusiasm into cold calculation", according to ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'' magazine.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.variety.com/review/VE1117759854.html?categoryid=31&cs=1|title=The Talented Mr. Ripley – film review|accessdate=April 21, 2010|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] | first=Todd | last=McCarthy | date=December 13, 1999}}</ref> He played a [[fallen angel]] who discusses [[pop culture]] as intellectual subject matter with Affleck in ''[[Dogma (film)|Dogma]]'' (1999).<ref>{{cite news | url=http://www.nytimes.com/movie/review?res=9507E4D81E3EF937A35753C1A96F958260 |title=Dogma (1999) Movie Review | newspaper=The New York Times |date=October 4, 1999 | author=Janet Maslin | accessdate=October 4, 2014 }}</ref>

Damon's attempts at essaying leading characters in romantic dramas such as 2000's ''[[All the Pretty Horses (film)|All the Pretty Horses]]'' and ''[[The Legend of Bagger Vance]]'' were commercially and critically unsuccessful.<ref name="bomojo"/> ''Variety'' said of his work in ''All the Pretty Horses'': "[Damon] just doesn't quite seem like a young man who's spent his life amidst the dust and dung of a [[Texas]] cattle ranch. Nor does he strike any sparks with [[Penélope Cruz|[Penelope] Cruz]]."<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.variety.com/review/VE1117796963.html?categoryid=31&cs=1|title=All the Pretty Horses – film review|accessdate=April 21, 2010|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] | first=Todd | last=McCarthy | date=December 13, 2000}}</ref> He was similarly deemed "uncomfortable being the center" of [[Robert Redford]]'s ''The Legend of Bagger Vance''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://nymag.com/nymetro/movies/reviews/4029/|title=The Legend of Bagger Vance – film review|accessdate=April 21, 2010|work=[[New York (magazine)|New York]]}}</ref>

=== Hollywood star: 2001–2007 ===
[[File:Ocean's11Cast.jpg|alt=Six actors, all but one wearing a leather jacket, are photographed on a stage with a blue curtain as a backdrop.|thumb|right|[[Brad Pitt]], [[George Clooney]], Damon, [[Andy García]] and [[Julia Roberts]] (the cast of ''[[Ocean's Eleven]]''), with director [[Steven Soderbergh]] in December 2001]]

From 2001 to 2007, Damon gained wider international recognition as part of two major film franchises. He co-starred as thief Linus Caldwell in [[Steven Soderbergh]]'s 2001 film ''[[Ocean's Eleven]]'', a remake of the [[Rat Pack]]'s 1960 film ''[[Ocean's 11]]''; the successful crime [[dramedy]] spawned two sequels, ''[[Ocean's Twelve]]'' (2004) and ''[[Ocean's Thirteen]]'' (2007).<ref name="People - Bio"/> He played [[amnesia]]c assassin [[Jason Bourne (character)|Jason Bourne]] in the hit action thrillers, ''[[The Bourne Identity (2002 film)|The Bourne Identity]]'' (2002), ''[[The Bourne Supremacy (film)|The Bourne Supremacy]]'' (2004) and ''[[The Bourne Ultimatum (film)|The Bourne Ultimatum]]'' (2007),<ref name="People - Bio"/> for which ''[[Entertainment Weekly]]'' named Damon among "the decade's best mixer of brawn and brains."<ref>{{cite journal|authors=Thom, Geier, Jeff Jensen, Tina Jordan, et. al.|date=2009-12-11|title=The 100 Greatest Movies, TV Shows, Albums, Books, Characters, Scenes, Episodes, Songs, Dresses, Music Videos, And Trends That Entertained Us Over The Past 10 Years|work=[[Entertainment Weekly]]|page=(1079/1080):74–84}}</ref>

[[File:RobertDeNiroMattDamonBerlinFeb07.jpg|alt= Photo of Damon and De Niro, each wearing a tuxedo jacket and a dark blue shirt.|thumb|right|Damon and [[Robert De Niro]] at Berlin in February 2007 for the premiere of ''[[The Good Shepherd (film)|The Good Shepherd]]'']]
