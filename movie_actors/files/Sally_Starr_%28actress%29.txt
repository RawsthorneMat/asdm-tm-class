'''Sally Starr''' (January 23, 1909 â€“ 1996) was a movie actress from [[Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania]].

She was a [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] actress before making her debut in motion pictures. She performed on stage from the age of fourteen. Starr debuted in ''Frolics'' featuring [[Ted Lewis (musician)|Ted Lewis]] as a teenager. She later appeared in [[George White's Scandals]] of 1924. 

When Starr came to [[Hollywood]] she signed a contract with [[Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer|MGM]]. She played leading roles in ''So This Is College'' (1929), ''[[The Woman Racket]]'' (1930), ''[[Not So Dumb]]'' (1930), ''Personality'' (1930), ''[[Pardon My Gun (1930 film)|Pardon My Gun]]'' (1930) and ''For The Love o' Lil'' (1930). Starr was signed among the cast of ''Swing High'' (1930), a production of [[Pathe Pictures]]. 

Starr continued her theatrical performances after her motion picture career began. She played with [[Eleanor Powell]] and George Hassell in ''The Optimists'', staged at the Century Roof Theater in January 1928.  The same year she was cast with [[Elliott Nugent]], [[Robert Montgomery (actor)|Robert Montgomery]], and Phyllis Crane in ''College Life''. Her final films are ''Meet The Bride'' (1937), ''Getting An Eyeful'' (1938), ''Love and Onions'' (1938), and ''Money on Your Life'' (1938).
